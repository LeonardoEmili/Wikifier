        Blythe Katherine Danner is an American actress. She is the recipient of several accolades, including two  |Primetime_Emmy_Awards|Primetime_Emmy_Award|  for  |Best_Supporting_Actress_in_a_Drama_Series|Primetime_Emmy_Award_For_Outstanding_Supporting_Actress_In_A_Drama_Series|  for her role as Izzy Huffstodt on  Huff  , and a  |Tony_Award|Tony_Award|  for  |Best_Actress|Tony_Award_For_Best_Featured_Actress_In_A_Play|  for her performance in  Butterflies Are Free  on  |Broadway|Broadway_Theatre|  . Danner was twice nominated for the  |Primetime_Emmy_for_Outstanding_Guest_Actress_in_a_Comedy_Series|Primetime_Emmy_Award_For_Outstanding_Guest_Actress_In_A_Comedy_Series|  for portraying Marilyn Truman on  Will & Grace  , and the  |Primetime_Emmy_for_Outstanding_Lead_Actress_in_a_Miniseries_or_Movie|Primetime_Emmy_Award_For_Outstanding_Lead_Actress_In_A_Limited_Series_Or_Movie|  for her roles in  We Were the Mulvaneys  and  Back When We Were Grownups  . For the latter, she also received a  |Golden_Globe_Award|Golden_Globe_Award_For_Best_Actress_–_Miniseries_Or_Television_Film|  nomination.     Danner played Dina Byrnes in  Meet the Parents  and its sequels  Meet the Fockers  and  Little Fockers  . She has collaborated on several occasions with  |Woody_Allen|Woody_Allen| , appearing in three of his films:  Another Woman  ,  Alice  , and  Husbands and Wives  . Her other notable film credits include  1776  ,  Hearts of the West  ,  The Great Santini  ,  Mr. and Mrs. Bridge  ,  The Prince of Tides  ,  To Wong Foo, Thanks for Everything! Julie Newmar  ,  The Myth of Fingerprints  ,  The X-Files  ,  Forces of Nature  ,  The Last Kiss  ,  Paul  ,  Hello I Must Be Going  ,  Ill See You in My Dreams  , and  What They Had  .     Danner is the sister of  |Harry_Danner|Harry_Danner|  and the widow of  |Bruce_Paltrow|Bruce_Paltrow| . She is the mother of actress  |Gwyneth_Paltrow|Gwyneth_Paltrow|  and director  |Jake_Paltrow|Jake_Paltrow| .       Danner was born in  |Philadelphia|Philadelphia| ,  |Pennsylvania|Pennsylvania| , the daughter of Katharine   and Harry Earl Danner, a bank executive.   She has a brother, opera singer and actor  |Harry_Danner|Harry_Danner| ; a sister, performer-turned-director Dorothy Dottie Danner; and a maternal half-brother, violin maker William Moennig. Danner has  |Pennsylvania_Dutch|Pennsylvania_Dutch|  , and some English and Irish, ancestry; her maternal grandmother was a German immigrant, and one of her paternal great-grandmothers was born in  |Barbados|Barbados|  .       Danner graduated from  |George_School|George_School| , a Quaker high school located near  |Newtown_Bucks_County_Pennsylvania|Newtown_Bucks_County_Pennsylvania|  in 1960.        A graduate of  |Bard_College|Bard_College| , Danners first roles included the 1967 musical  Mata Hari  , and the 1968 Off-Broadway production of Summertree. Her early Broadway appearances included  Cyrano de Bergerac  and her  |Theatre_World_Award|Theatre_World_Award| -winning performance in  The Miser  . She won the  |Tony_Award_for_Best_Featured_Actress_in_a_Play|Tony_Award_For_Best_Featured_Actress_In_A_Play|  for portraying a free-spirited divorcée in  Butterflies Are Free  .     In 1972, Danner portrayed  |Martha_Jefferson|Martha_Jefferson|  in the film version of  1776 . That same year, she played a wife whose husband has been unfaithful, opposite  |Peter_Falk|Peter_Falk|  and  |John_Cassavetes|John_Cassavetes| , in the  Columbo  episode Etude in Black .     Her earliest starring film role was opposite  |Alan_Alda|Alan_Alda|  in To Kill a Clown . Scott Fitzgerald and The Last of the Belles  . She was the eponymous heroine in the film  Lovin Molly  . She appeared in  Futureworld , playing Tracy Ballard with co-star  |Peter_Fonda|Peter_Fonda|  . In the 1982 TV movie  Inside the Third Reich , she played the wife of  |Albert_Speer|Albert_Speer| . She has appeared in two films based on the novels of  |Pat_Conroy|Pat_Conroy| ,  The Great Santini  and  The Prince of Tides  , as well as two television movies adapted from books by  |Anne_Tyler|Anne_Tyler| ,  Saint Maybe  and  Back When We Were Grownups , both for the  |Hallmark_Hall_of_Fame|Hallmark_Hall_Of_Fame| .        Danner appeared opposite  |Robert_De_Niro|Robert_De_Niro|  in the 2000 comedy hit  Meet the Parents , and its sequels,  Meet the Fockers  and  Little Fockers  .     From 2001 to 2006, she regularly appeared on NBCs sitcom  Will & Grace  as  |Will_Trumans|Will_Truman|  mother  |Marilyn|Supporting_Characters_On_Will_Gracewills_Family| . From 2004 to 2006, she starred in the main cast of the comedy-drama series  Huff . In 2005, she was nominated for three  |Primetime_Emmy_Awards|Primetime_Emmy_Award|  for her work on Will & Grace, Huff, and the television film  Back When We Were Grownups , winning for her role in Huff. The following year, she won a second consecutive Emmy Award for Huff. For 25 years, she has been a regular performer at the Williamstown Summer Theater Festival, where she also serves on the Board of Directors.      In 2006, Danner was awarded an inaugural  |Katharine_Hepburn|Katharine_Hepburn|  Medal by  |Bryn_Mawr_Colleges|Bryn_Mawr_College|  Katharine Houghton Hepburn Center. Danner has been involved in environmental issues such as  |recycling|Recycling|  and  |conservation|Conservation_Ethic|  for over 30 years.  She has been active with  |INFORM_Inc_|Inform_Inc_| , is on the Board of Environmental Advocates of New York and the Board of Directors of the  |Environmental_Media_Association|Environmental_Media_Association| ,  and won the 2002  |EMA_Board_of_Directors_Ongoing_Commitment_Award|Environmental_Media_Awards| .  In 2011, Danner joined Moms Clean Air Force,  to help call on parents to join in the fight against toxic air pollution.       After the death of her husband  |Bruce_Paltrow|Bruce_Paltrow|  from  |oral_cancer|Oral_Cancer| , she became involved with the  |nonprofit|5013| |_Oral_Cancer_Foundation|Oral_Cancer_Foundation| . She has since appeared on  |morning_talk_shows|Breakfast_Television|  and given interviews in such magazines as  People . The Bruce Paltrow Oral Cancer Fund, administered by the Oral Cancer Foundation, raises funding for oral cancer research and treatment, with a particular focus on those communities in which healthcare disparities exist.      She has also appeared in commercials for Prolia, a brand of  |denosumab|Denosumab|  used in the treatment of  |osteoporosis|Osteoporosis| .
