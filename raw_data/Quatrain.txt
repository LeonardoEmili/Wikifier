A quatrain is a type of  |stanza|Stanza| , or a complete  |poem|Poem| , consisting of four lines.     Existing in a variety of forms, the quatrain appears in  |poems|Poem|  from the poetic traditions of various ancient civilizations including  |Ancient_India|Ancient_India| ,  |Ancient_Greece|Ancient_Greece| ,  |Ancient_Rome|Ancient_Rome| , and  |China|Classical_Chinese_Poetry_Forms| , and continues into the 21st century, where it is seen in works published in many languages. During Europes  |Dark_Ages|Dark_Ages| , in the Middle East and especially Iran,  |polymath|Polymath|  poets such as  |Omar_Khayyam|Omar_Khayyam|  continued to popularize this form of poetry, also known as  |Rubai|Rubai| , well beyond their borders and time. Michel de Nostredame  used the quatrain form to deliver his famous  |prophecies|Les_Propheties|  in the 16th century. The  |Rubai|Rubai|  form of rhymed quatrain was favored by  |Omar_Khayyám|Omar_Khayyám| , among others. This work was a major inspiration for  |Edward_FitzGeralds|Edward_Fitzgerald|   Rubaiyat of Omar Khayyam , written in Persian. The rubai was a particularly widespread verse form: the form rubaiyat reflects the plural. One of FitzGeralds verses Verse VII, see  |Rubaiyat_version_at_Wikisource|S_The_Rubaiyat_Of_Omar_Khayyam|  may serve to illustrate:     Come, fill the Cup, and in the fire of Spring   Your Winter garment of Repentance fling:   The Bird of Time has but a little way   To flutter—and the Bird is on the Wing.     The  |Midnight_Songs_poetry|Midnight_Songs_Poetry|  form is from Fourth Century China, consisting of regular five-character lines, with each quatrain formed from a pair of rhymed couplets. The person matter involves the personal thoughts and feelings of a courtesan during the four seasons, into which the quatrains are individually assigned.    |Shairi|Shairi|  is an AAAA rhyming form used mainly in  |The_Knight_in_the_Panthers_Skin|The_Knight_In_The_Panthers_Skin| .   The  |Shichigon_zekku|Shichigon_Zekku|  form used on  |Classical_Chinese_poetry|Classical_Chinese_Poetry|  and  |Japanese|Japanese_Language|  poetry. This type of quatrain uses a seven characters length of line.
