           Hyaena is a  |genus|Genus|  comprising two of the living  |species|Species|  of  |hyenas|Hyena| : the  |striped_hyena|Striped_Hyena|   from  |western_Asia|Western_Asia|  and  |northern_Africa|North_Africa|  and the  |brown_hyena|Brown_Hyena|   from  |southern_Africa|Southern_Africa| . The brown hyena has sometimes been placed in a separate genus Parahyaena, or even included in the otherwise  |fossil|Fossil|  genus  Pachycrocuta , but recent sources have tended to place it in Hyaena.     The brown hyenas skull is larger than that of the striped hyena. The male brown hyena is slightly larger than the female, while the sexes of the striped hyena are equally sized . Both species are smaller than the  |spotted_hyena|Spotted_Hyena|  , but larger than the  |aardwolf|Aardwolf|  . They are predominantly  |scavengers|Scavenger| .
