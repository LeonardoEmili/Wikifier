   The politics of Gibraltar takes place within a framework of a  |parliamentary|Parliamentary_System| |_representative_democratic|Representative_Democracy| |_British_Overseas_Territory|British_Overseas_Territories| , whereby the  |Monarch_of_the_United_Kingdom|Monarchy_Of_The_United_Kingdom|  is the constitutional head of state represented by the  |Governor_of_Gibraltar|Governor_Of_Gibraltar| . The  |Chief_Minister_of_Gibraltar|Chief_Minister_Of_Gibraltar|  is the head of Government. As a British Overseas Territory, the  |Government_of_Gibraltar|Government_Of_Gibraltar|  is not subordinate to the  |Government_of_the_United_Kingdom|Government_Of_The_United_Kingdom| . The British Government, however, is responsible for defence and external affairs but Gibraltar has full internal  |self_government|Self_Governance|  under its  |2006_Constitution|Gibraltar_Constitution_Order_2006| . Gibraltar is represented in the  |European_Union|European_Union| , having been the only British Overseas Territory to have joined the  |European_Economic_Community|European_Economic_Community|  under the British Treaty of Accession .     The  |government_of_Spain|Government_Of_Spain|  continues with an  |irredentist|Irredentist| |_territorial_claim_to_Gibraltar|Disputed_Status_Of_Gibraltar| , which was ceded in perpetuity to the  |British_Crown|British_Crown|  in 1713 by  |Article_X|_S_Peace_And_Friendship_Treaty_Of_Utrecht_Between_Spain_And_Great_Britainarticle_X|  of the  |Treaty_of_Utrecht|Treaty_Of_Utrecht| . In a  |referendum_held_in_2002|2002_Gibraltar_Sovereignty_Referendum| , a proposal for shared  |sovereignty|Sovereignty|  was overwhelmingly rejected by the Gibraltar electorate with 98.97% voting against.  The sovereignty issue remains an important factor in local politics.     Gibraltar has a number of  |political_parties|List_Of_Political_Parties_In_Gibraltar|  which have developed to address local issues. The preamble to the 2006  |Constitution|Constitution_Of_Gibraltar|  repeated from the  |1969_Constitution|Gibraltar_Constitution_Order_1969|  states that  Her Majestys Government will never enter into arrangements under which the people of Gibraltar would pass under the sovereignty of another state against their freely and democratically expressed wishes.
