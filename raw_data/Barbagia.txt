        Barbagia  is a mountain area of inner  |Sardinia|Sardinia| . It is a  |natural_region|Natural_Region|  mostly contained in the  |province_of_Nuoro|Province_Of_Nuoro|  and located alongside the  |Gennargentu|Gennargentu|  massif.     The name comes from  |Cicero|Cicero| , who described it as a land of  |barbarians|Barbarians|  . This word derives from the Greek Βάρβαρος, which means stuttering . The  |Sardinians|Sardinians|  from this area were also known, by the  |ancient_Romans|Ancient_Rome| , with the pejorative term latrones mastrucati, which means thieves with a rough garment in wool ; Roman domination in this part of Sardinia was never more than nominal.     In 594, Pope  |Gregory_the_Great|Gregory_The_Great|  wrote a letter to  |Hospito|Hospito| , a Christian whom he calls the leader of the Barbaricini . Hospito apparently permitted the evangelisation of pagan Barbagia by Christian missionaries.     The area is usually divided into five Barbagias: the Barbagia of  |Ollolai|Ollolai| , the  |Barbagia_of_Seulo|Barbagia_Di_Seulo| , the  |Barbagia_of_Belvì|Barbagia_Of_Belvì| , the  |Mandrolisai|Mandrolisai| , and finally the Barbagia Trigònia, the historical name by which the area of  |Ogliastra|Ogliastra|  was once referred to. The latter two are named after a sub-region, and the others after their main villages.     The area is full of hard hills and mountains, and there is little human presence. Barbagia is one of the least populated areas in Europe, which has allowed Barbagia to preserve its cultural and natural treasures. Barbagia is one of the few Sardinian regions where the  |Sardinian_language|Sardinian_Language|  in its own varieties, both  |Nuorese|Logudorese_Dialect|  and  |Campidanese|Campidanese_Dialect| , is still spoken on an everyday basis, while the rest of the island has already undergone  |shift|Language_Shift|  to Italian.     One of the most important villages is  |Gavoi|Gavoi| .  |Orgosolo|Orgosolo|  was famous for its bandits and kidnappers and typical  |murals|Murals| .  |Oliena|Oliena|  is well known for its  |wines|Wine|  . Another well known town is  |Fonni|Fonni|.
