        Lieutenant General Shaul Mofaz  is an Israeli former soldier and politician. He joined the  |Israel_Defense_Forces|Israel_Defense_Forces|  in 1966 and served in the  |Paratroopers_Brigade|Paratroopers_Brigade| . He fought in the  |Six-Day_War|Six-Day_War| ,  |Yom_Kippur_War|Yom_Kippur_War| ,  |1982_Lebanon_War|1982_Lebanon_War| , and  |Operation_Entebbe|Operation_Entebbe|  with the paratroopers and  |Sayeret_Matkal|Sayeret_Matkal| , an elite  |special_forces|Special_Forces|  unit. In 1998 he became the sixteenth IDFs Chief of the General Staff, serving until 2002. He is of  |Iranian_Jewish|Persian_Jews|  ancestry.     After leaving the army, he entered politics. He was appointed  |Minister_of_Defense|Ministry_Of_Defense|  in 2002, holding the position until 2006 when he was elected to the Knesset on the  |Kadima|Kadima|  list. He then served as Deputy Prime Minister and  |Minister_of_Transportation_and_Road_Safety|Ministry_Of_Transport_And_Road_Safety|  until 2009. After becoming Kadima leader in March 2012 he became  |Leader_of_the_Opposition|Leader_Of_The_Opposition| , before returning to the cabinet during a 70-day spell in which he served as Acting Prime Minister, Vice Prime Minister and  |Minister_without_Portfolio|Minister_Without_Portfolio| . Kadima was reduced to just two seats in the  |2013_elections|2013_Israeli_Legislative_Election| , and Mofaz retired from politics shortly before the  |2015_elections|2015_Israeli_Legislative_Election| .              Shaul Mofaz was born Shahrām Mofazzazkār  on 4 November 1948 in  |Tehran|Tehran| , to  |Persian_Jewish|Persian_Jewish|  parents from  |Isfahan|Isfahan| . Mofaz  |immigrated_to_Israel|Aliyah|  with his parents in 1957. Upon graduating from high school in 1966, he joined the  |Israel_Defense_Forces|Israel_Defense_Forces|  and served in the  |Paratroopers_Brigade|Paratroopers_Brigade| . He served in the  |Six-Day_War|Six-Day_War| ,  |Yom_Kippur_War|Yom_Kippur_War| ,  |1982_Lebanon_War|1982_Lebanon_War| , and  |Operation_Entebbe|Operation_Entebbe|  with the paratroopers and  |Sayeret_Matkal|Sayeret_Matkal| , an elite  |special_forces|Special_Forces|  unit.        Mofaz was then appointed an infantry brigade commander for the  |1982_Lebanon_War|1982_Lebanon_War| . Afterwards he attended the US Marine Corps Command and Staff College in  |Quantico|Mcb_Quantico| ,  |Virginia|Virginia| ,  |United_States|United_States| . On his return he was briefly appointed commander of the Officers School, before returning to active service as commander of the 35th Paratroopers Brigade in 1986, and led its forces during  |Operation_Law_and_Order|Operation_Law_And_Order| . Gal Perl Finkel, ,  |The_Jerusalem_Post|The_Jerusalem_Post| , February 21, 2019.     Mofaz served in a series of senior military posts, having been promoted to the rank of Brigadier General . In 1993 he was made commander of the IDF forces in the  |West_Bank|West_Bank| . In 1994, he was promoted to Major General, commanding the Southern Corps. His rapid rise continued; in 1997 Mofaz was appointed Deputy Chief of the General Staff and in 1998 he was appointed Chief of the General Staff.     His term of Chief of Staff was noted for financial and structural reforms of the Israeli Army. But the most significant event in his tenure was the eruption of the  |Second_Intifada|Second_Intifada|  in September, 2000. The tough tactics undertaken by Mofaz drew widespread concern from the international community but were broadly supported by the Israeli public. Controversy erupted over the offensive in  |Jenin|Jenin| , intermittent raids in the  |Gaza_Strip|Gaza_Strip| , and the continued isolation of  |Yasser_Arafat|Yasser_Arafat| .     Mofaz foresaw the wave of violence coming early as 1999 and prepared the IDF for intense  |guerrilla_warfare|Guerrilla_Warfare|  in the territories. He fortified posts at the  |Gaza_Strip|Gaza_Strip|  and kept  |Israel_Defense_Forces|Israel_Defense_Forces|  casualties low. While he was known for claiming, Israel has the most moral army in the world,  Haaretz, 8 Jan 2015, he drew criticism from both Israeli and international human rights monitoring groups because of the methods he had undertaken, including using  |armored_bulldozers|Armored_Bulldozer|  to demolish 2,500 Palestinian civilian homes, displacing thousands, in order to create a security buffer zone along the  |Rafah|Rafah,_Egypt|  border.         Following a government crisis in 2002, Shaul Mofaz was appointed  |Defense_Minister|Defense_Minister_Of_Israel|  by Ariel Sharon.  Biography at Jewish Virtual Library Although he supported an agreement with the Palestinians, he was willing to make no compromise in the war against militant groups such as  |Hamas|Hamas| ,  |Islamic_Jihad|Islamic_Jihad_Movement_In_Palestine| ,  |Tanzim|Tanzim| , and  |Al-Aqsa_Martyrs_Brigades|Al-Aqsa_Martyrs_Brigades| .     The fact that he had only recently left his position as IDF Chief of Staff prevented him from participating in the  |2003_election|2003_Israeli_Legislative_Election|  . Nevertheless, Sharon reappointed him as Defense Minister in the new government.     On 21 November 2005, Mofaz rejected Sharons invitation to join his new party,  |Kadima|Kadima| , and instead announced his candidacy for the leadership of Likud. But, on 11 December 2005, one day after he promised he would never leave the Likud,  he withdrew from both the leadership race and the Likud to join Kadima.     Following the elections in late March 2006, Mofaz was moved from the position of Defense Minister and received the  |Transport_ministry|Transportation_Minister_Of_Israel|  in the new Cabinet installed on 4 May 2006.      In 2008, with Israels then prime minister,  |Ehud_Olmert|Ehud_Olmert| , being pressured to resign due to corruption charges, Mofaz announced that he would run for the leadership of the Kadima party.     On 5 August 2008, Mofaz officially entered the race to be leader of Kadima. That same day he received a blessing by  |Shas|Shas|  spiritual leader  |Rabbi|Rabbi| |Ovadia_Yosef|Ovadia_Yosef| . On 17 September 2008, he lost the Kadima party election, losing to  |Tzipi_Livni|Tzipi_Livni|  for the spot of the Prime Minister and leader of Kadima. Livnis narrow margin of 431 votes was 43.1% to Shaul Mofazs 42.0%, a huge difference from the 10 to 12-point  |exit_polls|Exit_Polls|  margins. She said the national responsibility by the public brings me to approach this job with great reverence .   Mofaz accepted the  |Kadima|Kadima|  primarys result, despite his lawyer, Yehuda Weinsteins appeal advice, and telephoned Livni congratulating her. Livni got 16,936 votes, with 16,505 votes, for Mofaz. Public Security Minister  |Avi_Dichter|Avi_Dichter|  and Interior Minister  |Meir_Sheetrit|Meir_Sheetrit|  had 6.5% and 8.5% respectively.     Placed second on the Kadima list, Mofaz retained his seat in the  |2009_elections|2009_Israeli_Legislative_Election| , but lost his cabinet position after Likud formed the government.     On 27 March 2012, Shaul Mofaz won the Kadima party leadership primaries by a landslide, defeating party chairwoman  |Tzipi_Livni|Tzipi_Livni| .  Mofaz became Vice Prime Minister as part of a deal reached for a government of national unity with  |Binyamin_Netanyahu|Binyamin_Netanyahu| .  Mofaz said during the Kadima primaries that he would not join a government led by Netanyahu.      Mofaz left over Netanyahus indecision over a draft reform law and warned that the prime minister was trying to patch together a majority for a vote to plunge the region into war. In the buildup to the  |2015_elections|2015_Israeli_Legislative_Election|  Kadima was not expect to pass the threshold, as it was raised to 3.25%. Mofaz negotiated with the  |Zionist_Union|Zionist_Union|  alliance to bring  |Kadima|Kadima|  onto their slate, but ended negotiations when it became clear he would not be their candidate for Defense Minister. Immediately after Mofaz announced he was not joining the  |Zionist_Union|Zionist_Union|  slate, it was announced the former  |Military_Intelligence_Directorate|Military_Intelligence_Directorate|  head  |Amos_Yadlin|Amos_Yadlin|  was appointed to the  |Zionist_Union|Zionist_Union|  slate and would be their candidate for Defense Minister. Within a week of his announcement that he was not running with the  |Zionist_Union|Zionist_Union| , Mofaz announced his retirement from politics.
