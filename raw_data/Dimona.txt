The citys name is derived from a biblical town, mentioned in  |Joshua|Joshua|  15:21-22.            Dimona was one of the  |development_towns|Development_Town|  created in the 1950s under the leadership of Israels first Prime Minister,  |David_Ben_Gurion|David_Ben_Gurion| . Dimona itself was conceived in 1953. The location chosen was close to the  |Dead_Sea_Works|Dead_Sea_Works| . It was established in 1955. The first residents were Jewish immigrants from  |North_Africa|North_Africa| , with an initial 36 families being the first to settle there. Its population in 1955 was about 300. The North African immigrants also constructed the citys houses.     When the  |Israeli_nuclear_program|Nuclear_Weapons_And_Israel|  began in 1958, a location not far from the city was chosen for the  |Negev_Nuclear_Research_Center|Negev_Nuclear_Research_Center|  due to its relative isolation in the desert and availability of housing. In the late 1950s and early 1960s, immigrants from Eastern Europe arrived. A textile factory was opened in 1958. That same year, Dimona became a  |local_council|Local_Council| . In 1961, it had a population of 5,000. The emblem of Dimona , adopted 2 March 1961, appeared on a stamp issued on 24 March 1965. Dimona was declared a city in 1969. In 1971, it had a population of 23,700.     In spite of a gradual decrease during the 1980s, the citys population began to grow once again with the beginning of the  |Russian_immigration_in_the_1990s|Aliyah_From_The_Commonwealth_Of_Independent_States_In_The_1990S| . Currently, Dimona is the third largest city in the Negev, with the population of almost 34,000. Due to projected rapid population growth in the Negev, the city is expected to triple in size by 2025.        Dimona is described as mini-India by many for its 7,500-strong Indian Jewish community. In the early 1980s, textile plants, such as Dimona Textiles Ltd., dominated the industrial landscape. Many plants have since closed. Dimona Silica Industries Ltd. manufactures precipitated silica and calcium carbonate fillers. About a third of the citys population works in industrial workplaces , and another third in the area of services. Due to the introduction of new technologies, many workers have been made redundant in the recent years, creating a total unemployment rate of about 10%.
