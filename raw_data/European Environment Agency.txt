The European Environment Agency is the  |agency_of_the_European_Union|Agency_Of_The_European_Union|  which provides independent information on the environment.   Its goal is to help those involved in developing, implementing and evaluating  |environmental_policy|Environmental_Policy| , and to inform the general public.       The EEA was established by the  |European_Economic_Community|European_Economic_Community|  Regulation 1210/1990 and became operational in 1994, headquartered in  |Copenhagen|Copenhagen| ,  |Denmark|Denmark| .     The agency is governed by a management board composed of representatives of the governments of its 33 member states, a  |European_Commission|European_Commission|  representative and two scientists appointed by the  |European_Parliament|European_Parliament| , assisted by a committee of scientists.   The current Executive Director of the agency is Professor  |Hans_Bruyninckx|Hans_Bruyninckx| , who has been appointed for a five-year term. He is the successor of Professor  |Jacqueline_McGlade|Jacqueline_Mcglade| .       The  |member_states|Member_States_Of_The_European_Union|  of the union are members; however other states may become members of it by means of agreements concluded between them and the EU.     It was the first EU body to open its membership to the 13 candidate countries .     The EEA has 33 member countries and six cooperating countries. The 33 member countries include the 28  |European_Union_member_states|European_Union_Member_State|  together with  |Iceland|Iceland| ,  |Liechtenstein|Liechtenstein| ,  |Norway|Norway| ,  |Switzerland|Switzerland|  and  |Turkey|Turkey| .     The six  |Western_Balkan|Western_Balkans|  countries are cooperating countries:  |Albania|Albania| ,  |Bosnia_and_Herzegovina|Bosnia_And_Herzegovina| ,  |Montenegro|Montenegro| ,  |North_Macedonia|North_Macedonia| ,  |Serbia|Serbia|  as well as  |Kosovo|Kosovo|  under the  |UN_Security_Council|Un_Security_Council|  Resolution 1244/99. , European Environment Agency, Retrieved on 24 July 2013. These cooperation activities are integrated into Eionet and are supported by the EU under the Instrument for Pre-Accession Assistance .     The EEA is an active member of the  |EPA_Network|Epa_Network| .             The European Environment Agency reported in 2017 that climate-related extreme events accounted ca €400 billion of economic losses in EEA area from 1980 to 2013, and were responsible for 85,000 deaths during 1980-2013. Nina Chestney  Reuters, January 25, 2017             The European environment information and observation network is a partnership network of the EEA and the countries. The EEA is responsible for developing the network and coordinating its activities. To do so, the EEA works closely together with national focal points , typically national environment agencies or environment ministries which are responsible for coordinating national networksof the National Reference Centres involving many institutions .     Apart from the NFPs and NRCs, Eionet covers six European Topic Centres in the areas of air and climate change, biological diversity, climate change impacts, vulnerability and adaptation, water, land use and spatial information and analysis and  |sustainable_consumption|Sustainable_Consumption|  and production.
