             The Hebrides  comprise a widespread and diverse  |archipelago|Archipelago|  off the west coast of mainland  |Scotland|Scotland| . There are two main groups: the  |Inner|Inner_Hebrides|  and  |Outer_Hebrides|Outer_Hebrides| .     These islands have a long history of occupation dating back to the  |Mesolithic|Mesolithic| , and the culture of the residents has been affected by the successive influences of  |Celtic-|Celtic_Language| ,  |Norse-|Old_Norse_Language| , and  |English-|English_Language| speaking peoples. This diversity is reflected in the names given to the islands, which are derived from the languages that have been spoken there in historic and perhaps  |prehistoric|Prehistoric|  times.     The Hebrides are the source of much of  |Scottish_Gaelic_literature|Scottish_Gaelic_Literature|  and  |Gaelic_music|Gaelic_Music| . Today the economy of the islands is dependent on  |crofting|Crofting| , fishing,  |tourism|Tourism| , the oil industry, and  |renewable_energy|Renewable_Energy| . The Hebrides have lower biodiversity than mainland Scotland, but there is a significant presence of seals and seabirds.
