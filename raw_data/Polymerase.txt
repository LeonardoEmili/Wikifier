   A polymerase is an  |enzyme|Enzyme|  that synthesizes long chains of  |polymers|Polymer|  or  |nucleic_acids|Nucleic_Acid| .  |DNA_polymerase|Dna_Polymerase|  and  |RNA_polymerase|Rna_Polymerase|  are used to assemble  |DNA|Dna|  and  |RNA|Rna|  molecules, respectively, by copying a DNA template strand using  |base-pairing|Base_Pair|  interactions or RNA by half ladder replication.     A DNA polymerase from the  |thermophilic|Thermophile| |bacterium|Bacteria| ,  Thermus aquaticus    is used in the  |polymerase_chain_reaction|Polymerase_Chain_Reaction| , an important technique of  |molecular_biology|Molecular_Biology| .        |DNA_polymerase|Dna_Polymerase| |DNA_polymerase_I|Dna_Polymerase_I| ; Pol  |γ|Polg| ,  |θ|Polq| ,  |ν|Dna_Polymerase_Nu| |DNA_polymerase_II|Dna_Polymerase_Ii| ; Pol  |α|Dna_Polymerase_Alpha| ,  |δ|Dna_Polymerase_Delta| ,  |ε|Dna_Polymerase_Epsilon| ,  |ζ|Rev3L| |DNA_polymerase_III_holoenzyme|Dna_Polymerase_Iii_Holoenzyme| |DNA_polymerase_IV|Dna_Polymerase_Iv|  –  |SOS|Sos_Response|  repair polymerase; Pol  |β|Dna_Polymerase_Beta| ,  |λ|Dna_Polymerase_Lambda| ,  |μ|Dna_Polymerase_Mu| |Terminal_deoxynucleotidyl_transferase|Terminal_Deoxynucleotidyl_Transferase|  , which lends diversity to antibody heavy chains.    Family Y:  |DNA_polymerase_V|Dna_Polymerase_V|  - SOS repair polymerase; Pol  |η|Dna_Polymerase_Eta| ,  |ι|Poli| ,  |κ|Polk| |Reverse_transcriptase|Reverse_Transcriptase| , an enzyme used by  |RNA|Rna| |retrovirus|Retrovirus| es like  |HIV|Hiv| , which is used to create a complementary strand to the preexisting strand of viral  |RNA|Rna|  before it can be integrated into the  |DNA|Dna|  of the  |host|Host| |cell|Cell| . It is also a major target for antiviral drugs.    |RNA_polymerase|Rna_Polymerase| |RNA_polymerase_I|Rna_Polymerase_I| ,  |RNA_polymerase_II|Rna_Polymerase_Ii| ,  |RNA_polymerase_III|Rna_Polymerase_Iii| |T7_RNA_polymerase|T7_Rna_Polymerase| ,  |POLRMT|Polrmt| |RNA_replicase|Rna_Replicase| |Primase|Primase| ,  |PrimPol|Primpol|      In general, viral single-subunit RNA polymerases/replicases/reverse transcriptase shares a common origin with DNA polymerase. They have a conserved palm domain.  Multi-subunit RNA polymerase forms an unrelated group.  Primases have a more complex story: bacterial primases with the Toprim domain are related to  |topoisomerase|Topoisomerase|  and mitochrondrial helicase,  while archaea and eukaryotic primases form an unrelated family, possibly related to the polymerase palm. Both families nevertheless associate to the same bunch of helicases.
