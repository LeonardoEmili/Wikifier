           Charles Robert Malden , was a nineteenth-century  |British|United_Kingdom| |naval|Royal_Navy|  officer, surveyor and educator. He is the discoverer of  |Malden_Island|Malden_Island|  in the central  |Pacific|Pacific| , which is named in his honour. He also founded  |Windlesham_House_School|Windlesham_House_School|  at  |Brighton|Brighton| ,  |England|England| .       Malden was born in  |Putney|Putney| ,  |Surrey|Surrey| , son of Jonas Malden, a surgeon. He entered British naval service at the age of 11 on 22 June 1809. He served nine years as a volunteer 1st class,  |midshipman|Midshipman| , and  |shipmate|Shipmate| , including one year in the  |English_Channel|English_Channel|  and  |Bay_of_Biscay|Bay_Of_Biscay|  , four years at the  |Cape_of_Good_Hope|Cape_Of_Good_Hope|  and in the  |East_Indies|East_Indies|  , two and a half years on the North American and West Indian stations , and a year and a half in the  |Mediterranean|Mediterranean|  . He was present at the capture of  |Mauritius|Mauritius|  and  |Java|Java| , and at the battles of  |Baltimore|Battle_Of_Baltimore|  and  |New_Orleans|Battle_Of_New_Orleans| .     He passed the examination in the elements of  |mathematics|Mathematics|  and the theory of  |navigation|Navigation|  at the  |Royal_Naval_Academy|Royal_Naval_Academy|  on 2â€“4 September 1816, and became a 1st Lieutenant on 1 September 1818. In eight years of active service as an officer, he served two and a half years in a surveying ship in the Mediterranean , one and a half years in a surveying sloop in the English Channel and off the coast of  |Ireland|Ireland|  , and one and a half years as Surveyor of the frigate  during a voyage to and from the  |Hawaiian_Islands|Hawaiian_Islands|  .    In Hawaii he surveyed harbours which, he noted, were said not to exist by Captains Cook and Vancouver. On the return voyage he discovered and explored uninhabited Malden Island in the central Pacific on 30 July 1825. After his return he left active service but remained at half pay. He served for several years as  |hydrographer|Hydrographer|  to  |King_William_IV|William_Iv_Of_The_United_Kingdom| .     He married Frances Cole, daughter of Rev. William Hodgson Cole, rector of  |West_Clandon|West_Clandon|  and Vicar of  |Wonersh|Wonersh| , near  |Guildford|Guildford| , Surrey, on 8 April 1828. Malden became the father of seven sons and a daughter.     From 1830-36 he took pupils for the Royal Navy at  |Ryde|Ryde| ,  |Isle_of_Wight|Isle_Of_Wight| . He purchased the school of  |Henry_Worsley|Sir_Henry_Worsley,_2Nd_Baronet|  at  |Newport|Newport,_Isle_Of_Wight| , Isle of Wight, in December 1836, reopened it as a  |preparatory_school|Preparatory_School|  on 20 February 1837, and moved it to Montpelier Road in Brighton in December 1837. He built the  |Windlesham_House_School|Windlesham_House_School| .
