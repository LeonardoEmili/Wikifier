   Johannes Rau  was a  |German|Germany| |_politician|Politician|  . He was  |President_of_Germany|President_Of_Germany|  from 1 July 1999 until 30 July 2004 and  |Minister_President|List_Of_Ministers_President_Of_North_Rhine_Westphalia|  of  |North_Rhine_Westphalia|North_Rhine_Westphalia|  from 20 September 1978 to 9 June 1998. In the latter role, he also served as  |President_of_the_Bundesrat|President_Of_The_Bundesrat|  in 1982/83 and in 1994/1995.       Rau was born in the  |Barmen|Barmen|  part of  |Wuppertal|Wuppertal| ,  |Rhine_Province|Rhine_Province| , as the third of five children. His family was strongly  |Protestant|Protestantism| . As a schoolboy, Rau was active in the  |Confessing_Church|Confessing_Church| , a circle of the German Protestant Church which resisted  |Nazism|Nazism| .     Rau left school in 1949 and worked as a publisher, especially with the Protestant Youth Publishing House.       Rau was a member of the  |All_German_People’s_Party|All_German_Peoples_Party|  , which was founded by  |Gustav_Heinemann|Gustav_Heinemann| . The party was known for proposing German reunification from 1952 until it was disbanded in 1957.     In 1958, the pacifist  Rau and his political mentor,  |Gustav_Heinemann|Gustav_Heinemann| , joined the  |Social_Democratic_Party_of_Germany|Social_Democratic_Party_Of_Germany|  , where he was active in the Wuppertal chapter. He served as deputy chairman of the SPD party of Wuppertal and was elected later on to the City Council , where he served as chairman of the SPD Group and later as Mayor .     In 1958, Rau was elected for the first time as member of the Landtag of  |North_Rhine_Westphalia|North_Rhine_Westphalia| . In 1967, he became chairman of the SPD fraction in the Landtag, and in 1970, he was Minister of Science and Education in the cabinet of Minister President  |Heinz_Kühn|Heinz_Kühn| . He soon gained a reputation as a reformer. As part of the mass education campaign of the 1970s, he founded five universities, each at different sites, in North Rhine-Westphalia and initiated Germany’s first  |distance_learning|Distance_Learning|  university at  |Hagen|Hagen|  .     In 1977, Rau became Chairman of the North Rhine-Westphalia SPD and, in 1978, Minister President of the state, which he remained until 1998, with four successful elections for the SPD, which became strongest party in the Landtag each time and gained an absolute majority three times, in 1980, 1985, 1990 and finally 1995. From 1995 onwards, Rau led an SPD- |Greens|Alliance_90_The_Greens|  coalition in North Rhine-Westphalia. Rau twice served as  |President|President_Of_The_German_Bundesrat|  of the  |Bundesrat|Bundesrat_Of_Germany|  in 1982/83 and 1994/95.     In 1987, Rau was his partys candidate to become chancellor of Germany for the SPD, but he lost the elections against  |Helmut_Kohl|Helmut_Kohl| ’s  |Christian_Democrats|Christian_Democratic_Union|  . In 1994, Rau was a candidate to become  |President_of_Germany|President_Of_Germany|  but lost to  |Roman_Herzog|Roman_Herzog| .     In 1998, Rau stepped down from his positions as SPD Chairman and Minister President, and on 23 May 1999, he was elected President of Germany by the  |Federal_Assembly_of_Germany|Federal_Assembly_Of_Germany|  to succeed  |Roman_Herzog|Roman_Herzog|  . On 1 July 2004, he was succeeded by  |Horst_Köhler|Horst_Köhler| . In common with all other Federal Presidents except for Heinemann, who had not wished to be seen off in this manner, Rau was honored by a  |Großer_Zapfenstreich|Großer_Zapfenstreich|  which, at his request, included the hymn Jesus bleibet meine Freude .     During 2000, Rau became the first German head of state to address the  |Knesset|Knesset| , the Israeli parliament, in German. The controversial step prompted some Israeli delegates to walk out. However, Israeli President  |Moshe_Katsav|Moshe_Katsav|  supported and praised him for bridging the gap between the two states. Rau had a deep and lifelong commitment to bringing reconciliation between Germany and its past.       Rau had a long history of heart disease and died 11 days after his 75th birthday on 27 January 2006. The funeral took place on 7 February following a funeral act of state on the  |Dorotheenstadt_cemetery|Dorotheenstadt_Cemetery|  in Berlin in the closest of family and friends.               The maxim of Rau was to reconcile, not divide .     As his personal motto, Rau adopted the  |Confessing_Church|Confessing_Church|  dictum teneo, quia teneor .     In his acceptance speech after his election, Rau claimed A patriot I will be because a patriot is someone who loves his fatherland, a nationalist is someone who despises the fatherlands of the others . The quote can be attributed to the French writer  |Romain_Gary|Romain_Gary| .       Rau was awarded fifteen honorary  |doctorates|Doctorate| .   In 2001, he received the  |Leo_Baeck_Medal|Leo_Baeck_Institute|  for his humanitarian work promoting tolerance and social justice.       Rau was known as a practising Christian . He held lay positions in, and was a member of, the  |Synod|Synod|  of the  |Evangelical_Church_in_the_Rhineland|Evangelical_Church_In_The_Rhineland| , a member church of the  |Evangelical_Church_in_Germany|Evangelical_Church_In_Germany| .     On 9 August 1982, Rau married the political scientist, Christina Delius . Christina Rau is a granddaughter of her husbands mentor,  |Gustav_Heinemann|Gustav_Heinemann| , former  |President_of_Germany|President_Of_Germany| . The couple had three children: Anna Christina, born 1983, Philip Immanuel, born 1985 and Laura Helene, born 1986.   Since 1995 Rau was aware of his dangerous  |aneurysm|Aneurysm|  in the  |abdominal_aorta|Abdominal_Aorta| , but declined an operation out of respect for his office and the upcoming election as president. On 23 July 2000 the operation took place at the University Hospital of Essen. On 18 August 2004, he had to undergo serious heart surgery, in which an  |artificial_heart_valve|Artificial_Heart_Valve|  was inserted. Only two months later a  |hematoma|Hematoma|  in the  |abdominal_cavity|Abdominal_Cavity|  was surgically removed.     After leaving office, Rau lived with his family in the federal capital,  |Berlin|Berlin| .
