This article is about the  |demographic|Demographics|  features of the  |population|Population|  of  |Eswatini|Eswatini| , including  |population_density|Population_Density| ,  |ethnicity|Ethnic_Group| , education level, health of the populace, economic status, religious affiliations and other aspects of the population.        The majority of  |Eswatinis|Eswatini|  population is ethnic  |Swazi|Swazi_People| , mixed with a small number of  |Zulus|Zulu_People|  and white Africans, predominantly of  |British|British_Diaspora_In_Africa|  and  |Afrikaner|Afrikaner|  origin. This population also includes a small segment within it that is mixed with any number of these ancestries.     Traditionally Swazis have been subsistence farmers and herders, but most now work in the growing urban formal economy and in government. Some Swazis work in the mines in  |South_Africa|South_Africa| . Eswatini also received  |Portuguese|Portuguese_People|  settlers and black refugees from  |Mozambique|Mozambique| .  |Christianity|Christianity|  in Eswatini is sometimes mixed with traditional beliefs and practices. Most Swazis ascribe a special spiritual role to the  |Swazi_Royal_Family|Swazi_Royal_Family| .     The countrys official languages are  |Siswati|Siswati|  and  |English|English_Language| . Government and commercial business is conducted mainly in English. Asians, Afrikaners, Portuguese, and black Mozambicans speak their own languages.          Eswatinis population is 1,467,152 according to the 2017 estimate from the  |CIA_World_Factbook|The_World_Factbook| .   The 2007 Census put the nations population at 912,229. This number is lower than the 1997 Census, which gave 929,718 residents. The small difference is believed to be the result of massive emigration of Swazis to South Africa in search of work.      According to the 2010 revision of the World Population Prospects the total population was 1,186,000 in 2010, compared to only 273,000 in 1950. The proportion of children below the age of 15 in 2010 was 38.4%, 58.2% was between 15 and 65 years of age, while 3.4% was 65 years or older   .
