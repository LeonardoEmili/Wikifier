The Cornwall Wildlife Trust is a charitable organisation founded in 1962 that is concerned solely with  |Cornwall|Cornwall| , England.     It deals with the conservation and preservation of Cornwalls wildlife and habitats managing over 50 nature reserves covering approximately , amongst them  |Looe_Island|Looe_Island| .     Cornwall Wildlife Trust is part of  |The_Wildlife_Trusts_partnership|The_Wildlife_Trusts_Partnership|  of 47 wildlife trusts in the  |United_Kingdom|United_Kingdom| . It works in conjunction with the  |Isles_of_Scilly_Wildlife_Trust|Isles_Of_Scilly_Wildlife_Trust| , and jointly produces a thrice yearly magazine called Wild Cornwall & Wild Scilly     The direction and work that the Trust currently does is guided by the Cornwall  |Biodiversity|Biodiversity|  action plan. Living Seas and Living Landscapes are two such projects. The Trust runs ERCCIS , a county wide database of sightings of animals and plants. It also gives  |planning|Land_Use_Planning|  advice to land developers.     The Trust is based at  |Allet|Allet|  near  |Truro|Truro|  in  |Cornwall|Cornwall| .
