     Sigtuna Municipality  is a  |municipality|Municipalities_Of_Sweden|  in  |Stockholm_County|Stockholm_County|  in east central  |Sweden|Sweden| . Its seat is located in the town of  |Märsta|Märsta| , approximately  north of the Swedish capital,  |Stockholm|Stockholm| .     The municipality is a part of  |Metropolitan_Stockholm|Metropolitan_Stockholm| .     The municipality consists of several former local government units and was formed in 1971. It got its name from the small, but very old, City of Sigtuna , but the seat was placed in the larger modern town of  |Märsta|Märsta| .     The three towns of the municipality are  |Märsta|Märsta|  ,  |Sigtuna|Sigtuna|  and  |Rosersberg|Rosersberg|  , of which Märsta is the municipal seat and Sigtuna with its old and important history is a popular  |tourist_destination|Tourist_Destination| .                On the 31st of December 2017 the number of people with a  |foreign_background|:Sv:Utländsk_Bakgrund|  was 20  291, or 43.04% of the population . On the 31st of December 2002 the number of residents with a foreign background was 9  426, or 26.35% of the population .  On 31 December 2017 there were 47  146 residents in Sigtuna, of which 15  268 people were born in a country other than Sweden.
