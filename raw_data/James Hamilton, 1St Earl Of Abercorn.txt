           James Hamilton, 1st Earl of Abercorn  |PC|Privy_Council_Of_Scotland|  was a Scottish peer and diplomat for  |James_VI_and_I|James_Vi_And_I| . He also was an important undertaker in the  |Plantations_of_Ireland|Plantations_Of_Ireland|  of that king.          James was born on 12 August 1575, probably at  |Paisley|Paisley_Renfrewshire| , as the eldest son of  |Claud_Hamilton|Claud_Hamilton_1St_Lord_Paisley|  and his wife Margaret Seton. His father was the first Lord Paisley. His paternal grandfather was  |James_Hamilton_2nd_Earl_of_Arran|James_Hamilton_2Nd_Earl_Of_Arran| . His mother was a daughter of George Seton, 6th Lord Seton.  His parents married in 1574. Several of his siblings died in their infancy or childhood. Six reached adulthood:     He heads the list of siblings below as the eldest:   James , the subject of this article;   John, who married Johanna Everard, daughter of Levimus Everard;   Claud , of Shawfield, who was appointed to the Privy Council of Ireland, and whose daughter Margaret married Sir  |John_Stewart_of_Methven|John_Stewart_Of_Methven| ;   George of Greenlaw and Roscrea, who lived at Derrywoone Castle in  |County_Tyrone|County_Tyrone| ;    |Frederick|Frederick_Hamilton|  , who built  |Manorhamilton|Manorhamilton|  and served Sweden in the  |Thirty_Years_War|Thirty_Years_War| ;   Margaret , who married  |William_Douglas_1st_Marquess_of_Douglas|William_Douglas_1St_Marquess_Of_Douglas| .       Shortly before or in 1592 he married Marion, daughter of  |Thomas_Boyd_6th_Lord_Boyd|Thomas_Boyd_6Th_Lord_Boyd| . She was a prominent  |Roman_Catholic|Roman_Catholic|  and would in 1628 be excommunicated by the synod of the  |Church_of_Scotland|Church_Of_Scotland|  in Glasgow. after his death. They had nine children, five boys and four girls:   Anne , who married  |Hugh_Sempill_5th_Lord_Sempill|Hugh_Sempill_5Th_Lord_Sempill|  in 1611;   Isobel ;    |James|James_Hamilton_2Nd_Earl_Of_Abercorn|  , who became the 2nd Earl of Abercorn;    |Claud|Claud_Hamilton_2Nd_Baron_Hamilton_Of_Strabane|  , who established himself in Ireland;   William , who became  |Baronet_Hamilton_of_Westport|Hamilton_Baronetshamilton_Baronets_Of_West_Port|  and represented  |Henrietta_Maria|Henrietta_Maria_Of_France| , Charles Is widow, at the pope;    |George|Sir_George_Hamilton_1St_Baronet_Of_Donalong|  , who became  |Baronet_Hamilton_of_Donalong|Hamilton_Baronetshamilton_Baronets_Of_Donalong_And_Nenagh| ;   Lucy , for whom a marriage was arranged with  |Randal_MacDonnell_1st_Marquess_of_Antrim|Randal_Macdonnell_1St_Marquess_Of_Antrim| , but the wedding never took place;   Alexander , who founded the German branch of the family; and   Margaret , who married Sir William Cunninghame of Caprington.
