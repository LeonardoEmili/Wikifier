         Politics of Macau is a framework of  |political_system|Political_System| , dominated by the  |Peoples_Republic_of_China|China| . It includes the  |legislature|Legislative_Assembly_Of_Macau| , the  |judiciary|Judiciary_Of_Macau| , the  |government|Government_Of_Macau| , and a  |multi-party_system|Multi-Party_System| .  |Executive_power|Executive|  is exercised by the government, led by the Chief Executive.       In accordance with Article 31 of the  |Constitution_of_the_Peoples_Republic_of_China|Constitution_Of_The_Peoples_Republic_Of_China| , Macau has  |Special_Administrative_Region|Special_Administrative_Region_Of_The_Peoples_Republic_Of_China|  status, which provides constitutional guarantees for implementing the policy of  |one_country,_two_systems|One_Country,_Two_Systems|  and the constitutional basis for enacting the  |Basic_Law_of_the_Macau_Special_Administrative_Region|Basic_Law_Of_The_Macau_Special_Administrative_Region| . Although geographically part of  |Guangdong|Guangdong|  Province, the Macau Special Administrative Region is directly under the authority of the central government of the Peoples Republic of China in Beijing, which controls the foreign affairs and defence of Macau but otherwise grants the region a high degree of authority. The Basic Law took force upon handover of sovereignty from  |Portugal|Portugal|  on 20 December 1999, and is to remain in effect for fifty years .     Macaus seven deputies to the  |National_Peoples_Congress|National_Peoples_Congress|  are selected by an electoral conference; they attended their first session of the NPC in Beijing in March 2000. Previously, in December 1999, the NPC Standing Committee approved the membership of the NPC Committee for the Basic Law of the Macau Special Administrative Region, chaired by NPC Vice Chairman  |Qiao_Xiaoyang|Qiao_Xiaoyang| , for a five-year term. Half of the ten members are from Macau, the others from mainland China.
