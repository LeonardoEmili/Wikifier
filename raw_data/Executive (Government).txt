         The executive is the branch of government exercising  |authority|Authority|  in and holding  |responsibility|Moral_Responsibility|  for the  |governance|Governance|  of a  |state|State| . The executive executes and enforces  |law|Law| .     In  |political_systems|Political_System|  based on the principle of  |separation_of_powers|Separation_Of_Powers| ,  |authority|Authority|  is distributed among several branches —an attempt to prevent the concentration of power in the hands of a single group of people. In such a system, the executive does not pass laws or interpret them . Instead, the executive enforces the law as written by the legislature and interpreted by the judiciary. The executive can be the source of certain types of law, such as a  |decree|Decree|  or  |executive_order|Executive_Order| . Executive bureaucracies are commonly the source of  |regulations|Regulation| .       In the  |Westminster_political_system|Westminster_System| , the principle of separation of powers is not as entrenched as in some others. Members of the executive, called  |ministers|Minister| , are also members of the legislature, and hence play an important part in both the writing and enforcing of law.     In this context, the executive consists of a leader of an office or multiple offices. Specifically, the top leadership roles of the executive branch may include:      |head_of_state|Head_Of_State|  – often the  |supreme_leader|Supreme_Leader| , the  |president|President|  or  |monarch|Monarch| , the chief public representative and living symbol of national unity.    |head_of_government|Head_Of_Government|  – often the  |prime_minister|Prime_Minister| , overseeing the administration of all affairs of state.    |defence_minister|Defence_Minister|  – overseeing the  |armed_forces|Armed_Forces| , determining military policy and managing external safety.    |interior_minister|Interior_Minister|  – overseeing the  |police_forces|Police_Forces| , enforcing the law and managing internal safety.    |foreign_minister|Foreign_Minister|  – overseeing the  |diplomatic_service|Diplomatic_Service| , determining  |foreign_policy|Foreign_Policy|  and managing  |foreign_relations|Foreign_Relations| .    |finance_minister|Finance_Minister|  – overseeing the treasury, determining  |fiscal_policy|Fiscal_Policy|  and managing  |national_budget|National_Budget| .    |justice_minister|Justice_Minister|  – overseeing criminal prosecutions, corrections, enforcement of court orders.         In a  |presidential_system|Presidential_System| , the leader of the executive is both the head of state and head of government.  In a  |parliamentary_system|Parliamentary_System| , a  |cabinet|Cabinet| |minister|Minister| |responsible_to_the|Vote_Of_No_Confidence| |legislature|Legislature|  is the head of government, while the head of state is usually a largely ceremonial monarch or president.
