        Paul J. McAuley is a British botanist and science fiction author.     A biologist by training, McAuley writes mostly  |hard_science_fiction|Hard_Science_Fiction| . His novels dealing with themes such as  |biotechnology|Biotechnology| ,  |alternative_history|Alternate_History| /alternative reality, and  |space_travel|Spaceflight| .     McAuley began with far-future  |space_opera|Space_Opera|   Four Hundred Billion Stars , its sequel Eternal Light, and the planetary-colony adventure  Of the Fall . Red Dust, set on a far-future Mars colonized by the Chinese, is a  |planetary_romance|Planetary_Romance|  featuring many  |emerging_technologies|Emerging_Technologies|  and SF motifs:  |nanotechnology|Nanotechnology| ,  |biotechnology|Biotechnology| ,  |artificial_intelligence|Artificial_Intelligence| , personality downloads,  |virtual_reality|Virtual_Reality| . The Confluence series, set in an even more distant future , is one of a number of novels to use  |Frank_J._Tiplers|Frank_J._Tipler| |Omega_Point|Omega_Point|  Theory as one of its themes.    About the same time, he published Pasquales Angel, set in an alternative  |Italian_Renaissance|Italian_Renaissance|  and featuring Niccolò Machiavegli  and  |Leonardo_da_Vinci|Leonardo_Da_Vinci|  as major characters.     McAuley has also used biotechnology and nanotechnology themes in near-future settings:  Fairyland  describes a dystopian, war-torn Europe where genetically engineered dolls are used as disposable slaves. Since 2001 he has produced several SF-based  |techno-thrillers|Techno-Thriller|  such as The Secret of Life, Whole Wide World, and White Devils.
