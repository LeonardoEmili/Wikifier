      Commonwealth  is a traditional English term for a political community founded for the  |common_good|Common_Good| . Historically it has sometimes been synonymous with  |republic|Republic|  . The noun commonwealth , meaning public welfare general good or advantage , dates from the 15th century. Originally a phrase it comes from the old meaning of  |wealth|Wealth|  , which is well-being , and is itself a loose translation of the Latin  |res_publica|Res_Publica|  . The term literally meant common well-being . In the 17th century, the definition of commonwealth expanded from its original sense of  |public_welfare|Public_Welfare|  or  |commonweal|Wikt_Commonweal|  to mean a state in which the supreme power is vested in the people; a republic or  |democratic_state|Democracy|  .     The term evolved to become a title to a number of political entities. Three countries –  |Australia|Australia| , the  |Bahamas|Bahamas| , and  |Dominica|Dominica|  – have the official title Commonwealth , as do four  |U_S_states|U_S_State|  and two  |U_S_territories|Territories_Of_The_United_States| . Since the early 20th century, the term has been used to name some fraternal associations of nations, most notably the  |Commonwealth_of_Nations|Commonwealth_Of_Nations| , an organization primarily of former territories of the  |British_Empire|British_Empire| , which is often referred to as simply the Commonwealth .             Translations of Roman writers works to English have on occasion translated  Res publica  , and variants thereof, to the commonwealth , a term referring to the Roman state as a whole.         The  |Commonwealth_of_England|Commonwealth_Of_England|  was the official name of the political unit  that replaced the  |Kingdom_of_England|Kingdom_Of_England|   from 1649–53 and 1659–60, under the rule of  |Oliver_Cromwell|Oliver_Cromwell|  and his son and successor  |Richard|Richard_Cromwell| . From 1653 to 1659, although still legally known as a Commonwealth, the republic, united with the former  |Kingdom_of_Scotland|Kingdom_Of_Scotland| , operated under different institutions  and is known by historians as the Protectorate. In a British context, it is sometimes referred to as the Old Commonwealth .         The  |Icelandic_Commonwealth|Icelandic_Commonwealth|  or the  |Icelandic_Free_State|Icelandic_Free_State|   was the state existing in Iceland between the establishment of the  |Althing|Althing|  in 930 and the pledge of fealty to the  |Norwegian_king|King_Of_Norway|  in 1262. It was initially established by a public consisting largely of recent immigrants from  |Norway|Norway|  who had fled the unification of that country under  |King|King| |_Harald_Fairhair|Harald_Fairhair| .            The  |Commonwealth_of_the_Philippines|Commonwealth_Of_The_Philippines|  was the administrative body that governed the  |Philippines|Philippines|  from 1935 to 1946, aside from a  |period_of_exile|Government_In_Exile|  in the  |Second_World_War|Second_World_War|  from 1942 to 1945 when  |Japan_occupied_the_country|Japanese_Occupation_Of_The_Philippines| . It replaced the  |Insular_Government|Insular_Government| , a  |United_States_territorial|Territory_Of_The_United_States|  government, and was established by the  |Tydings–McDuffie_Act|Tydings–Mcduffie_Act| . The Commonwealth was designed as a transitional administration in preparation for the countrys full achievement of independence, which was achieved in 1946. The Commonwealth of the Philippines was a founding member of the  |United_Nations|United_Nations| .             Republic is still an alternative translation of the traditional name of the  |Polish–Lithuanian_Commonwealth|Polish–Lithuanian_Commonwealth| .  |Wincenty_Kadłubek|Wincenty_Kadłubek|  used for the first time the original Latin term  res publica  in the context of Poland in his Chronicles of the Kings and Princes of Poland . The name was used officially for the confederal country formed by  |Poland|Crown_Of_The_Kingdom_Of_Poland|  and  |Lithuania|Grand_Duchy_Of_Lithuania|  1569–1795.     It is also often referred as  |Nobles_Commonwealth|Golden_Liberty|  . In the contemporary political doctrine of the Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth, our state is a Republic under the presidency of the King . The Commonwealth introduced a doctrine of religious tolerance called  |Warsaw_Confederation|Warsaw_Confederation| , had its own parliament  Sejm  .     A commonwealth of good counsaile was the title of the 1607 English translation of the work of  |Wawrzyniec_Grzymała_Goślicki|Wawrzyniec_Grzymała_Goślicki|  De optimo senatore that presented to English readers many of the ideas present in the political system of the Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth.            Between 1914 and 1925,  |Catalonia|Catalonia|  was an autonomous region of Spain. Its government during that time was given the title  mancomunidad  , which is translated into English as commonwealth . The  |Commonwealth_of_Catalonia|Commonwealth_Of_Catalonia|  had limited powers and was formed as a federation of the four Catalan provinces. A number of Catalan-language institutions were created during its existence.            Between 1838 and 1847,  |Liberia|Liberia|  was officially known as the Commonwealth of Liberia . It changed its name to the Republic of Liberia when it declared independence in 1847. All four were part of  |Great_Britains_possessions|British_America|  along the Atlantic coast of North America prior to the formation of the United States of America in 1776. As such, they share a strong influence of English  |common_law|Common_Law|  in some of their laws and institutions.
