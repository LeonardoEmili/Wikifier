A slow fire is a term used in  |library_and_information_science|Library_And_Information_Science|  to describe  |paper|Paper|  embrittlement resulting from acid decay. The term is taken from the title of Terry Sanders 1987 film Slow Fires: On the preservation of the human record.     Solutions to this problem include the use of  |acid-free_paper|Acid-Free_Paper|  stocks, reformatting brittle books by microfilming, photocopying or digitization, and a variety of  |deacidification|Mass_Deacidification|.
