The United States territory of  |Guam|Guam|  has no  |railways|Railway|  or  |freeways|Freeway| , nor does it have a  |merchant_marine|Merchant_Marine| . The largest  |port|Seaport|  is  |Apra_Harbor|Apra_Harbor| ,  which serves almost all commercial traffic including cruise, cargo and fishing vessels. There are smaller harbors located on the island which serve recreational boaters. Roads are primarily paved by a  |coral|Coral| /oil mixture that when it gets wet, the oil tends to float to the surface of the roads making them dangerous. This is one of the reasons the  |speed_limit|Speed_Limit|  on most of the island is 35  mph. But, during road repair or maintenance, a different mixture of asphalt that is not as slippery is used. Its main commercial  |airport|Airport|  is the  |Antonio_B_Won_Pat_International_Airport|Antonio_B_Won_Pat_International_Airport| .
