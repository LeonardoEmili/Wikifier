Baron Aberdare, of Duffryn in the  |County_of_Glamorgan|Glamorgan| , is a title in the  |Peerage_of_the_United_Kingdom|Peerage_Of_The_United_Kingdom| . It was created on 23 August 1873 for the  |Liberal|Liberal_Party|  politician  |Henry_Bruce|Henry_Bruce_1St_Baron_Aberdare| . He served as  |Home_Secretary|Home_Secretary|  from 1868 to 1873. His grandson, the third Baron, was a soldier, cricketer and tennis player and a member of the  |International_Olympic_Committee|International_Olympic_Committee| . His son, the fourth Baron, held office in the  |Conservative_administration|Conservative_Government_1970_1974|  of  |Edward_Heath|Edward_Heath|  and was later a Deputy Speaker of the  |House_of_Lords|House_Of_Lords| . Lord Aberdare was one of the ninety-two elected  |hereditary_peers|Hereditary_Peer|  that were allowed to remain in the House of Lords after the passing of the  |House_of_Lords_Act_1999|House_Of_Lords_Act_1999| .  the title is held by his son, the fifth Baron, who succeeded in 2005 and was elected to the House of Lords in 2009.
