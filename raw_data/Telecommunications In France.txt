Telecommunications in France is highly developed. France is served by an extensive system of automatic  |telephone_exchanges|Telephone_Exchange|  connected by modern networks of  |fiber_optic|Fiber_Optic|  cable,  |coaxial_cable|Coaxial_Cable| ,  |microwave_radio_relay|Microwave_Radio_Relay| , and a domestic satellite system; cellular telephone service is widely available, expanding rapidly, and includes  |roaming|Roaming|  service to foreign countries.       The telephony system employs an extensive system of modern network elements such as digital  |telephone_exchanges|Telephone_Exchange| ,  |mobile_switching_centres|Mobile_Switching_Centre| ,  |media_gateways|Media_Gateway|  and  |signalling_gateways|Signalling_Gateway|  at the core, interconnected by a wide variety of transmission systems using  |fibre_optics|Fibre_Optics|  or  |Microwave_radio_relay|Microwave_Transmission|  networks. The  |access_network|Access_Network| , which connects the subscriber to the core, is highly diversified with different copper-pair, optic-fibre and wireless technologies. The fixed-line telecommunications market is dominated by the former state-owned monopoly  |France_Telecom|France_Telecom| .
