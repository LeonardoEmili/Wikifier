Earth is one of the four  |classical_elements|Classical_Element|  in ancient  |Greek_philosophy|Greek_Philosophy|  and science. It was commonly associated with qualities of heaviness, matter and the terrestrial world. Due to the  |hero_cults|Hero_Cult| , and  |chthonic|Chthonic| |_underworld_deities|List_Of_Greek_Mythological_Figureschthonic_Deities| , the element of earth is also associated with the sensual aspects of both life and death in later  |occultism|Occultism| .      |Empedocles|Empedocles|  of Acragas  proposed four  archai  by which to understand the  |cosmos|Cosmos|  fire , air ,  water , and earth.  |Plato|Plato|   believed the elements were geometric forms  and he assigned the  |cube|Cube|  to the element of earth in his dialogue  Timaeus . Plato, Timaeus, chap. 22–23;  |Gregory_Vlastos|Gregory_Vlastos| , Platos Universe, pp. 66–82.  |Aristotle|Aristotle|  believed earth was the heaviest element, and his theory of  natural place  suggested that any earth–laden substances, would fall quickly, straight down, towards the center of the cosmos.  |G_E_R_Lloyd|G_E_R_Lloyd| , Aristotle, chapters 7–8.     In Classical  |Greek|Greek_Mythology|  and  |Roman|Roman_Mythology|  myth, various goddesses   represented the Earth, seasons, crops and fertility, including  |Demeter|Demeter|  and  |Persephone|Persephone| ;  |Ceres|Ceres| ; the  |Horae|Horae|  , and  |Proserpina|Proserpina| ; and  |Hades|Hades|   who ruled the souls of dead in the  |Underworld|Underworld| .     In  |ancient_Greek_medicine|Medicine_In_Ancient_Greece| , each of the  |four_humours|Four_Humours|  became associated with an element.  |Black_bile|Melancholia|  was the humor identified with earth, since both were cold and dry. Other things associated with earth and black bile in ancient and  |medieval_medicine|Medieval_Medicine|  included the season of  |fall|Fall| , since it increased the qualities of cold and aridity; the melancholic temperament ; the  |feminine|Femininity| ; and the southern point of the compass.          In  |alchemy|Alchemy| , earth was believed to be primarily dry, and secondarily cold, .  Beyond those classical attributes, the chemical substance  |salt|Salt| , was associated with earth and its  |alchemical_symbol|Alchemical_Symbol|  was a downward-pointing triangle, bisected by a horizontal line. In the  |Rigveda|Rigveda| , earth and sky are frequently addressed as a  |duality|Dualism| , often indicated by the idea of two complementary half-shells. In addition, the element Earth is associated with  |Budha|Budha|  or Mercury who represents communication, business, mathematics and other practical matters.       Earth and the other Greek classical elements were incorporated into the  |Golden_Dawn|Hermetic_Order_Of_The_Golden_Dawn|  system. The  |elemental_weapon|Magical_Weapon|  of earth is the  |Pentacle|Pentacle| . Regardie, Golden Dawn, p.322; Kraig, Modern Magick, pp. 149-53. Each of the elements has several associated spiritual beings. Regardie, Golden Dawn, pp. 280-286; Kraig, Modern Magick, pp. 206-209. Many of these associations have since spread throughout the occult community.
