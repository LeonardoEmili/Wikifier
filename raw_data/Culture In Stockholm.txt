   Apart from being a large city with an active cultural life,  |Stockholm|Stockholm| , the capital of Sweden, houses many national cultural institutions. There are two  |UNESCO|Unesco| |World_Heritage_sites|World_Heritage_Site|  in the  |Stockholm_County|Stockholm_County|  area: the  |Royal_Palace_Drottningholm|Drottningholm_Palace|   and the  |Skogskyrkogården|Skogskyrkogården|  .     Stockholm was the 1998  |European_City_of_Culture|European_Capital_Of_Culture| .       Authors connected to Stockholm include the poet and songwriter  |Carl_Michael_Bellman|Carl_Michael_Bellman|  , novelist and dramatist  |August_Strindberg|August_Strindberg|  , and novelist  |Hjalmar_Söderberg|Hjalmar_Söderberg|  , all of whom made Stockholm part of their works. Other authors with notable heritage in Stockholm were the  |Nobel_Prize_laureate|Nobel_Prize_In_Literature| |Eyvind_Johnson|Eyvind_Johnson|  and the popular poet and composer  |Evert_Taube|Evert_Taube|  . The novelist  |Per_Anders_Fogelström|Per_Anders_Fogelström|  wrote a popular series of historical novels depicting life in Stockholm from the 19th to the mid-20th century.               The citys oldest section is  |Gamla_stan|Gamla_Stan|  , located on the original small islands of the citys earliest settlements and still featuring the  |medieval|Medieval|  street layout. Some notable buildings of Gamla Stan are the large  |German_Church|German_Church,_Stockholm|   and several mansions and palaces: the  Riddarhuset  , the  |Bonde_Palace|Bonde_Palace| , the  |Tessin_Palace|Tessin_Palace|  and the  |Oxenstierna_Palace|Oxenstierna_Palace| . The oldest building in Stockholm is the  |Riddarholmskyrkan|Riddarholmskyrkan|  from the late 13th century. After a fire in 1697 when the original medieval castle was destroyed,  |Stockholm_Palace|Stockholm_Palace|  was erected in a  |baroque|Baroque|  style.  |Storkyrkan|Storkyrkan|  Cathedral, the episcopal seat of the Bishop of Stockholm, stands next to the castle. It was founded in the 13th century but is clad in a baroque exterior dating to the 18th century.     As early as the 15th century, the city had expanded outside of its original borders. Some pre-industrial, small-scale buildings from this era can still be found in  |Södermalm|Södermalm| . During the 19th century and the age of industrialization Stockholm grew rapidly, with plans and architecture inspired by the large cities of the continent such as  |Berlin|Berlin|  and  |Vienna|Vienna| . Notable works of this time period include public buildings such as the  |Royal_Swedish_Opera|Royal_Swedish_Opera|  and private developments such as the luxury housing developments on  |Strandvägen|Strandvägen| .        In the 20th century, a nationalistic push spurred a new architectural style inspired by medieval and renaissance ancestry as well as influences of the  |Jugend|Jugend|  /  |Art_Nouveau|Art_Nouveau|  style. A key landmark of Stockholm, the  |Stockholm_City_Hall|Stockholm_City_Hall| , was erected 1911–1923 by architect  |Ragnar_Östberg|Ragnar_Östberg| . Other notable works of these times are the  |Stockholm_Public_Library|Stockholm_Public_Library|  and the Forest Cemetery,  |Skogskyrkogården|Skogskyrkogården| |Modernism|Modernism|  characterized the style of the  |Stockholm_International_Exhibition|Stockholm_International_Exhibition|  and the development of the city as it grew in that decade. New residential areas sprang up such as the development on  |Gärdet|Gärdet|  while industrial development added to the growth, such as the KF manufacturing industries on Kvarnholmen located in the  |Nacka_Municipality|Nacka_Municipality| . In the 1950s, suburban development entered a new phase with the introduction of the  |Stockholm_metro|Stockholm_Metro| . The modernist developments of  |Vällingby|Vällingby|  and  |Farsta|Farsta|  where internationally praised. In the 1960s this suburban development continued but with the aesthetic of the times, the industrialised and mass-produced blocks of flats received a large amount of criticism.     At the same time that this suburban development was happening the most central areas of the inner city were being redesigned.  |Sergels_Torg|Sergels_Torg| , with its  |five_high-rise_office_towers|Hötorget_Buildings|  was created in the 1960s, followed by the total clearance of large areas to make room for new development projects. The most notable buildings from this period is the ensemble of the  |House_of_Culture|The_House_Of_Culture| ,  |City_Theatre|Stockholm_City_Theatre|  and  |National_Bank|Sveriges_Riksbank|  building, designed by architect  |Peter_Celsing|Peter_Celsing| . One of the most renowned museums is the  |Nationalmuseum|Nationalmuseum| , with the largest national collection of art: 16,000 paintings and 30,000 objects of art handicraft. The collection dates back to the days of  |Gustav_Vasa|Gustav_Vasa|  in the 16th century, and has since been expanded with works by artists such as  |Rembrandt|Rembrandt| , and  |Antoine_Watteau|Antoine_Watteau| , as well as constituting a main part of Swedens art heritage, manifested in the works of  |Alexander_Roslin|Alexander_Roslin| ,  |Anders_Zorn|Anders_Zorn| ,  |Johan_Tobias_Sergel|Johan_Tobias_Sergel| ,  |Carl_Larsson|Carl_Larsson| ,  |Carl_Fredrik_Hill|Carl_Fredrik_Hill|  and  |Ernst_Josephson|Ernst_Josephson| .     The Museum of Modern Art, or  |Moderna_Museet|Moderna_Museet| , is Swedens national museum of modern art. It has works by famous modern artists such as  |Picasso|Picasso|  and  |Salvador_Dalí|Salvador_Dalí| . Distinguished among Stockholms many theatres are the  |Royal_Dramatic_Theatre|Royal_Dramatic_Theatre|  , one of Europes most renowned theatres, and the  |Royal_Swedish_Opera|Royal_Swedish_Opera| , inaugurated in 1773.     Other notable theatres are the  |Stockholm_City_Theatre|Stockholm_City_Theatre| , the Peoples Opera , the Modern Theatre of Dance , the  |China_Theatre|Chinateatern| , the  |Göta_Lejon|Göta_Lejon|  Theatre, the  |Mosebacke_Theatre|Mosebacke| , and the  |Oscar_Theatre|Oscarsteatern| .     The stages of Stockholm number in their fifties at the least, and a wide variety of plays are constantly on, from classical to newly written.       Stockholm is basically the media center of Sweden. It has four nationwide daily newspapers, is also the central location of the publicly funded radio  and television ; in addition, all other major television channels have their base in Stockholm . All major magazines are also located to Stockholm, as are the largest literature publisher, the  |Bonnier|Bonnier_Group|  group.          The most popular spectator sports are  |football|Association_Football|  and  |ice_hockey|Ice_Hockey| . The three most popular football teams are  |AIK|Aik_Fotboll| ,  |Hammarby_IF|Hammarby_If|  and  |Djurgårdens_IF|Djurgårdens_If_Fotboll| .     Historically, the city was the host of the  |1912_Summer_Olympics|1912_Summer_Olympics| . From those days stem the  |Stockholms_Olympiastadion|Stockholms_Olympiastadion|  which has since hosted numerous sports events, notably football and athletics. Stadion is the previous home arena of  |AIK|Aik|  and is the current home arena of Djurgårdens IF. For  |Sweden_mens_national_ice_hockey_team|Sweden_Mens_National_Ice_Hockey_Team| , the home arena is  |Ericsson_Globe|Ericsson_Globe| , one of the largest spherical building in the world, but it is also hosting concerts and other events.
