          In the  |Netherlands|Netherlands| , Liberation Day  is celebrated each year on May the 5th to mark the end of the  |occupation|Netherlands_In_World_War_Ii|  by  |Nazi_Germany|Nazi_Germany|  during  |World_War_II|World_War_Ii| .     The nation was liberated by Canadian forces, British infantry divisions, the  |British_I_Corps|I_Corps| , the  |1st_Polish_Armoured_Division|1St_Polish_Armoured_Division| ,  |American|Timberwolf_Division| ,  |Belgian|1St_Belgian_Infantry_Brigade| ,  |Dutch|Royal_Netherlands_Motorized_Infantry_Brigade|  and  |Czechoslovak|1St_Czechoslovak_Armoured_Brigade|  troops. Parts of the country, in particular the south-east, were liberated by the  |British_Second_Army|Second_Army|  which included American and Polish airborne forces  and French airbornes . On 5 May 1945 the Canadian  |General|General| |_Charles_Foulkes|Charles_Foulkes|  and the German  |Commander_in_Chief|Commander_In_Chief| |_Johannes_Blaskowitz|Johannes_Blaskowitz|  reached an agreement on the  |capitulation|Capitulation|  of German forces in the Netherlands in  |Hotel_de_Wereld|Hotel_De_Wereld|  in  |Wageningen|Wageningen| . One day later the capitulation document was signed in the auditorium of  |Wageningen_University|Wageningen_University|  located next door.     After liberation in 1945 Liberation Day was celebrated every five years. In 1990 the day was declared a  |national_holiday|Public_Holiday|  when liberation would be remembered and celebrated every year.     On May 4 the Dutch hold Dodenherdenking  |Remembrance_of_the_Dead|Remembrance_Of_The_Dead|  for the people who  |fought_and_died|Killed_In_Action|  during  |World_War_II|World_War_Ii|  and in wars in general. There are remembrance gatherings all over cities and in the country, the better-known at the  |National_Monument|National_Monument|  on  |Dam_Square|Dam_Square|  in  |Amsterdam|Amsterdam|  and at the  |Waalsdorpervlakte|Waalsdorpervlakte|  in the dunes near The Hague, one of the infamous Nazi execution places. Throughout the country two  |minutes_of_silence|Minutes_Of_Silence| .
