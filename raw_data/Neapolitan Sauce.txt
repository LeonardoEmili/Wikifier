Neapolitan sauce, also called Napoli sauce or Napoletana sauce, is the collective name given  to various basic  |tomato|Tomato| -based  |sauces|Sauce|  derived from  |Italian_cuisine|Italian_Cuisine| , often served over or alongside  |pasta|Pasta| .     In  |Naples|Naples| , Neapolitan sauce is simply referred to as la salsa, which literally translates to the sauce.  |Basil|Basil| ,  |bay_leaf|Bay_Leaf| ,  |thyme|Thyme| ,  |oregano|Oregano| ,  |peppercorns|Black_Pepper| ,  |cloves|Clove| ,  |olives|Olive| , and  |mushrooms|Edible_Mushroom|  may be included depending on taste preferences. Some variants include  |carrots|Carrot|  and  |celery|Celery| .  The basic sauce is  |vegetarian|Vegetarian| , although meat such as minced beef or sausage can be added.     Many Italians do not know what Neapolitan sauce is, especially in association with some recipe names such as, for instance, spaghetti napolitana . The name itself, in fact, is not even spelled in proper Italian.
