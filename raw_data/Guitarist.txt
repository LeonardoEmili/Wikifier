         A guitarist  is a person who plays the  |guitar|Guitar| . Guitarists may play a variety of guitar family  |instruments|Musical_Instrument|  such as  |classical_guitars|Classical_Guitar| ,  |acoustic_guitars|Acoustic_Guitar| ,  |electric_guitars|Electric_Guitar| , and  |bass_guitars|Bass_Guitar| . Some guitarists accompany themselves on the guitar by  |singing|Singing|  or playing the harmonica.       The guitarist may employ any of several methods for sounding the guitar, including finger picking, depending on the type of strings used , and including  |strumming|Strumming|  with the fingers, or a  |guitar_pick|Guitar_Pick|  made of bone, horn, plastic, metal, felt, leather, or paper, and melodic  |flatpicking|Flatpicking|  and  |finger-picking|Fingerstyle_Guitar| .     The guitarist may also employ various methods for selecting notes and chords, including fingering, thumbing, the barre , and bottleneck or steel-guitar slides, usually made of glass or metal. These left- and right-hand techniques may be intermixed in performance.              Several  |magazines|Magazine|  and  |websites|Website|  have compiled what they intend as lists of the greatest guitarists—for example The 100 Greatest Guitarists of All Time by  Rolling Stone  magazine, or 100 Greatest Guitarists of All Time by  Guitar World  magazine.     ;Rolling Stone Rolling Stone  magazine published a list called The 100 Greatest Guitarists of All Time. This list included 100 guitarists whom the magazine editor  |David_Fricke|David_Fricke|  considered the best, with a brief introduction for each of them.  The first in this list is the American guitarist  |Jimi_Hendrix|Jimi_Hendrix|  introduced by  |Pete_Townshend|Pete_Townshend| , guitarist for  |The_Who|The_Who| , who was, in his turn, ranked at 50 in the list.     :In describing the list to readers, Paul MacInnes from British newspaper  The Guardian  wrote, Surprisingly enough for an American magazine, the top 10 is fair jam-packed with Yanks, though he also noted three exceptions in the top 10.  The online magazine  Blogcritics  criticized the list for introducing some  allegedly undeserving guitarists while forgetting some artists the writer considered perhaps more worthy, such as  |Johnny_Marr|Johnny_Marr| ,  |Al_Di_Meola|Al_Di_Meola| ,  |Phil_Keaggy|Phil_Keaggy|  or  |John_Petrucci|John_Petrucci| .      :In 2011, Rolling Stone updated the list, which this time was chosen by a panel of guitarists and other experts with the top 5 consisting of  |Jimi_Hendrix|Jimi_Hendrix| ,  |Eric_Clapton|Eric_Clapton| ,  |Jimmy_Page|Jimmy_Page| ,  |Keith_Richards|Keith_Richards|  and  |Jeff_Beck|Jeff_Beck| .
