When Sultan  |Qaboos_bin_Said_Al_Said|Qaboos_Bin_Said_Al_Said|  assumed power in 1970,  |Oman|Oman|  had limited contacts with the outside world, including neighbouring Arab states. A special treaty relationship permitted the United Kingdom close involvement in Omans civil and military affairs. Ties with the United Kingdom have remained very close under Sultan Qaboos along with strong ties to the United States.     Since 1970, Oman has pursued a moderate foreign policy and expanded its diplomatic relations dramatically. It supported the 1979  |Camp_David_accords|Camp_David_Accords|  and was one of three  |Arab_League|Arab_League|  states, along with  |Somalia|Somalia|  and  |Sudan|Sudan| , which did not break relations with Egypt after the signing of the  |Egyptian-Israeli_Peace_Treaty|Egyptian-Israeli_Peace_Treaty|  in 1979. During the  |Persian_Gulf|Persian_Gulf|  crisis, Oman assisted the  |United_Nations|United_Nations|  coalition effort. Oman has developed close ties to its neighbors; it joined the six-member  |Gulf_Cooperation_Council|Gulf_Cooperation_Council|  when it was established in 1980.     Oman has traditionally supported  |Middle_East|Middle_East|  peace initiatives, as it did those in 1983. In April 1994, Oman hosted the plenary meeting of the Water Working Group of the peace process, the first Persian Gulf state to do so.        During the  |Cold_War|Cold_War|  period, Oman avoided relations with communist countries because of the communist support for the insurgency in  |Dhofar|Dhofar| . In recent years, Oman has undertaken diplomatic initiatives in the  |Central_Asia|Central_Asia| n republics, particularly in  |Kazakhstan|Kazakhstan| , where it is involved in a joint oil pipeline project. In addition, Oman maintains good relations with  |Iran|Iran| , its north-eastern neighbor across the  |Gulf_of_Oman|Gulf_Of_Oman| , and the two countries regularly exchange delegations. Oman is an active member in international and regional organizations, notably the Arab League and the Gulf Cooperation Council. Its foreign policy is overseen by  |its_Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs|Ministry_Of_Foreign_Affairs| .          The northern boundary with the  |United_Arab_Emirates|United_Arab_Emirates|  has not been bilaterally defined; the northern section in the  |Musandam_Peninsula|Musandam_Peninsula|  is an administrative boundary.
