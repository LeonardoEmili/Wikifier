   The politics of Mayotte  takes place in a framework of a  |French_overseas|Overseas_France| |_region|Overseas_Region|  and  |department|Overseas_Department| , until 2011 an  |overseas_collectivity|Overseas_Collectivity| . Local politics takes place in a  |parliamentary|Parliamentary_System| |_representative_democratic|Representative_Democracy|  setting whereby the President of the General Council is the  |head_of_government|Head_Of_Government| , of a  |multi_party_system|Multi_Party_System| .  |Executive_power|Executive_Power|  is exercised by the  |government|Government_Of_France| .   The status of Mayotte changed in 2001 towards one very close to the status of the  département s of mainland France, with the particular designation of  collectivité départementale , although the island is still claimed by the  |Comoros|Comoros| . This change was approved by 73% at a referendum on Mayotte. After the constitutional reform of 2003 it became a  collectivité doutre-mer  while keeping the title collectivité départementale de Mayotte. Mayotte became an  |overseas_department|Overseas_Department|  of France  on 31 March 2011 following the result of the March 2009  |Mahoran_status_referendum|2009_Mahoran_Status_Referendum| , which was overwhelmingly approved by around 95% of voters.       The head of state is the  |President_of_France|President_Of_France|  as represented by prefect  |Frédéric_Veau|Frédéric_Veau| . The head of government is President of the General Council  |Soibahadine_Ibrahim_Ramadani|Soibahadine_Ibrahim_Ramadani| .       The  |General_Council|General_Council_Of_Mayotte|  has 19 members, elected for a three-year term in single seat constituencies.   The island is  |represented_by_one_deputy|Mayottes_1St_Constituency|  in   the  |National_Assembly|National_Assembly|  of France.
