              Shetland , also called the Shetland Islands and formerly Zetland, is a  |subarctic|Subarctic| |archipelago|Archipelago|  in the  |Northern_Isles|Northern_Isles|  of  |Scotland|Scotland| , situated in the  |Northern_Atlantic|Northern_Atlantic| , between  |Great_Britain|Great_Britain| , the  |Faroe_Islands|Faroe_Islands|  and  |Norway|Norway| .     The islands lie some  to the northeast of  |Orkney|Orkney| ,  from the Scottish mainland and  west of Norway. They form part of the division between the  |Atlantic_Ocean|Atlantic_Ocean|  to the west and the  |North_Sea|North_Sea|  to the east. The total area is , Shetland Islands Council p. 4 and the population totalled 23,210 in 2011.  The islands comprise the  |Shetland_constituency|Shetland|  of the  |Scottish_Parliament|Scottish_Parliament| . The local authority,  |Shetland_Islands_Council|Shetland_Islands_Council| , is one of the 32  |council_areas|Council_Areas|  of Scotland. The islands administrative centre and only  |burgh|Burgh|  is  |Lerwick|Lerwick| , which has been the capital of Shetland since taking over from  |Scalloway|Scalloway|  in 1708.     The largest island, known as  |Mainland|Mainland,_Shetland|  , has an area of , making it the third-largest  |Scottish_island|List_Of_Islands_Of_Scotland|  Haswell-Smith p. 406 and the  |fifth-largest|List_Of_The_British_Isles_By_Area|  of the  |British_Isles|British_Isles| . There are an additional 15 inhabited islands. The archipelago has an  |oceanic_climate|Oceanic_Climate| , a complex geology, a rugged coastline and many low, rolling hills.     Humans have lived in Shetland since the  |Mesolithic|Mesolithic|  period. The  |early_historic_period|Early_Middle_Ages|  was dominated by  |Scandinavian|Norsemen|  influences, especially from  |Norway|Norway| . The islands became part of Scotland in the 15th century. When Scotland became part of the  |Kingdom_of_Great_Britain|Kingdom_Of_Great_Britain|  in 1707, trade with northern Europe decreased. Fishing continues to be an important aspect of the economy up to the present day. The discovery of  |North_Sea_oil|North_Sea_Oil|  in the 1970s significantly boosted Shetlands economy, employment and public sector revenues.     The local way of life reflects the Scottish and Norse heritage of the isles, including the  |Up_Helly_Aa|Up_Helly_Aa|  fire festival and a strong musical tradition, especially the traditional  |fiddle|Fiddle|  style. The islands have produced a variety of writers of prose and poetry, often in the distinct  |Shetland_dialect|Shetland_Dialect|  of the  |Scots_language|Scots_Language| . There are numerous areas set aside to protect the local  |fauna|Fauna|  and  |flora|Flora| , including a number of important sea bird nesting sites. The  |Shetland_pony|Shetland_Pony|  and  |Shetland_Sheepdog|Shetland_Sheepdog|  are two well-known  |Shetland_animal_breeds|Shetland_Animal_Breeds| . Other local breeds include the  |Shetland_sheep|Shetland_Sheep| ,  |cow|Shetland_Cattle| ,  |goose|Shetland_Goose| , and  |duck|Shetland_Duck| . The Shetland pig, or  |grice|Grice| , has been extinct since about 1930.     The islands motto, which appears on the Councils  |coat_of_arms|Coat_Of_Arms| , is  . The geographical isolation and recent glacial history of Shetland have resulted in a depleted mammalian fauna and the  |brown_rat|Brown_Rat|  and  |house_mouse|House_Mouse|  are two of only three species of rodent present in the islands. It is possible that Apodemus was introduced from Orkney where a population has existed since at the least the  |Bronze_Age|Bronze_Age| .
