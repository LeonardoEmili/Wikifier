       Nicaragua  is a  |presidential|Presidential_System| |republic|Republic| , in which the  |President_of_Nicaragua|President_Of_Nicaragua|  is both  |head_of_state|Head_Of_State|  and  |head_of_government|Head_Of_Government| , and there is a  |multi-party_system|Multi-Party_System| .  |Executive_power|Executive_Power|  is exercised by the government.      |Legislative_power|Legislative_Power|  is vested in both the  |government|Government|  and the  |National_Assembly|National_Assembly_Of_Nicaragua| . The  |judiciary|Judiciary|  is independent of the executive and the legislature.          In 1995, the executive and legislative branches negotiated a reform of the 1987  |Sandinista|Sandinista_National_Liberation_Front|  constitution which gave extensive new powers and independence to the  |National_Assembly|National_Assembly_Of_Nicaragua| , including permitting the Assembly to override a presidential  |veto|Veto|  with a simple majority vote and eliminating the  |presidents|President_Of_Nicaragua|  ability to pocket veto a bill. Members of the unicameral National Assembly are elected to concurrent five-year terms.     In January 2014, the National Assembly approved changes to the constitution, removing presidential term limits.
