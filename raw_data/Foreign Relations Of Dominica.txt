      Like its  |Eastern_Caribbean|Eastern_Caribbean|  neighbours, the main priority of  |Dominicas|Dominica|  foreign relations is economic development. The country maintains missions in Washington, New York, London, and Brussels and is represented jointly with other  |Organisation_of_Eastern_Caribbean_States|Organisation_Of_Eastern_Caribbean_States|  members in Canada. Dominica is also a member of the  |Caribbean_Development_Bank|Caribbean_Development_Bank|  , and the  |Commonwealth_of_Nations|Commonwealth_Of_Nations| . It became a member of the  |United_Nations|United_Nations|  and the  |International_Monetary_Fund|International_Monetary_Fund|  in 1978 and of the  |World_Bank|World_Bank|  and  |Organization_of_American_States|Organization_Of_American_States|  in 1979.     As a member of  |CARICOM|Caricom| , in July 1994 Dominica strongly backed efforts by the United States to implement  |United_Nations_Security_Council_Resolution_940|United_Nations_Security_Council_Resolution_940| , designed to facilitate the departure of  |Haitis|Haiti|  de facto authorities from power. The country agreed to contribute personnel to the multinational force, which restored the democratically elected government of Haiti in October 1994.     In May 1997, Prime Minister  |James|Edison_James|  joined 14 other Caribbean leaders, and President  |Clinton|Bill_Clinton| , during the first-ever U.S.-regional summit in  |Bridgetown,_Barbados|Bridgetown,_Barbados| . The summit strengthened the basis for regional cooperation on justice and counternarcotics issues, finance and development, and trade. Dominica previously maintained official relations with the  |Republic_of_China|Republic_Of_China|  instead of the  |Peoples_Republic_of_China|Peoples_Republic_Of_China| , but on 23 March 2004, a joint communique was signed in Beijing, paving the way for diplomatic recognition of the Peoples Republic. Beijing responded to Dominicas severing relations with the Republic of China by giving them a $12 million aid package, which includes $6 million in  |budget_support|Budget_Support|  for the year 2004 and $1 million annually for six years.     Dominica is also a member of the  |International_Criminal_Court|International_Criminal_Court|  with a Bilateral Immunity Agreement of protection for the  |US|United_States| -military .         Dominica claims  |Venezuela|Venezuela| n controlled  |Isla_Aves|Isla_Aves|  located roughly 90  km. west of Dominica.                   The Commonwealth of Dominica has been a member of the  |Commonwealth_of_Nations|Commonwealth_Of_Nations|  since 1978, when it became an independent  |Commonwealth_republic|Commonwealth_Republic|  from the United Kingdom.     Dominicas highest court of appeal is the  |Caribbean_Court_of_Justice|Caribbean_Court_Of_Justice| , in effect from 6 March 2015. Previously, the nations ultimate court of appeal was the |Judicial_Committee_of_the_Privy_Council|Judicial_Committee_Of_The_Privy_Council|  in London.          The Commonwealth of Dominica has been a member of  |Organisation_internationale_de_la_Francophonie|Organisation_Internationale_De_La_Francophonie|  since 1979.     As a French and then  |British_colony|British_Empire| ;  |Antillean_Creole|Antillean_Creole| , a  |French-based_creole|French-Based_Creole_Languages|  language, is spoken by 90% of the population.
