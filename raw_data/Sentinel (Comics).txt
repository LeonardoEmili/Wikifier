           The Sentinels are a  |fiction|Fiction| al variety of mutant-hunting  |robots|Robot|  appearing in  |American_comic_books|American_Comic_Book|  published by  |Marvel_Comics|Marvel_Comics| . They are typically depicted as antagonists to the  |X_Men|X_Men| .     The Sentinels played a large role in the 1990s  |X_Men_animated_series|X_Men|  and have been featured in several X-Men  |video_games|Video_Game| . The Sentinels are featured prominently in the 2014 film  X-Men: Days of Future Past  while  |simulated_versions|Danger_Room|  made brief appearances in the 2006 film  X-Men: The Last Stand  and the 2016 film  X-Men: Apocalypse . In 2009, The Sentinels were ranked as  |IGNs|Ign|  38th Greatest Comic Book Villain of All Time.           Created by  |Stan_Lee|Stan_Lee|  and  |Jack_Kirby|Jack_Kirby| , they first appeared in  The X-Men  14 .     Sentinels are programmed to locate  |mutants|Mutant|  and capture or kill them. Though several types of Sentinels have been introduced, the typical Sentinel is three stories tall, is capable of  |flight|Flight| , projects energy blasts, and can detect mutants.            Sentinels are designed to hunt  |mutants|Mutant| . While many are capable of  |tactical_thought|Tactic| , only a handful are  |self_aware|Self_Awareness| .     Sentinels are technologically advanced, and have exhibited a wide variety of abilities. They are armed , capable of flight, and can detect mutants at long range. They possess vast physical strength, and their bodies are highly resistant to damage. Some are able to alter their physical forms or re-assemble and reactivate themselves after they have been destroyed.     Some Sentinel variants have the ability to learn from their experiences, developing their defenses during an engagement. Several groups of Sentinels have been created and/or led by a single massive Sentinel called  |Master_Mold|Master_Mold| . Some Sentinels are also equipped with an inconspicuous logic loop in case they should go rogue to convince them that they are mutants as demonstrated in the Tri-Sentinel. The Sentinels are featured in  X-Men: Days of Future Past  as a secondary threat to mutants.  In 1973, Bolivar Trask pitches the Sentinel concept to the US Congress but declines so Trask attempts pitching to foreign powers.  |Mystique|Mystique|  hunts down and kills Trask in revenge for the murder of mutants that were experimented on during the Sentinels conception to accelerate research. Inspired by the fear of Trasks death, the US government approves the Sentinel program to hunt and kill mutants, and experimentation on Mystique captured soon after Trasks murder leads to the creation of Sentinels that can adapt to and use mutant powers. The Sentinels eventually expand their targets beyond mutants to baseline humans based on the logic to have the potential to produce mutant descendants, culminating in a  |dystopian_future|Days_Of_Future_Past|  where most of humanity and mutantkind have been wiped out. With  |Kitty_Prydes|Kitty_Pryde|  new ability to project the minds of others into their past selves, the X-Men project Wolverine - the only person capable of surviving the time-travels psychological strain - back into his own past version in 1973 to rally the younger versions of Charles Xavier and Erik Lensherr to stop Trasks assassination, setting into motion the films events. The storyline concludes with Mystique defeating Magneto and then deciding not to kill Trask on Xaviers and  |Beasts|Beast|  encouragement, resulting in the government witnessing clear evidence that not all mutants are a threat to normal humans and deciding to abandon the Sentinel program. The original prototypes from 1973 were similar in appearance to their comic counterparts being three times the size of a human, possessed gatling guns on one of their arms capable of firing 3000 rounds per minute, sensors that allow to track X-Gene carriers and could fly using the vent-like systems on their chest, and were built out of a space-age polymer to be immune to Magnetos powers . In contrast, the future iterations were smaller and sleeker, with a body built out of adaptive mechanical scales, and has Mystiques adaptive powers which can reshape their hands into stabbing blades. The Sentinels were designed by  |Legacy_Effects|Legacy_Effects|  with  |Digital_Domain|Digital_Domain|  building digital models based on their full-scale practical model while the future variant were all computer graphics made by  |Moving_Picture_Company|Moving_Picture_Company| .
