The population of  |Guinea_Bissau|Guinea_Bissau|  is ethnically diverse with distinct languages, customs, and social structures. Most Guineans, 99%, are Blacks   mostly  |Fula|Fula_Language|  and  |Mandinka|Mandinka_Language| -speakers concentrated in the north and northeast, the  |Balanta|Balanta_People|  and Papel, living in the southern coastal regions, and the  |Manjaco|Manjak_People|  and Mancanha, occupying the central and northern coastal areas. Due to the exodus of most Portuguese settlers after independence, less than 1% of Guinea-Bissauans are pure Portuguese. The country also has a  |Chinese|Overseas_Chinese|  minority, including  |Macanese_people|Macanese_People|  of mixed Portuguese and Chinese blood from  |Macau|Macau| .     Most people are farmers. 38%-45% are  |Muslims|Muslim|  - this makes Guinea-Bissau the only Portuguese-speaking nation with a sizable Muslim population. Most Muslims are  |Sunnis|Sunni_Islam| . The rest of the population are  |pagans|Animism| , principally the  |Balanta|Balanta_People| , and  |Christians|Christianity| , mostly  |Roman_Catholics|Roman_Catholicism| .
