      Garnet Edward  Ace  Bailey was a  |Canadian|Canadians|  professional  |ice_hockey|Ice_Hockey|  player and scout who was a member of  |Stanley_Cup|Stanley_Cup|  and  |Memorial_Cup|Memorial_Cup|  winning teams. He died at the age of 53 while aboard  |United_Airlines_Flight_175|United_Airlines_Flight_175| , which crashed into the  |South_Tower|2_World_Trade_Centeroriginal_Building|  of the  |World_Trade_Center|World_Trade_Center|  in  |New_York_City|New_York_City|  during the  |September_11_attacks|September_11_Attacks| .       Garnet Edward Ace Bailey was born June 13, 1948 in  |Lloydminster|Lloydminster| ,  |Saskatchewan|Saskatchewan| . He was not related to  |Irvine_Ace_Bailey|Ace_Bailey| , who played for the  |Toronto_Maple_Leafs|Toronto_Maple_Leafs|  from 1926 to 1933.          Bailey played junior hockey with the  |Edmonton_Oil_Kings|Edmonton_Oil_Kings|  from 1964 to 1967, during which time the Oil Kings won the  |Memorial_Cup|Memorial_Cup|  in 1966. He joined the  |Boston_Bruins|Boston_Bruins|  in 1968 and was a member of their  |Stanley_Cup|Stanley_Cup|  championship teams in  |1970|1969–70_Nhl_Season|  and  |1972|1971–72_Nhl_Season| . He later played for the  |Detroit_Red_Wings|Detroit_Red_Wings| ,  |St._Louis_Blues|St._Louis_Blues|  and the  |Washington_Capitals|Washington_Capitals| . In 1978-79, Bailey returned to Edmonton to play with the  |Edmonton_Oilers|Edmonton_Oilers|  of the  |World_Hockey_Association|World_Hockey_Association| , where he took rookie  |Wayne_Gretzky|Wayne_Gretzky|  under his wing. He was head coach of the  |Wichita_Wind|Wichita_Wind| , the Oilers  |Central_Hockey_League|Central_Hockey_League|  affiliate, in the 1980–81 season. Bailey then worked as a scout with the Oilers from 1981 to 1994, during which time the team won five Stanley Cups ; his name was engraved on three of them .     In an  |NHL|National_Hockey_League|  career spanning 10 seasons and 568 games, Bailey scored 107 goals and 171 assists with 633 penalty minutes. His most productive season offensively was  |1975|1974–75_Nhl_Season| , when he scored 19 goals and 58 points for the Blues and the Capitals. In his sole  |WHA|World_Hockey_Association|  season, he scored 5 goals and 4 assists with 22 penalty minutes in 38 games.     At the time of his death, he was the  |Los_Angeles_Kings|Los_Angeles_Kings|  director of pro scouting. .  |National_Hockey_League|National_Hockey_League| . October 15, 2012.          Bailey died when the plane in which he was traveling,  |United_Airlines_Flight_175|United_Airlines_Flight_175| , was hijacked and deliberately crashed into the South Tower of the  |World_Trade_Center|World_Trade_Center|  in  |New_York_City|New_York_City|  during the  |September_11_attacks|September_11_Attacks| . Bailey and amateur scout  |Mark_Bavis|Mark_Bavis|  were traveling from Boston to Los Angeles when the flight was hijacked. They had been in  |Manchester|Manchester,_New_Hampshire|  visiting the Los Angeles Kings AHL affiliate, the  |Monarchs|Manchester_Monarchs| .
