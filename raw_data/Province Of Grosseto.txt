   The Province of Grosseto  is a  |province|Provinces_Of_Italy|  in the  |Tuscany|Tuscany|  region of  |Italy|Italy| . Its capital is the city of  |Grosseto|Grosseto| . As of 2013 the province had a total population of 225,098 people.           The Province of Grosseto completely occupies the southern end of Tuscany, and with a territorial area of , it is the most extensive in the region and one of the least dense in population in Italy. The province is bordered to the northwest by the  |Province_of_Livorno|Province_Of_Livorno| , to the north by the  |Province_of_Pisa|Province_Of_Pisa| , to the northeast by the  |Province_of_Siena|Province_Of_Siena| , and to the southeast by the  |Province_of_Viterbo|Province_Of_Viterbo|  in  |Lazio|Lazio| . To the south is the  |Tyrrhenian_Sea|Tyrrhenian_Sea| , which includes the southern islands of the Tuscan archipelago, including  |Isola_del_Giglio|Isola_Del_Giglio|   and the smaller  |Giannutri|Giannutri|  islands and  |Formiche_di_Grosseto|Formiche_Di_Grosseto|  and Formica di Burano. The  |Arcipelago_Toscano_National_Park|Arcipelago_Toscano_National_Park|  spans both the provinces of Grosseto and Livorno, and includes the seven main islands of the Tuscan Archipelago:  |Elba|Elba| , Isola del Giglio,  |Capraia|Capraia| ,  |Montecristo|Montecristo| ,  |Pianosa|Pianosa| , Giannutri,  |Gorgona|Gorgona,_Italy| , and some of the minor islands and rock outcrops.  The highest point in the park is  |Mount_Capanne|Mount_Capanne| , at  in elevation, on the island of Elba.      The  |Colline_Metallifere|Colline_Metallifere|  line the border in the south with Lazio, and contain the Natural Park of  |Maremma|Maremma| , that protects also some of the remainings of the large swamps that once covered the area. Other protected areas are the Diaccia Botrona marshland.  The principal rivers are the  |Ombrone|Ombrone| ,   |Fiora|Fiora_River| ,  |Albegna|Albegna| ,  |Pecora|Pecora| ,  |Bruna|Bruna| ,  |Merse|Merse| ,  |Lente|Lente| ,  |Farma|Farma|  and  |Chiarone|Chiarone| . The coastline between the  |Gulf_of_Follonica|Gulf_Of_Follonica|  and the mouth of the Chiarone is dominated by blue waters and pine forests, and is home to resorts such as  |Marina_di_Grosseto|Marina_Di_Grosseto| ,  |Principina_a_Mare|Principina_A_Mare| ,  |Castiglione_della_Pescaia|Castiglione_Della_Pescaia| ,  |Punta_Ala|Punta_Ala| ,  |Puntone_di_Scarlino|Puntone_Di_Scarlino|  and  |Talamone|Talamone| .  Lakes include  |Lago_dellAccesa|Lago_Dellaccesa| ,  |Lago_di_Burano|Lago_Di_Burano| ,  |Lago_di_San_Floriano|Lago_Di_San_Floriano|  and  |Lago_Acquato|Lago_Acquato| . Also of note is the volcanic cone of  |Mount_Amiata|Mount_Amiata| , Bandite di Scarlino , Promontorio di Punta Ala e delle Rocchette , Monti dellUccellina , Monte Argentario , and Promontorio di Ansedonia .
