   Mario Berlinguer  was an Italian lawyer and politician. He descended from a noble  |Sardinia|Sardinia| n family of  |Catalan|Catalans|  origins.     Born in  |Sassari|Sassari| , in his youth he was a follower of the Meridionalist activist  |Gaetano_Salvemini|Gaetano_Salvemini| . After his graduation in law he collaborated with the newspaper  La Nuova Sardegna  and other Italian newspapers.     He was elected to the  |Italian_Chamber_of_Deputies|Italian_Chamber_Of_Deputies|  in 1924. The following year he founded the clandestine newspaper Sardegna libera which attracted him the hostility of the  |Fascist|Fascist|  regime. After the  |armistice_with_Italy|Armistice_With_Italy|  , he joined the  |Action_Party|Action_Party| . For the latter Berlinguer was a member of the second government formed by  |Pietro_Badoglio|Pietro_Badoglio|  in southern Italy.     In 1945 he was named to the  |Consulta_Nazionale|Consulta_Nazionale|  and collaborated to the elaboration of the special regional status for Sardinia. He was a deputy for PSI from 1948 to 1953.     He was the father of  |Giovanni|Giovanni_Berlinguer|  and  |Enrico_Berlinguer|Enrico_Berlinguer| , outstanding members of the  |Italian_Communist_Party|Italian_Communist_Party| .
