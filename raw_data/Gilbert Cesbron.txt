Gilbert Cesbron was a French  |novelist|Novelist| .     Born in Paris, Cesbron attended what is now known as  |Lycée_Condorcet|Lycée_Condorcet| . In 1944, he published his first novel, Les innocents de Paris , in Switzerland. He first came into wide public acclaim with the release of Notre prison est un royaume  in 1948, and Il est minuit, docteur Schweitzer  in 1950.     In his works, Cesbron tended to illustrate and describe relevant social topics such as:  |juvenile_delinquency|Juvenile_Delinquency|  in Chiens perdus sans collier , violence in Entre chiens et loups ,  |euthanasia|Euthanasia|  in Il est plus tard que tu ne penses , and  |working_priests|Worker_Priest|  in Les Saints vont en enfer .     In 1955, Cesbrons book Chiens perdus sans collier, the story of an orphan boy and a benevolent judge, was made  starring  |Jean_Gabin|Jean_Gabin|  and  |Robert_Dalban|Robert_Dalban| .
