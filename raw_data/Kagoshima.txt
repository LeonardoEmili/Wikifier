It has been nicknamed the  |Naples|Naples|  of the  |Eastern_world|Eastern_World|  for its bay location , hot climate, and emblematic  |stratovolcano|Stratovolcano| ,  |Sakurajima|Sakurajima| . The city was officially founded on April 1, 1889.               Kagoshima Prefecture  was the center of the territory of the  |Shimazu_clan|Shimazu_Clan|  for many centuries. It was a busy political and commercial port city throughout the medieval period and into the  |Edo_period|Edo_Period|  when it formally became the capital of the Shimazus  |fief|Han| , the Satsuma Domain. The official emblem is a modification of the Shimazus  |kamon|Mon|  designed to resemble the character 市 . Satsuma remained one of the most powerful and wealthiest domains in the country throughout the period, and though international trade was  |banned|Sakoku|  for much of this period, the city remained quite active and prosperous. It served not only as the political center for Satsuma, but also for the semi-independent vassal kingdom of  |Ryūkyū|Ryūkyū_Kingdom| ;  |Ryūkyūan|Ryukyuan_People|  traders and emissaries frequented the city, and a special  |Ryukyuan_embassy_building|Ryūkyū-Kan|  was established to help administer relations between the two  |polities|Polity|  and to house visitors and emissaries. Kagoshima was also a significant center of  |Christian|Kirishitan|  activity in Japan prior to the imposition of bans against that religion in the late 16th and early 17th centuries.      |Kagoshima_was_bombarded|Bombardment_Of_Kagoshima|  by the British  |Royal_Navy|Royal_Navy|  in 1863 to punish the  daimyō  of  |Satsuma|Satsuma_Province|  for the murder of  |Charles_Lennox_Richardson|Charles_Lennox_Richardson|  on the  |Tōkaidō|Tōkaidō|  highway the previous year and its refusal to pay an indemnity in compensation.     Kagoshima was the birthplace and scene of the  |last_stand|Last_Stand|  of  |Saigō_Takamori|Saigō_Takamori| , a legendary figure in  |Meiji_Era|Meiji_Era|  Japan in 1877 at the end of the  |Satsuma_Rebellion|Satsuma_Rebellion| .     Japans industrial revolution is said to have started here, stimulated by the young students train station. Seventeen young men of Satsuma broke the  |Tokugawa|Tokugawa_Shogunate|  ban on foreign travel, traveling first to England and then the United States before returning to share the benefits of the best of Western science and technology.  A statue was erected outside the train station as a tribute to them.     Kagoshima was also the birthplace of  |Tōgō_Heihachirō|Tōgō_Heihachirō| . After naval studies in  |England|England|  between 1871 and 1878, Togos role as Chief Admiral of the Grand Fleet of the  |Imperial_Japanese_Navy|Imperial_Japanese_Navy|  in the  |Russo-Japanese_War|Russo-Japanese_War|  made him a legend in Japanese military history, and earned him the nickname  |Nelson|Horatio_Nelson,_1St_Viscount_Nelson|  of the Orient in Britain. He led the Grand Fleet to two startling victories in 1904 and 1905, completely destroying Russia as a naval power in the East, and thereby contributing to the failed  |revolution_in_Russia|Russian_Revolution_Of_1905|  in 1905.     The Japanese diplomat  |Sadomitsu_Sakoguchi|Sadomitsu_Sakoguchi|  revolutionized Kagoshimas environmental economic plan with his dissertation on water pollution and orange harvesting. On the night of June 17, 1945 the 314th bombardment wing of the Army Air Corps dropped 809.6 tons of incendiary and cluster bombs destroying  of Kagoshima . Kagoshima was targeted because of its largely expanded naval port as well as its position as a railway terminus. A single B-29 was lost to unknown circumstances. Area bombing was chosen over precision bombing because of the cloudy weather over Japan during the middle of June. The planes were forced to navigate and bomb entirely by radar. Headquarters, XXI Bomber Command APO 234, Tactical Mission Report Mission No. 206-209. June 18, 1945. Kagoshima City is approximately 40 minutes from  |Kagoshima_Airport|Kagoshima_Airport| , and features shopping districts and malls located wide across the city. Transportation options in the city include the  Shinkansen  , local train, city trams, buses, and ferries to-and-from Sakurajima. The large and modern Kagoshima City Aquarium, situated near a shopping district known as Dolphin Port and the Sakurajima Ferry Terminal, was established in 1997 along the docks and offers a direct view of Sakurajima. One of the best places to view the city is from the  |Amuran_Ferris_wheel|Amuran|  atop of  |Amu_Plaza_Kagoshima|Amu_Plaza_Kagoshima| , and the shopping center attached to the central  |Kagoshima-Chūō_Station|Kagoshima-Chūō_Station| . Just outside the city is the early-Edo Period  |Sengan-en|Sengan-En|  Japanese Garden. The garden was originally a villa belonging to the  |Shimazu_clan|Shimazu_Clan|  and is still maintained by descendants today. Outside the garden grounds is a Satsuma kiriko cut-glass factory where visitors are welcome to view the glass blowing and cutting processes, and the Shoko Shūseikan Museum, which was built in 1865 and registered as a National Historic Site in 1959. The former Shuseikan industrial complex and the former machine factory were submitted to the UNESCO World Heritage as part of a group list titled Modern Industrial Heritage Sites in Kyushu and Yamaguchi Prefecture.
