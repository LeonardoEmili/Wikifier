The New Oxford American Dictionary  is a single-volume  |dictionary|Dictionary|  of  |American_English|American_English|  compiled by American editors at the  |Oxford_University_Press|Oxford_University_Press| .     NOAD is based upon the  New Oxford Dictionary of English  , published in the United Kingdom in 1998, although with substantial editing, additional entries, and the inclusion of illustrations. It is based on a  |corpus_linguistics|Corpus_Linguistics|  analysis of Oxfords 200 million word database of contemporary  |American_English|American_English| .     NOAD includes a  |diacritical_respelling_scheme|Pronunciation_Respelling_For_English|  to convey pronunciations, as opposed to the  |Gimson_phonemic_IPA|Pronunciation_Respelling_For_Englishinternational_Phonetic_Alphabet|  system that is used in NODE.           Published in September 2001, the first edition was edited by Elizabeth J. Jewell and Frank Abate.       Published in May 2005, the second edition was edited by  |Erin_McKean|Erin_Mckean| . The edition added nearly 3,000 new words, senses, and phrases. It was in a large format, with 2096 pages, and was 8Â½ by 11 in size. It included a CD-ROM with the full text of the dictionary for  |Palm_OS|Palm_Os|  devices.     Since 2005  |Apple_Inc.s|Apple_Inc.| |Mac_OS_X|Mac_Os_X|  operating system has come bundled with a  |dictionary|Dictionary|  application and  |widget|Widget|  which credits as its source Oxford American Dictionaries , and contains the full text of NOAD2. The  |Amazon_Kindle|Amazon_Kindle|  reading device also uses NOAD as its built-in dictionary, along with a choice for the  Oxford Dictionary of English .     Oxford University Press published NOAD2 in electronic form in 2006 at the OxfordAmericanDictionary.com,  and in 2010, along with the Oxford Dictionary of English, as part of  |Oxford_Dictionaries_Online|Oxforddictionaries.Com| .        Published in August 2010, the third edition was edited by Angus Stevenson and Christine A. Lindberg.
