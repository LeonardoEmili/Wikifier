     The politics of Ethiopia are the  |activities_associated_with_the_governance|Politics|  of the  |Ethiopia|Ethiopia| . The government is structured as a federal parliamentary republic with both a President and Prime Minister.          The government of Ethiopia is structured in the form of a  |federal|Federal_Republic| |parliamentary|Parliamentary_System| |republic|Republic| , whereby the  |Prime_Minister|Prime_Minister_Of_Ethiopia|  is the  |head_of_government|Head_Of_Government| .  |Executive_power|Executive_Power|  is exercised by the government while  |legislative_power|Legislative_Power|  is vested in the  |Parliament|Parliament_Of_Ethiopia| . The  |Judiciary|Judiciary|  is more or less independent of the executive and the legislature. There are 9 ethnically based  |administrative_regions|Regions_Of_Ethiopia|  and two self-governing administrations; the capital city  |Addis_Ababa|Addis_Ababa|  and  |Dire_Dawa|Dire_Dawa| .     The  |president_of_Ethiopia|President_Of_Ethiopia|  is elected by the  |House_of_Peoples_Representatives|House_Of_Peoples_Representatives|  for a six-year term. The prime minister is chosen by the parliament. The prime minister is designated by the party in power following legislative elections. The Council of Ministers, according to the  |1995_constitution|1995_Constitution_Of_Ethiopia| , is comprised by the Prime Minister, the Deputy Prime Ministers, various Ministers and other members as determined and approved by the House of Peoples Representatives. At the current time, this includes the 20 members of  |Council_of_Ministers|Council_Of_Ministers_Of_Ethiopia| .     The  |Federal_Parliamentary_Assembly|Federal_Parliamentary_Assembly|  has two  |chambers|Bicameralism| |Council_of_Peoples_Representatives|House_Of_Peoples_Representatives|   with 547 members, elected for five-year terms in single-seat  |constituencies|Constituency| ; and the  |Council_of_the_Federation|House_Of_Federation|   with 110 members, one for each  |nationality|Demographics_Of_Ethiopia| , and one additional representative for each one million of its population, designated by the regional councils, which may elect them themselves or through popular elections.     The president and vice president of the  |Federal_Supreme_Court|Federal_Supreme_Court_Of_Ethiopia|  are recommended by the prime minister and appointed by the House of Peoples Representatives; for other federal judges, the prime minister submits candidates selected by the Federal Judicial Administrative Council to the Peoples Representatives for appointment.
