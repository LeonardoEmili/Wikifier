     Jean-Jacques Ampère was a French  |philologist|Philologist|  and man of letters.     Born in  |Lyon|Lyon| , he was the only son of the physicist  |André-Marie_Ampère|André-Marie_Ampère|  . Jean-Jacques mother died while he was an infant. On his tomb at the cemetery of  |Montmartre|Montmartre| , Paris, he is named Jean-Jacques Antoine Ampère. His fathers father was also named Jean-Jacques Ampère .     He studied the  |folk_songs|Folk_Song|  and popular poetry of the Scandinavian countries in an extended tour in northern Europe. Returning to France in 1830, he delivered a series of lectures on Scandinavian and early German poetry at the Athenaeum in  |Marseille|Marseille| . The first of these was printed as De lHistoire de la poésie , and was practically the first introduction of the French public to the Scandinavian and German epics.     Moving to Paris, he taught at the  |Sorbonne|University_Of_Paris| , and became professor of the history of French literature at the Collège de France. A journey in northern Africa was followed by a tour in  |Greece|Greece|  and Italy, in company with  |Prosper_Mérimée|Prosper_Mérimée| ,  |Jean_de_Witte|Jean_De_Witte|  and  |Charles_Lenormant|Charles_Lenormant| . This bore fruit in his Voyage dantesque , which did much to popularize the study of  |Dante|Dante_Alighieri|  in France.     In 1848 he became a member of the  |Académie_française|Académie_Française| , and in 1851 he visited America. From this time he was occupied with his chief work, LHistoire romaine à Rome , until his death at  |Pau|Pau,_Pyrénées-Atlantiques| .     The Correspondence et souvenirs of A-M and J-J Ampère was published in 1875. Notices of J-J Ampère are to be found in  |Sainte-Beuves|Charles_Augustin_Sainte-Beuve|  Portraits littéraires, vol. iv., and Nouveaux Lundis, vol. xiii.; in P Mérimées Portraits historiques et littéraires ; and in  |Alexis_de_Tocquevilles|Alexis_De_Tocqueville|  Recollections .
