     The demographic profile of Cameroon is complex for a country of its population.  |Cameroon|Cameroon|  comprises an estimated 250 distinct  |ethnic_groups|Ethnic_Groups| , which may be formed into five large regional-cultural divisions:   western highlanders , including the  |Bamileke|Bamileke| ,  |Bamum|Bamum_People|  , and many smaller  |Tikar|Tikar|  groups in the  |Northwest|Northwest_Province,_Cameroon|  ;   coastal tropical forest peoples, including the  |Bassa|Bassa_People| ,  |Duala|Duala_People|  , and many smaller groups in the  |Southwest|Southwest_Province,_Cameroon|  ;   southern tropical forest peoples, including the  |Beti-Pahuin|Beti-Pahuin| , Bulu , Fang ,  |Maka|Maka_People| ,  |Njem|Njem| , and  |Baka|Baka| |pygmies|Pygmy|  ;   predominantly Islam ic peoples of the northern semi-arid regions  and central highlands, including the  |Fulani|Fula_People|   ; and the Kirdi , non-Islamic or recently Islamic peoples of the northern desert and central highlands .     The Cameroon government held two national censuses during the countrys first 44 years as an independent country, in 1976 and again in 1987. Results from the second head count were never published. A third census, expected to take years to produce results, began on November 11, 2005, with a three-week interviewing phase. It is one of a series of projects and reforms required by the  |International_Monetary_Fund|International_Monetary_Fund|  as prerequisites for foreign  |debt_relief|Debt_Relief| . The first results were published in 2010.         According to  the total population was  in , compared to only 4 466 000 in 1950.
