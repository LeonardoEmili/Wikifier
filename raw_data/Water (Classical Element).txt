   Water is one of the  |elements|Classical_Element|  in  |ancient_Greek_philosophy|Pre-Socratics| , in the  |Asian_Indian|India|  system  Panchamahabhuta , and in the  |Chinese|China|  cosmological and  |physiological|Traditional_Chinese_Medicine|  system  Wu Xing . In contemporary  |esoteric_traditions|Esotericism| , it is commonly associated with the qualities of  |emotion|Emotion|  and  |intuition|Intuition| .       Water was one of many archai proposed by the Pre-socratics, most of whom tried to reduce all things to a single substance. However,  |Empedocles|Empedocles|  of Acragas selected four archai for his four roots:  |air|Air| ,  |fire|Fire| , water and  |earth|Earth| . Empedocles roots became the four classical elements of Greek philosophy.  |Plato|Plato|  took over the four elements of Empedocles. In the  |Timaeus|Timaeus| , his major cosmological dialogue, the  |Platonic_solid|Platonic_Solid|  associated with water is the  |icosahedron|Icosahedron|  which is formed from twenty equilateral triangles. This makes water the element with the greatest number of sides, which Plato regarded as appropriate because water flows out of ones hand when picked up, as if it is made of tiny little balls. Plato, Timaeus, chap. 22–23;  |Gregory_Vlastos|Gregory_Vlastos| , Plato’s Universe, pp. 66–82.     Plato’s student  |Aristotle|Aristotle|  developed a different explanation for the elements based on pairs of qualities. The four elements were arranged concentrically around the center of the  |Universe|Universe|  to form the  |sublunary_sphere|Sublunary_Sphere| . According to Aristotle, water is both cold and wet and occupies a place between air and earth among the elemental spheres.  |G._E._R._Lloyd|G._E._R._Lloyd| , Aristotle, chapters 7–8.          In  |ancient_Greek_medicine|Medicine_In_Ancient_Greece| , each of the  |four_humours|Four_Humours|  became associated with an element.  |Phlegm|Phlegm|  was the humor identified with water, since both were cold and wet. Other things associated with water and phlegm in ancient and  |medieval_medicine|Medieval_Medicine|  included the season of Winter, since it increased the qualities of cold and moisture; the phlegmatic temperament, the  |feminine|Femininity| , the  |brain|Human_Brain|  and the  |west|West| ern point of the compass.     In  |alchemy|Alchemy| , the  |chemical_element|Chemical_Element|  of  |mercury|Mercury|  was often associated with water and its  |alchemical_symbol|Alchemical_Symbol|  was a downward-pointing triangle.
