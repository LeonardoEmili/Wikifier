       Gabon  is a country in  |Central_Africa|Central_Africa| , lying along the  |Atlantic_Ocean|Atlantic_Ocean| , just south of the  |Bight_of_Biafra|Bight_Of_Biafra| .       Gabon has a total of 3,261  km of international boundaries. It borders  |Equatorial_Guinea|Equatorial_Guinea|  and  |Cameroon|Cameroon|  to the north and the  |Republic_of_the_Congo|Republic_Of_The_Congo|  to the east and south. Gabon lies on the  |equator|Equator| .   ; Maritime claims:   : Territorial sea:    : Contiguous zone:    : Exclusive economic zone:        Gabon has the moist, hot climate typical of tropical regions. The hottest month is January, with an average high at  |Libreville|Libreville|  of 31  °C and an average low of 23  °C. Average July temperatures in the capital range between 20 and 28  °C. From June to September there is virtually no rain but high humidity; there is occasional rain in December and January. During the remaining months, rainfall is heavy. The excessive rainfall is caused by the condensation of moist air resulting from the meeting, directly off the coast, of the cold Benguela Current from the south and the warm Guinea Current from the north. At Libreville, the average annual rainfall is more than 2540  mm. Farther north on the coast, it is 3810  mm.
