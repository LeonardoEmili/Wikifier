The House of Sforza  was a ruling family of  |Renaissance_Italy|Renaissance_Italy| , based in  |Milan|Milan| . They acquired the  |Duchy_of_Milan|Duchy_Of_Milan|  following the extinction of the  |Visconti_family|Visconti_Family|  in the mid-15th century, Sforza rule ending in Milan with the death of the last member of the familys main branch in 1535.          |Francesco_I_Sforza|Francesco_I_Sforza|  ruled Milan, having acquired the title of  |Duke_of_Milan|Duke_Of_Milan|  after marrying in 1441 the natural daughter and only heir of the last  |Duke_of_Milan|Duke_Of_Milan| ,  |Filippo_Maria_Visconti|Filippo_Maria_Visconti| ,  |Bianca_Maria|Bianca_Maria_Visconti|  , making the Sforzas the heirs of the  |house_of_Visconti|House_Of_Visconti| .     The family also held the seigniory of  |Pesaro|Pesaro| , starting with Muzio Attendolos second son,  |Alessandro|Alessandro_Sforza|  . The Sforza held Pesaro until 1512, after the death of  |Costanzo_II_Sforza|Costanzo_Ii_Sforza| .     Muzios third son, Bosio , founded the branch of  |Santa_Fiora|Santa_Fiora| , who held the title of count of  |Cotignola|Cotignola| ; the Sforza ruled the small  |county_of_Santa_Fiora|County_Of_Santa_Fiora|  in southern  |Tuscany|Tuscany|  until 1624. Members of this family also held important ecclesiastical and political positions in the  |Papal_States|Papal_States| , and moved to Rome in 1674, taking the name of Sforza Cesarini.     The Sforza became allied with the  |Borgia_family|Borgia|  through the  |arranged_marriage|Arranged_Marriage|  between  |Lucrezia_Borgia|Lucrezia_Borgia|  and  |Giovanni|Giovanni_Sforza|  .    This alliance failed, as the Borgia family annulled the marriage once the Sforza family were no longer needed.     In 1499, in the course of the  |Italian_Wars|Italian_War_Of_1499–1504| , the army of  |Louis_XII_of_France|Louis_Xii_Of_France|  took Milan from  |Ludovico_Sforza|Ludovico_Sforza|  .     After Imperial  |German|Germans|  troops drove out the  |French|France| ,  |Maximilian_Sforza|Maximilian_Sforza| , son of Ludovico, became Duke of Milan until the French returned under  |Francis_I_of_France|Francis_I_Of_France|  and imprisoned him.     In 1521  |Charles_V|Charles_V,_Holy_Roman_Emperor|  drove out the French and restored the younger son of Ludovico,  |Francesco_II_Sforza|Francesco_Ii_Sforza|  to the duchy. Francesco remained the ruler of Milan until his death in 1535 and as he was childless the Duchy reverted to the Emperor, who passed it to his son  |Philip_II|Philip_Ii_Of_Spain|  in 1540, thus
