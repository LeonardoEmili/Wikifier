Within Armenia, new electric trains connect passengers from Yerevan to Armenias second largest city of  |Gyumri|Gyumri| . The new trains run four times a day and the journey takes approximately two hours.      There is also discussion to establish a rail link between Yerevan and Tehran. Armenia is pursuing funding from the Asian Development Bank to launch construction of this infrastructure project.  The completion of the project could establish a major commodities transit corridor and would serve as the shortest transportation route between  |Europe|Europe|  and the  |Persian_Gulf|Persian_Gulf| .  In June 2019, Iranian president  |Hassan_Rouhani|Hassan_Rouhani|  backed this project and stated that “we want the Persian Gulf and the  |Gulf_of_Oman|Gulf_Of_Oman|  to be connected to the Black Sea, and one of the ways to make this happen is through Iran, Armenia and Georgia.”             The capital city of Armenia,  |Yerevan|Yerevan| , is serviced by the  |Yerevan_Metro|Yerevan_Metro| . The system was launched in 1981 and like most former Soviet Metros, its stations are very deep and intricately decorated with national motifs. The metro runs on a 13.4 kilometres line and currently serves 10 active stations. Trains run every five minutes from 6:30  a.m. until 11 p.m local  |AMT|Armenia_Time|  time. As of 2017, the annual ridership of the metro is 16.2 million passengers.  Free wi-fi is available at all stations and some trains.            Land borders are open with both Georgia and Iran. Yerevan Central Bus Station, also known as Kilikia Bus Station is the main bus terminal in Yerevan with buses connecting to both internal and international destinations. There are daily bus connections between Yerevan and  |Tbilisi|Tbilisi|  and Yerevan and  |Tehran|Tehran| .  Approximately three times daily, buses depart from Yerevan Central Bus Station to  |Stepanakert|Stepanakert| , the capital of the partially recognized state of  |Artsakh|Republic_Of_Artsakh| .  There are also scheduled bus routes which connect Yerevan with  |Kiev|Kiev| ,  |Moscow|Moscow| ,  |Saint_Petersburg|Saint_Petersburg|  as well as several other cities across  |Russia|Russia| .  It is also possible to connect to  |Chișinău|Chișinău|  Moldova,  |Minsk|Minsk|  Belarus and other cities in  |Eastern_Europe|Eastern_Europe|  from Yerevan through connecting bus routes via Georgia and Ukraine.  In addition, there is a once a week bus service to  |Istanbul|Istanbul|  via Georgia.  In June 2019, a new bus route from  |Baghdad|Baghdad|  to Yerevan via Iran began.           The Armenian bus network connects all major cities and towns and many villages throughout the country. In larger cities and towns such as  |Yerevan|Yerevan| ,  |Gyumri|Gyumri| ,  |Vanadzor|Vanadzor|  and  |Armavir|Armavir,_Armenia| , bus stations are equipped with a waiting room and a ticket office, in other towns bus stations may not have shelters. Most of the routes are operated by minivans  |GAZelle|Gazelle|  with a capacity of 15 passengers, some routes are operated by soviet bus producer  |LiAZ|Liaz| . Yerevan itself has a large integrated bus network, with a newly acquired bus fleet, passengers are able to connect from one end of the city to the other.  |Wi-fi|Wi-Fi|  is available on most city buses.  Despite this, buses often have difficulty meeting the demand for capacity, mainly in Yerevan, where vehicles are typically overcrowded. There are no night services between 11pm and 6am. There is no ticket system in the country, passengers pay in cash to drivers. Passengers on the national bus network pay before boarding, passengers on the Yerevan bus network pay after the ride, while leaving the vehicle. Timetables and fares are published on .     From  |Yeritasardakan|Yeritasardakan|  metro station in downtown Yerevan, travelers can take the 201 airport shuttle which goes directly to Zvartnots International Airport, which takes approximately 20 minutes from the city center.           Since independence, Armenia has been developing its internal highway network. The North-South Road Corridor Investment Program is a major infrastructure project which aims at connecting the southern border of Armenia with its northern by means of a 556  km-long Meghri-Yerevan-Bavra highway. It is a major US$1.5 billion infrastructure project funded by the  |Asian_Development_Bank|Asian_Development_Bank| ,  |European_Investment_Bank|European_Investment_Bank|  and the  |Eurasian_Development_Bank|Eurasian_Development_Bank| . When completed, the highway will provide access to European countries via the  |Black_Sea|Black_Sea| . It could also eventually interconnect the Black Sea ports of Georgia with the major ports of Iran, thus positioning Armenia in a strategic transport corridor between Europe and Asia.   Armenia is pursuing further loans from  |China|China|  as part of the  |Belt_and_Road_Initiative|Belt_And_Road_Initiative|  to complete the North-South highway.      Armenia connects to European road networks via the  |International_E-road_network|International_E-Road_Network|  through various routes such as;  |European_route_E117|European_Route_E117| ,  |European_route_E691|European_Route_E691| ,  |European_route_E001|European_Route_E001|  and  |European_route_E60|European_Route_E60| . Armenia also connects to the  |Asian_Highway_Network|Asian_Highway_Network|  through routes  |AH81|Ah81| ,  |AH82|Ah82|  and  |AH83|Ah83| . Air transportation in Armenia is the most convenient and comfortable means of getting into the country. There are large international airports that accept both external and domestic flights throughout the Republic. As of 2019, 11 airports operate in Armenia, however, only  |Yerevans|Yerevan| |Zvartnots_International_Airport|Zvartnots_International_Airport|  and  |Gyumris|Gyumri| |Shirak_Airport|Shirak_Airport|  are in use for commercial aviation. There are 3 additional civil airports currently under reconstruction in Armenia, including  |Syunik_Airport|Syunik_Airport| ,  |Stepanavan_Airport|Stepanavan_Airport|  and  |Goris_Airport|Goris_Airport| . The main Armenian airlines in operation are  |Armenia_Aircompany|Armenia_Aircompany| ,  |Atlantis_European_Airways|Atlantis_European_Airways| ,  |Armenia_Airways|Armenia_Airways|  and  |Taron_Avia|Taron_Avia| .     There are plenty of air connections between Yerevan and other regional cities, including Athens, Barcelona, Beirut, Berlin, Bucharest, Brussels, Damascus, Doha, Dubai, Istanbul, Kiev, Kuwait City, London, Milan, Minsk, Moscow, Paris, Prague, Riga, Rome, Tehran, Tel-Aviv, Tbilisi, Vienna, Venice and Warsaw, as well as daily connections to most major cities within the  |CIS|Commonwealth_Of_Independent_States|  region.  Statistics show that the number of tourists arriving in the country by air transportation increases yearly.  In 2018, passenger flow at the two main airports of Armenia reached a record high of 2,856,673 million people.      In November 2019, the creation of a Free Route Airspace between Armenia and Georgia was announced. The process has been carried out through the joint efforts of the  |General_Department_of_Civil_Aviation_of_Armenia|General_Department_Of_Civil_Aviation_Of_Armenia| , the  |Georgian_Civil_Aviation_Administration|Georgian_Civil_Aviation_Administration|  and  |Eurocontrol|Eurocontrol| . The Free Route Airspace between the two  |South_Caucasus|South_Caucasus|  countries will increase flights to around 40,000 annually.
