The Triumph of Time is a poem by  |Algernon_Charles_Swinburne|Algernon_Charles_Swinburne| , published in  Poems and Ballads  in 1866. It is in adapted  |ottava_rima|Ottava_Rima|  and is full of elaborate use of literary devices, particularly  |alliteration|Alliteration| . The theme, which purports to be autobiographical, is that of rejected love. The speaker deplores the ruin of his life, and in tones at times reminiscent of  Hamlet , craves oblivion, for which the sea serves as a constant metaphor.
