Modes of  |transport|Transport|  in  |Mozambique|Mozambique|  include  |rail|Rail_Transport| ,  |road|Road_Transport| ,  |water|Water_Transport| , and  |air|Air_Transport| . There are rail links serving principal cities and connecting the country with  |Malawi|Malawi| ,  |Zimbabwe|Zimbabwe|  and  |South_Africa|South_Africa| . There are over 30,000km of roads, but much of the network is unpaved.     On the  |Indian_Ocean|Indian_Ocean|  coast are several large  |seaports|Seaport| , including  |Nacala|Nacala| ,  |Beira|Beira|  and  |Maputo|Maputo| , with further ports being developed. There are 3,750km of  |navigable|Navigability|  inland waterways. There is an international  |airport|Airport|  at  |Maputo|Maputo_International_Airport| , 21 other paved airports, and over 100 with unpaved runways.                  The Mozambican railway system developed over more than a century from three different ports on the  |Indian_Ocean|Indian_Ocean|  that serve as terminals for separate lines to the hinterland. The railroads were major targets during the  |Mozambican_Civil_War|Mozambican_Civil_War| , were sabotaged by  |RENAMO|Renamo| , and are being rehabilitated.
