   This article is about the  geography  of the  Northern Mariana Islands .     The Northern Mariana Islands, together with  |Guam|Guam|  to the south, compose the  |Mariana_Islands|Mariana_Islands| . The southern islands are  |limestone|Limestone| , with level terraces and fringing  |coral|Coral|  reefs. The northern islands are volcanic, with active  |volcano|Volcano| es on  |Anatahan|Anatahan| ,  |Pagan|Pagan_Island|  and  |Agrihan|Agrihan| . The volcano on Agrihan has the highest elevation at . About one-fifth of the land is arable; another tenth is pasture. The primary natural resource is fish, some of which are  |endangered_species|Endangered_Species| , which leads to conflict. It is about  long and  wide. Anatahan began erupting suddenly from its east crater on May 10, 2003, at about 6 p.m. . It has since alternated between eruptive and calm periods. The islands have a  |tropical_marine_climate|Tropical_Marine_Climate|  moderated by seasonal northeast  |trade_winds|Trade_Winds| . There is little seasonal temperature variation. The dry season runs from December to June, and the rainy season from July to November and can include  |typhoons|Typhoon| . The  |Guinness_Book_of_World_Records|Guinness_Book_Of_World_Records|  has cited  |Saipan|Saipan|  as having the most equable climate in the world.
