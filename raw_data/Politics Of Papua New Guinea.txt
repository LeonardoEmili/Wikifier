The Head of the Commonwealth, or simply Queen of Papua New Guinea, is represented in  |Papua_New_Guinea|Papua_New_Guinea|  by a governor general who acts on the advice of the prime minister and the cabinet. The  |Governor_General_of_Papua_New_Guinea|Governor_General_Of_Papua_New_Guinea|  is elected by parliament.     The governments of Papua New Guinea are characterized by weak political parties and highly unstable parliamentary coalitions.   The  |prime_minister|Prime_Minister| , elected by Parliament, chooses the other members of the  |cabinet|Cabinet_Of_Papua_New_Guinea| . Each ministry is headed by a cabinet member, who is assisted by a permanent secretary, a career public servant, who directs the staff of the ministry. The cabinet consists of members, including the  |Prime_Minister|Prime_Minister|  and ministers of executive departments. They answer politically to the parliament.     The Governor General appoints the Chief Justice of the Supreme Court on the advice of the Prime Minister and the Leader of the Opposition. The Governor General appoints the other justices with the advice of a judicial commission.
