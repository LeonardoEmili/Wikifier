|Taiwan|Taiwan| , officially the Republic of China , is an  |island_state|Island_Country|  in  |East_Asia|East_Asia| . The main island of Taiwan, known historically as Formosa, makes up 99% of the  |area_controlled_by_the_ROC|Free_Area_Of_The_Republic_Of_China| , measuring  and lying some  across the  |Taiwan_Strait|Taiwan_Strait|  from the southeastern coast of  |mainland_China|Mainland_China| . The  |East_China_Sea|East_China_Sea|  lies to its north, the  |Philippine_Sea|Philippine_Sea|  to its east, the  |Luzon_Strait|Luzon_Strait|  directly to its south and the  |South_China_Sea|South_China_Sea|  to its southwest.  |Smaller_islands|List_Of_Islands_Of_Taiwan|  include a number in the Taiwan Strait including the  |Penghu|Penghu|  archipelago, the  |Kinmen|Kinmen|  and  |Matsu_Islands|Matsu_Islands|  near the Chinese coast, and some of the  |South_China_Sea_Islands|South_China_Sea_Islands| .     The main island is a tilted  |fault_block|Fault_Block| , characterized by the contrast between the eastern two-thirds, consisting mostly of five rugged mountain ranges parallel to the east coast, and the flat to gently rolling plains of the western third, where the majority of Taiwans population reside. There are several peaks over 3,500  m, the highest being  |Yu_Shan|Yu_Shan|  at , making Taiwan the worlds  |fourth-highest_island|List_Of_Islands_By_Highest_Point| . The tectonic boundary that formed these ranges is still active, and the island experiences many earthquakes, a few of them highly destructive. There are also many active  |submarine_volcano|Submarine_Volcano| es in the Taiwan Straits.     The climate ranges from  |tropical|Tropical_Monsoon_Climate|  in the south to  |subtropical|Humid_Subtropical_Climate|  in the north, and is governed by the  |East_Asian_Monsoon|East_Asian_Monsoon| . The main island is struck by an average of four  |typhoons|Typhoon|  in each year.
