Agnes Maria of Andechs-Merania was a Queen of  |France|France| . She is called Marie by some of the French chroniclers.       Agnes Maria was the daughter of  |Berthold,_Duke_of_Merania|Berthold,_Duke_Of_Merania| , who was Count of  |Andechs|Andechs| , a castle and territory near  |Ammersee|Ammersee| ,  |Bavaria|Bavaria| . Her mother was  |Agnes_of_Rochlitz|Agnes_Of_Rochlitz| .      In June 1196 Agnes married  |Philip_II_of_France|Philip_Ii_Of_France| , who had repudiated his second wife  |Ingeborg_of_Denmark|Ingeborg_Of_Denmark,_Queen_Of_France|  in 1193.  |Pope_Innocent_III|Pope_Innocent_Iii|  espoused the cause of Ingeborg; but Philip did not submit until 1200, when, nine months after  |interdict|Interdict|  had been added to  |excommunication|Excommunication| , he consented to a separation from Agnes.     Agnes died broken-hearted in July of the next year, at the castle of  |Poissy|Poissy| , and was buried in the  |Convent_of_St_Corentin|Convent_Of_St_Corentin| , near  |Nantes|Nantes| .       Agnes and Philip had two children:  |Philip_I,_Count_of_Boulogne|Philip_I,_Count_Of_Boulogne|  and  |Mary|Marie_Of_France,_Duchess_Of_Brabant| ,   who were legitimized by the Pope in 1201 at the request of the King. Little is known of the personality of Agnes, beyond the remarkable influence which she seems to have exercised over Philip.       She has been made the heroine of a  |tragedy|Tragedy|  by  |François_Ponsard|François_Ponsard| , Agnès de Méranie, and of an  |opera|Opera|  by  |Vincenzo_Bellini|Vincenzo_Bellini| ,  La straniera .
