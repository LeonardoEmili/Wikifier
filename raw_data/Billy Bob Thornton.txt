        Billy Bob Thornton  is an American actor, filmmaker, singer, songwriter, and musician.     Thornton had his first break when he co-wrote and starred in the 1992 thriller  One False Move , and received international attention after writing, directing, and starring in the  |independent|Independent_Film| |drama_film|Drama|   Sling Blade  , for which he won an  |Academy_Award_for_Best_Adapted_Screenplay|Academy_Award_For_Best_Adapted_Screenplay|  and was nominated for an  |Academy_Award_for_Best_Actor|Academy_Award_For_Best_Actor| . He appeared in several major film roles in the 1990s following Sling Blade, including  |Oliver_Stones|Oliver_Stone|  neo-noir  U Turn  , political drama  Primary Colors  , science fiction disaster film  Armageddon  , the highest-grossing film of that year, and the crime drama  A Simple Plan  , which earned him his third  |Oscar|Academy_Awards|  nomination.     In the 2000s, Thornton achieved further success in starring dramas  Monsters Ball  ,  The Man Who Wasnt There  , and  Friday Night Lights  ; comedies  Bandits  ,  Intolerable Cruelty  , and  Bad Santa  ; and action films  Eagle Eye  and  Faster  . In 2014, Thornton starred as  |Lorne_Malvo|Lorne_Malvo|  in the first season of the anthology series  Fargo , earning a nomination for the  |Outstanding_Lead_Actor_in_a_Miniseries_or_TV_Movie|Primetime_Emmy_Award_For_Outstanding_Lead_Actor_In_A_Miniseries_Or_A_Movie|  at the  |Emmy_Awards|66Th_Primetime_Emmy_Awards|  and won  |Best_Actor_in_a_Miniseries_or_TV_Film|Golden_Globe_Award_For_Best_Actor_-_Miniseries_Or_Television_Film|  at the  |72nd_Golden_Globe_Awards|72Nd_Golden_Globe_Awards| . In 2016, he starred in an Amazon original series,  Goliath , which earned him a  |Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Actor_–_Television_Series_Drama|Golden_Globe_Award_For_Best_Actor_–_Television_Series_Drama| .     Thornton has been vocal about his distaste for  |celebrity_culture|Celebrity_Culture| , choosing to keep his life out of the public eye. However, the attention of the media has proven unavoidable in certain cases, his marriage to  |Angelina_Jolie|Angelina_Jolie|  being a notable example.  Thornton has written a variety of films, usually set in the  |Southern_United_States|Southern_United_States|  and mainly co-written with  |Tom_Epperson|Tom_Epperson| , including  A Family Thing  and  The Gift  . After Sling Blade, he directed several other films, including  Daddy and Them  ,  All the Pretty Horses  , and  Jayne Mansfields Car  .     Thornton has received the Presidents Award from the  |Academy_of_Science_Fiction,_Fantasy_&_Horror_Films|Academy_Of_Science_Fiction,_Fantasy_&_Horror_Films| , a Special Achievement Award from the  |National_Board_of_Review|National_Board_Of_Review| , and a star on the  |Hollywood_Walk_of_Fame|Hollywood_Walk_Of_Fame| . He has also been nominated for an  |Emmy_Award|Emmy_Award| , four  |Golden_Globes|Golden_Globe| , and three  |Screen_Actors_Guild_Awards|Screen_Actors_Guild_Award| . In addition to film work, Thornton began a career as a singer-songwriter. He has released four solo albums and is the vocalist of the  |blues_rock|Blues_Rock|  band  |The_Boxmasters|The_Boxmasters| .       Billy Bob Thornton   was born on August 4, 1955,  in  |Alpine,_Arkansas|Alpine,_Arkansas| ,    the son of Virginia Roberta , a self-proclaimed psychic, and William Raymond Billy Ray Thornton , a high school history teacher and basketball coach. His brother Jimmy Don wrote a number of songs; Thornton recorded two of them on his solo albums.  He is of part Irish descent.  He has another brother, John David Thornton.     Thornton lived in numerous places in Arkansas during his childhood, including  |Alpine|Alpine,_Arkansas| ,  |Malvern|Malvern,_Arkansas| , and  |Mount_Holly|Mount_Holly,_Arkansas| . He was raised  |Methodist|Methodist|   in an extended family in a shack that had no electricity or plumbing.  He graduated from  |Malvern_High_School|Malvern_High_School|  in 1973. A good high school  |baseball|Baseball|  player, he tried out for the  |Kansas_City_Royals|Kansas_City_Royals| , but was released after an injury.  After a short period laying asphalt for the  |Arkansas_State_Transportation_Department|Arkansas_State_Highway_And_Transportation_Department| , he attended  |Henderson_State_University|Henderson_State_University|  to pursue studies in psychology but dropped out after two semesters.      In the mid-1980s, Thornton settled in  |Los_Angeles|Los_Angeles|  to pursue his career as an actor, with future writing partner Tom Epperson. He had a difficult time succeeding as an actor and worked in telemarketing, offshore  |wind_farm|Wind_Farm| ing, and fast food management between auditioning for acting jobs. He also played the drums and sang with South African rock band Jack Hammer. While working as a waiter for an industry event, he served film director and screenwriter  |Billy_Wilder|Billy_Wilder| . He struck up a conversation with Wilder, who advised Thornton to consider a career as a screenwriter.              Thorntons first screen role was in 1980s  South of Reno , where he played a small role as a counter man in a restaurant. He also made an appearance as a pawn store clerk in the 1987  Matlock  episode The Photographer . Another one of his early screen roles was as a cast member on the CBS sitcom Hearts Afire and in 1989 he appeared as an angry heckler in Adam Sandlers debut film  Going Overboard . His role as the villain in 1992s  One False Move , which he also co-wrote, brought him to the attention of critics. He also had small roles in the 1990s films  Indecent Proposal ,  On Deadly Ground ,  Bound by Honor , and  Tombstone . He went on to write, direct, and star in the 1996  |independent_film|Independent_Film|   Sling Blade . The film, an expansion of the short film  Some Folks Call It a Sling Blade , introduced the story of a  |mentally_handicapped|Mentally_Handicapped|  man imprisoned for a gruesome and seemingly inexplicable murder.     Sling Blade garnered international acclaim.
