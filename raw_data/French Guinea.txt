   French Guinea  was a French colonial possession in  |West_Africa|West_Africa| . Its borders, while changed over time, were in 1958 those of the current independent nation of  |Guinea|Guinea| .     French Guinea was established by France in 1891, within the same borders as its previous colony known as  |Rivières_du_Sud|Rivières_Du_Sud|  . Prior to 1882, the coastal portions of French Guinea were part of the French colony of  |Senegal|History_Of_Senegal| .     In 1891, Rivières du Sud was placed under the colonial lieutenant governor at  |Dakar|Dakar| , who had authority over the French coastal regions east to  |Porto-Novo|Porto-Novo|  . In 1894 Rivières du Sud,  |Cote_dIvoire|Cote_Divoire|  and  |Dahomey|Dahomey|  were separated into independent colonies, with Rivières du Sud being renamed as the Colony of French Guinea. In 1895, French Guinea was made one of several dependent colonies and its Governor became one of several Lieutenant Governors who reported to a  |Governor-General|French_West_Africahistory|  in  |Dakar|Dakar| . In 1904, this federation of colonies was formalised as  |French_West_Africa|French_West_Africa| . French Guinea, Senegal, Dahomey, Cote dIvoire and  |Upper_Senegal_and_Niger|Upper_Senegal_And_Niger| , were each ruled by a lieutenant governor, under the Governor General in Dakar.          Guinea was ruled by France until 1958. It became independent from  |France|France|  in 1958 following its voters rejection of  |Charles_de_Gaulles|Charles_De_Gaulle| |Constitution_of_1958|Constitution_Of_France| . At the time French Guinea was the only colony to reject the new constitution. French Guinea became the modern-day country of  |Guinea|Guinea| , keeping  |French|French_Language| .
