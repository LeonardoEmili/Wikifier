A reflux suppressant is any one of a number of  |drugs|Medication|  used to combat  |oesophageal_reflux|Oesophageal_Reflux| . Commonly, following ingestion a raft of  |alginic_acid|Alginic_Acid|  is created, floating on the stomach contents by carbon dioxide released by the drug. This forms a mechanical barrier to further reflux. Some preparations also contain  |antacids|Antacid|  to protect the  |oesophagus|Oesophagus| .     Reflux can also be coincidentally reduced by the  |motility_stimulants|:Category:Motility_Stimulants|  and  |antidopaminergics|Antidopaminergic| .
