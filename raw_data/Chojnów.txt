The town survived the  |Hussites|Hussite| , who burned almost the entire town Center and castle, but it quickly helped recover its former glory. The largest boom Haynau experienced was in the 16th century, however by the end of that century began to decline due to fires and epidemic, which claimed many victims in 1613.     From 1618 to 1648 , there was another outbreak in the city and in 1740 Hanyau was incorporated by  |Prussia|Prussia| .     During the  |Napoleonic_wars|Napoleonic_Wars|  there were more epidemics. A railway line was connected in the 19th century.  |Sewer|Sewerage| ,  |Gas_light|Gas_Light| ing a Newspaper and a hospital soon followed as the towns economy improved.     The city was not spared in  |World_War_II|World_War_Ii| , with 60% of the town being destroyed on February 10, 1945 when Soviet  |Red_Army|Red_Army|  troops took the town. After World War II and the implementation of the  |Oder-Neisse_line|Oder-Neisse_Line|  in 1945, the town passed to the  |Republic_of_Poland|Peoples_Republic_Of_Poland| . The German population was  |expelled|Flight_And_Expulsion_Of_Germans_From_Poland_During_And_After_World_War_Ii|  from the region.     In March 31, 2011, the city had 14,367 inhabitants. Among local products are: paper, agricultural machinery, chains, metal furniture for hospitals, equipment for the  |meat_industry|Meat_Industry| , beer, wine, leather clothing, and clothing for infants, children and adults. The local government-run weekly newspaper is  |Gazeta_Chojnowska|Gazeta_Chojnowska| , which has been published since 1992.     Among the interesting monuments of Chojnów are the 13th-century castle of the  |Dukes_of_Legnica|Duchy_Of_Legnica|  , two old churches, the Baszta Tkaczy  and preserved fragments of city walls.     The biggest green area in Chojnów is small forest Park Piastowski , named after  |Piast_dynasty|Piast_Dynasty| . Wild animals that can be found in the Chojnów area are roe-deer , foxes, rabbits and wild domestic animals, especially cats.     Every year in the first days of June, the Days of Chojnów  are celebrated. The Whole-Poland bike race Masters has been organized yearly in Chojnów for the past few years. Editions have a run of 900 copies and it is one of the oldest newspapers in Poland issued without interruption. The Chojnów is the official newspaper of Chojnów with copy run of 750 copies.
