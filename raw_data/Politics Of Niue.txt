Politics of Niue  takes place in a framework of a  |parliamentary|Parliamentary_System| |representative_democratic|Representative_Democracy| |dependency|Dependent_Territory| , whereby the Chief Minister is the  |head_of_government|Head_Of_Government| , and of a non-partisan system. Niue is self-governing in free association with  |New_Zealand|New_Zealand|  and is fully responsible for internal affairs. New Zealand retains some responsibility for external affairs, in consultation with Niue. The  |Niue_Constitution_Act_1974|Niue_Constitution_Act_1974|  vests executive authority in  |Her_Majesty_the_Queen_in_Right_of_New_Zealand|Monarchy_In_New_Zealand|  and the  |Governor-General_of_New_Zealand|Governor-General_Of_New_Zealand| . The constitution specifies that in everyday practice, it is exercised by a  |Cabinet|Cabinet|  of the Premier of Niue and three other ministers. The premier and ministers must be members of the  |Niue_Assembly|Niue_Assembly| , the nations legislative assembly.
