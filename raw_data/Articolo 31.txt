Articolo 31 was a band from  |Milan|Milan| ,  |Italy|Italy| , formed in 1990 by  |J-Ax|J-Ax|  and DJ Jad, combining  |hip_hop|Hip_Hop_Music| ,  |funk|Funk| , pop and traditional Italian musical forms. They are one of the most popular Italian hip hop groups.       Articolo 31 were formed by rapper  |J-Ax|J-Ax|  and DJ Jad .    In the spoken intro of the album Strade di Città , it is stated that the band is named after the article of the  |Irish_constitution|Irish_Constitution|  guaranteeing freedom of the press, although article 31 of the Irish constitution is not about the freedom of the press. They probably meant the  |Section_31_of_the_Broadcasting_Authority_Act|Censorshipintherepublicofirelandthetroubles| .     Articolo 31 released one of the first Italian hip hop records, Strade di città, in 1993. Soon, they signed with  |BMG_Ricordi|Bertelsmann_Music_Group|  and started to mix rap with pop music - a move that earned them great commercial success but that alienated the underground hip hop scene, who perceived them as traitors.   In 1997,  |DJ_Gruff|Dj_Gruff|  dissed Articolo 31 in a track titled 1 vs 2 on the first album of the beatmaker Fritz da Cat, starting a feud that would go on for years.     In 2001, Articolo 31 collaborated with the American  |old_school|Old_School_Hip_Hop|  rapper  |Kurtis_Blow|Kurtis_Blow|  on the album XChé SI!. In the same year, they made the film  Senza filtro  . Their producer was  |Franco_Godi|Franco_Godi| , who also produced the music for the Signor Rossi animated series.      Their 2002 album Domani smetto represented a further departure from hip hop, increasingly relying on the formula of rapping over pop music samples.  Several of their songs rotate around the theme of soft drugs legalization in Italy .     Following their 2003 album Italiano medio , the band took a break. Both J Ax and DJ Jad have been involved with solo projects. In 2006, the group declared an indefinite  |hiatus|Recess| .
