     Married... with Children is an American television  |sitcom|Sitcom|  that aired on  |Fox|Fox_Broadcasting_Company| , created by  |Michael_G._Moye|Michael_G._Moye|  and  |Ron_Leavitt|Ron_Leavitt| . Originally broadcast from April 5, 1987 to June 9, 1997, it is the longest-lasting live-action sitcom on Fox and the first to be broadcast in the networks primetime slot. One episode was broadcast on  |FX|Fx|  on June 18, 2002.     The show follows the  |suburban|Chicagoland| |Chicago|Chicago|  lives of  |Al_Bundy|Al_Bundy| , a once glorious  |high_school_football|High_School_Football|  player turned hard-luck womens shoe salesman; his lazy wife,  |Peggy|Peggy_Bundy| ; their attractive, dumb and popular daughter,  |Kelly|Kelly_Bundy| ; and their smart, horny and unpopular son,  |Bud|Bud_Bundy| . Their neighbors are the upwardly mobile  |Steve_Rhoades|Steve_Rhoades|  and his feminist wife  |Marcy|Marcy_Darcy| , who later gets remarried to  |Jefferson_DArcy|Jefferson_Darcy| , a white-collar criminal who becomes her trophy husband and Als sidekick. Most storylines involve Als schemes being foiled by his own cartoonish dim wit and bad luck.     The series comprises 259 episodes and 11 seasons. Its  |theme_song|Theme_Song|  is  |Love_and_Marriage|Love_And_Marriage|  by  |Sammy_Cahn|Sammy_Cahn|  and  |Jimmy_Van_Heusen|Jimmy_Van_Heusen| , performed by  |Frank_Sinatra|Frank_Sinatra|  from the 1955 television production  |Our_Town|Our_Town| .     The first two seasons of the series were videotaped at  |ABC_Television_Center|The_Prospect_Studios|  in Hollywood. From season three to season eight, the show was taped at  |Sunset_Gower_Studios|Sunset_Gower_Studios|  in Hollywood, and the remaining three seasons were taped at  |Sony_Pictures_Studios|Sony_Pictures_Studios|  in  |Culver_City|Culver_City| . The series was produced by  |Embassy_Communications|Elp_Communications|  during its first season and half of its second season and the remaining seasons by ELP Communications under the studio  |Columbia_Pictures_Television|Columbia_Pictures_Television| .
