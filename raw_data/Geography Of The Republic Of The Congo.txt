           The  Republic of the Congo  is located in the western part of  |Central_Africa|Central_Africa| . Situated on the  |Equator|Equator| , it is bordered by the  |Angola|Angola|  exclave of  |Cabinda|Cabinda_Province|  to the south , the  |Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo|Democratic_Republic_Of_The_Congo|  to the south and east , the  |Central_African_Republic|Central_African_Republic|  and  |Cameroon|Cameroon|  to the north and  |Gabon|Gabon|  to the west . Congo has a 169  km long  |Atlantic|Atlantic_Ocean|  coast with several important ports. The Republic of the Congo covers an area of 342,000  km², of which 341,500  km² is land while 500  km² is water. Congo claims  of territorial sea.     The capital of the Republic of the Congo is  |Brazzaville|Brazzaville| , located on the  |Congo_River|Congo_River|  immediately across from  |Kinshasa|Kinshasa| , capital of the  |Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo|Democratic_Republic_Of_The_Congo| . With a metropolitan population of approximately 1.5 million, Brazzaville is by far the largest city in the Republic, having almost twice the population of  |Pointe_Noire|Pointe_Noire|  , the countrys second largest city. About 70% of the population lives in Brazzaville, Pointe-Noire, or along the railroad between them.           Congo is a tropical nation, which means it has a tropical climate. The wet season lasts from March to June and the dry season for the rest of the year. Temperature and humidity are high as in all tropical nations. The rivers of the country are flooded seasonally.       The terrain is a variation of  |coastal_plains|Coastal_Plain| ,  |mountainous|Mountain|  regions,  |plateaus|Plateau|  and fertile  |valleys|Valley| . About 70 percent of the countrys area is covered by  |rain_forest|Rain_Forest| . The highest point, at 1,020 m, is  |Mont_Nabeba|Mont_Nabeba|  in the  |Mayumbe|Mayumbe|  mountains. The major rivers are the  |Congo_River|Congo_River|  at the border with the  |Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo|Democratic_Republic_Of_The_Congo| , and the  |Kouilou_Niari_River|Kouilou_Niari_River| .     A 2014 expedition leaving from Itanga village discovered a  |peat_bog|Bog|  as big as  |England|England|  which stretches into neighboring  |Democratic_Republic_of_Congo|Democratic_Republic_Of_Congo| .       |UNESCO|Unesco|  has declared two world  |biosphere_reserves|Man_And_The_Biosphere_Programme|  in the country:  |Odzala|Odzala_National_Park|  in 1977 and  |Dimonika|Dimonika_Biosphere_Reserve|  in 1988.          Natural resources include  |petroleum|Petroleum| ,  |timber|Timber| ,  |potash|Potash| ,  |lead|Lead| ,  |zinc|Zinc| ,  |uranium|Uranium| ,  |copper|Copper| ,  |phosphates|Phosphate| ,  |gold|Gold| ,  |magnesium|Magnesium| ,  |natural_gas|Natural_Gas| , and  |hydropower|Hydropower| .     As of a 2012 estimate, 1.55% of the land is arable, while only 0.20% contains permanent crops. Approximately 20  km² is irrigated .         Environmental issues include the high level of air pollution from vehicle emissions, water pollution from the dumping of raw sewage, tap water not being potable, and deforestation.     Congo is party to the international agreements on  |Biodiversity|Biodiversity| ,  |Climate_Change|United_Nations_Framework_Convention_On_Climate_Change| ,  |Desertification|Desertification| ,  |Endangered_Species|Endangered_Species| ,  |Ozone_Layer_Protection|Ozone_Layer_Protection| ,  |Tropical_Timber_83|Tropical_Timber_83| ,  |Tropical_Timber_94|Tropical_Timber_94| ,  |Wetlands|Wetlands| . It has signed but not ratified the  |Law_of_the_Sea|Law_Of_The_Sea|  and so on.
