Robert Gordis was an American leading  |Conservative|Conservative_Judaism| |rabbi|Rabbi| . He founded the first Conservative  |Jewish_day_school|Jewish_Day_School| , served as President of the  |Rabbinical_Assembly|Rabbinical_Assembly|  and the  |Synagogue_Council_of_America|Synagogue_Council_Of_America| , and was a professor at  |Jewish_Theological_Seminary_of_America|Jewish_Theological_Seminary_Of_America|  from 1940 to 1992.     He wrote one of the first pamphlets explaining Conservative ideology in 1946, and in 1988 he chaired the Commission on the Philosophy of Conservative Judaism which produced the official statement of Conservative ideology Emet Ve-Emunah .     Gordis was the founding editor in 1951 of the quarterly journal Judaism.
