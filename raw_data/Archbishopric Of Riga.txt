         The Archbishopric of Riga  was an  |archbishop|Archbishop| ric in  |Medieval_Livonia|Terra_Mariana| , a subject to the  |Holy_See|Holy_See| . It was established in 1186 as the bishopric of  |Livonia|Livonia|  at  |Ikšķile|Ikšķile| , then after moving to  |Riga|Riga|  it became the bishopric of Riga in 1202 and was elevated to an archbishopric in 1255.         The archbishops of Riga were also the secular rulers of  |Riga|Riga|  until 1561 when during the  |reformation|Reformation|  the territory converted from  |Catholicism|Catholicism|  to  |Lutheranism|Lutheranism|  and all church territories were secularized. The  |see|Episcopal_See|  was restored as a  |diocese|Diocese|  of the  |Catholic_Church|Catholic_Church|  in 1918 and raised into an archdiocese in 1923.               A new  |Bishopric_of_Livonia|Diocese_Of_Inflanty|  was established in  |Latgalia|Latgalia|  in 1621 during the  |Inflanty_Voivodeship|Inflanty_Voivodeship|  of the  |Polish–Lithuanian_Commonwealth|Polish–Lithuanian_Commonwealth| .         The Archbishops of Riga were innovators in the field of  |minting|Mint|  currency, reviving techniques abandoned since the collapse of Rome. The names of individual archbishops after 1418, as well as the years of their respective reigns, are stamped on Livonian  |pennies|Penny|.
