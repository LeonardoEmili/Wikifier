            Gorillaz are a British  |virtual_band|Virtual_Band|  created in 1998 by  |Blur|Blur|  frontman  |Damon_Albarn|Damon_Albarn|  and artist  |Jamie_Hewlett|Jamie_Hewlett| . The band primarily consists of four animated members:  |Stuart_2-D_Pot|2-D| ,  |Murdoc_Niccals|Murdoc_Niccals| ,  |Noodle|Noodle| , and  |Russel_Hobbs|Russel_Hobbs| . Their  |fictional_universe|Fictional_Universe|  is explored through music videos, interviews, and other short cartoons. In reality, Albarn is the only permanent musical contributor, and often collaborates with other musicians.     With Gorillaz, Albarn departed from the distinct  |Britpop|Britpop|  of his band  |Blur|Blur|  and explored influences including  |hip_hop|Hip_Hop| ,  |electronic_music|Electronic_Music| , and  |world_music|World_Music|  through an eccentrically  |postmodern|Postmodern|  approach. The bands 2001 debut album  Gorillaz  went triple platinum in the UK and double platinum in Europe and earned the group an entry in the  Guinness Book of World Records  as the Most Successful Virtual Band.  It was nominated for the  |Mercury_Prize|Mercury_Prize| , but the nomination was withdrawn at the bands request.  Their second studio album,  Demon Days  , went six times platinum in the UK  and  |double_platinum|Music_Recording_Sales_Certification|  in the US.        The third Gorillaz studio album,  Plastic Beach , was released on 3 March 2010. The fourth,  The Fall , was released on 25 December 2010 as a surprise gift for paying fan club members, then on 18 April 2011 as an official release. The fifth,  Humanz , was released after a six-year hiatus on 28 April 2017.  Their sixth studio album,  The Now Now , was released on 29 June 2018. Musician  |Damon_Albarn|Damon_Albarn|  and comic creator  |Jamie_Hewlett|Jamie_Hewlett|  met in 1990 when guitarist  |Graham_Coxon|Graham_Coxon| , a fan of Hewletts work, asked him to interview  |Blur|Blur| , a band Albarn and Coxon had recently formed.  The interview was published in  |Deadline|Deadline|  magazine, home of Hewletts comic strip  Tank Girl . Hewlett initially thought Albarn was arsey, a wanker ; despite becoming acquaintances with the band, they often did not get on, especially after Hewlett began seeing Coxons ex-girlfriend Jane Olliver. Despite this, Albarn and Hewlett started sharing a flat on  |Westbourne_Grove|Westbourne_Grove|  in London in 1997.  Hewlett had recently broken up with Olliver and Albarn was at the end of his highly publicised relationship with  |Justine_Frischmann|Justine_Frischmann|  of  |Elastica|Elastica| .     The idea to create Gorillaz came about when Albarn and Hewlett were watching  |MTV|Mtv| . Hewlett said, If you watch MTV for too long, its a bit like hell  – theres nothing of substance there. So we got this idea for a virtual band, something that would be a comment on that.   The band originally identified themselves as Gorilla and the first song they recorded was Ghost Train ,  which was later released as a  |B-side|B-Side|  on their single  |Rock_the_House|Rock_The_House|  and the B-side compilation  G Sides . In April 2012, Albarn told  The Guardian  that he and Hewlett had fallen out and that future Gorillaz projects were unlikely .  Tension between the two had been building, partly due to a belief held by Hewlett that his contributions to Gorillaz were being diminished. Speaking to The Guardian in April 2017, Hewlett explained: Damon had half the Clash on stage, and Bobby Womack and Mos Def and De La Soul, and fucking Hypnotic Brass Ensemble and Bashy and everyone else. It was the greatest band ever. And the screen on stage behind them seemed to get smaller every day. I’d say, ‘Have we got a new screen?’ and the tour manager was like, ‘No, it’s the same screen.’ Because it seemed to me like it was getting smaller.  On 25 April 2012, in an interview with Metro, Albarn was more optimistic about Gorillaz future, saying that once he had worked out his differences with Hewlett, he was sure that they would make another record.  On 24 June 2013, Hewlett stated that he and Albarn planned to someday record a follow-up to their 2010 album  Plastic Beach . Télémoustique 5 May 2010, page 59, Roundhouse concert review by Luc Lorfèvre      Gorillaz kicked off their first ever world tour in October 2010, with some preceding festival dates in the summer, such as at the Byblos Festival in Lebanon, the  |Roskilde_Festival|Roskilde_Festival|  in Denmark, headlining the  |Glastonbury_Festival|Glastonbury_Festival|  in England and one show at the  |Citadel_of_Damascus|Citadel_Of_Damascus|  in Syria.   The  |Escape_to_Plastic_Beach_Tour|Escape_To_Plastic_Beach_Tour|  took them across North America in October, Europe in November, and finally through Australia, New Zealand, and with one date in Hong Kong in December.  During the final show of the tour at the  |Vector_Arena|Vector_Arena|  in  |Auckland|Auckland| , New Zealand on 21 December 2010, Albarn announced that it would be their final show with the  Plastic Beach  line up.      On 8 July 2017, Gorillaz embarked upon the  |Humanz_Tour|Humanz_Tour| , the bands second world tour and first since 2010. With 53 scheduled shows and lasting over a year, the tour was Gorillaz longest tour in their history.  On 6 December, Gorillaz announced they would be playing  |BoomTown_Fair|Boomtown_Fair|  as an exclusive one-off UK show .  After 53 performances, on 30 March 2018, Humanz Tour concluded in  |São_Paulo|São_Paulo| , Brazil.      On 1 June 2018, Gorillaz embarked  |The_Now_Now_Tour|The_Now_Now_Tour| , in  |Nuremberg|Nuremberg| ,  |Germany|Germany| . On 20 October, Gorillaz played  |Song_2|Song_2|  by  |Blur|Blur| , at Demon Dayz Festival, with  |Graham_Coxon|Graham_Coxon| , in  |Los_Angeles|Los_Angeles| ,  |California|California| .  After 29 performances, on 24 October, The Now Now Tour concluded, in  |Mexico_City|Mexico_City| .   The Now Now Tour had participations of  |The_Internet|The_Internet|  and  |Little_Dragon|Little_Dragon| .
