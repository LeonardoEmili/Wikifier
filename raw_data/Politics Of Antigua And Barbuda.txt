   The politics of Antigua and Barbuda takes place in a framework of a  |unitary|Unitary_State| |parliamentary|Parliamentary_System| |representative_democratic|Representative_Democracy|  monarchy, wherein the  |Sovereign_of_Antigua_and_Barbuda|Monarchy_In_Antigua_And_Barbuda|  is the  |head_of_state|Head_Of_State| , appointing a  |Governor-General|List_Of_Governors-General_Of_Antigua_And_Barbuda|  to act as  |vice-regal|Viceroy|  representative in the nation. A  |Prime_Minister|List_Of_Prime_Ministers_Of_Antigua_And_Barbuda|  is appointed by the Governor-General as the  |head_of_government|Head_Of_Government| , and of a  |multi-party_system|Multi-Party_System| ; the Prime Minister advises the Governor-General on the appointment of a Council of Ministers.  |Executive_power|Executive_Power|  is exercised by the government.  |Legislative_power|Legislative_Power|  is vested in both the government and the two chambers of the  |Parliament|Parliament_Of_Antigua_And_Barbuda| . The  |bicameral|Bicameral|  Parliament consists of the Senate and the House of Representatives .     Antigua and Barbuda has a long history of free elections, three of which have resulted in peaceful changes of government. Since the  |1951_general_election|Antigua_And_Barbuda_General_Election,_1951| , the party system has been dominated by the  |Antigua_Labour_Party|Antigua_Labour_Party|  , for a long time was dominated by the Bird family, particularly Prime Ministers  |Vere|Vere_Bird|  and  |Lester_Bird|Lester_Bird| . The opposition claimed to be disadvantaged by the ALPs longstanding monopoly on patronage and its control of the media, especially in the  |1999_general_election|Antigua_And_Barbuda_General_Election,_1999| . The  |most_recent_elections|Antigua_And_Barbuda_General_Election,_2009|  to the House of Representatives were held on 12 June 2014. The  |Antigua_Labour_Party|Antigua_And_Barbuda_Labour_Party|  government was elected with fourteen seats. The  |United_Progressive_Party|United_Progressive_Party|  has three seats in the House of Representatives.     Constitutional safeguards include freedom of speech, press, worship, movement, and association. Antigua and Barbuda is a member of the eastern Caribbean court system. The  |Judiciary|Judiciary|  is independent of the executive and the legislature. Jurisprudence is based on  |English_common_law|English_Lawcommon_Law| .
