Charles Chandler Krulak is a retired  |United_States_Marine_Corps|United_States_Marine_Corps|  officer who served as the 31st  |Commandant_of_the_Marine_Corps|Commandant_Of_The_Marine_Corps|  from July 1, 1995 to June 30, 1999. He is the son of Lieutenant General  |Victor_H_Brute_Krulak|Victor_H_Krulak| , who served in  |World_War_II|World_War_Ii| ,  |Korea|Korean_War| , and  |Vietnam|Vietnam_War| . He was the 13th President of  |Birmingham_Southern_College|Birmingham_Southern_College|  after his stint as a non-executive director of English  |association_football|Association_Football|  club  |Aston_Villa|Aston_Villa_F_C_| .       Krulak was born in  |Quantico_Virginia|Quantico_Virginia| , on March 4, 1942, the son of Amy  and  |Victor_H_Krulak|Victor_H_Krulak| . He graduated from  |Phillips_Exeter_Academy|Phillips_Exeter_Academy|  in  |Exeter_New_Hampshire|Exeter_New_Hampshire| , in 1960, where he was classmates with novelist  |John_Irving|John_Irving| . Krulak then attended the  |United_States_Naval_Academy|United_States_Naval_Academy| , graduating in 1964 with a bachelors degree. Krulak also holds a masters degree in labor relations from  |George_Washington_University|George_Washington_University|  . He is a graduate of the  |Amphibious_Warfare_School|Amphibious_Warfare_School|  ; the  |Army_Command_and_General_Staff_College|Army_Command_And_General_Staff_College|  ; and the  |National_War_College|National_War_College|  .       After his commissioning and graduation from  |The_Basic_School|The_Basic_School|  at  |Marine_Corps_Base_Quantico|Marine_Corps_Base_Quantico| , Krulak held a variety of command and staff positions. His command positions included:  |commanding_officer|Commanding_Officer|  of a platoon and two rifle companies during two tours of duty in  |Vietnam|Vietnam_War| ; commanding officer of Special Training Branch and Recruit Series at  |Marine_Corps_Recruit_Depot_San_Diego|Marine_Corps_Recruit_Depot_San_Diego| ,  |California|California|  ; commanding officer of Counter-Guerilla Warfare School,  |Northern_Training_Area|Camp_Gonsalves|  on  |Okinawa|Okinawa|  , Company officer at the United States Naval Academy ; commanding officer of the Marine Barracks at  |Naval_Air_Station_North_Island|Naval_Air_Station_North_Island| , California , and commanding officer,  |3rd_Battalion_3rd_Marines|3Rd_Battalion_3Rd_Marines|  .     Krulaks staff assignments included: operations officer,  |2nd_Battalion_9th_Marines|2Nd_Battalion_9Th_Marines|  ; chief of the Combat Arms Monitor Section at  |Headquarters_Marine_Corps|Headquarters_Marine_Corps| ,  |Washington_D_C_|Washington_D_C_|  ; executive assistant to the Director of Personnel Management, Headquarters Marine Corps ; Plans Office, Fleet Marine Forces Pacific,  |Camp_H_M_Smith|Camp_H_M_Smith| ,  |Hawaii|Hawaii|  ; executive officer,  |3rd_Marine_Regiment|3Rd_Marine_Regiment| ,  |1st_Marine_Expeditionary_Brigade|1St_Marine_Expeditionary_Brigade| ; assistant chief of staff, maritime pre-positioning ships, 1st MEB; assistant chief of staff for operations, 1st Marine Expeditionary Brigade; and the military assistant to the assistant secretary of defense for command, control, communications and intelligence, Office of the  |Secretary_of_Defense|United_States_Secretary_Of_Defense| .     Krulak was assigned duty as the deputy director of the  |White_House_Military_Office|White_House_Military_Office|  in September 1987. While serving in this capacity, he was selected for promotion to  |brigadier_general|Brigadier_General|  in November 1988. He was advanced to that grade on June 5, 1989, and assigned duties as the commanding general, 10th MEB/Assistant division commander,  |2nd_Marine_Division|2Nd_Marine_Division| , Fleet Marine Force Atlantic, at  |Marine_Corps_Base_Camp_Lejeune|Marine_Corps_Base_Camp_Lejeune| ,  |North_Carolina|North_Carolina|  on July 10, 1989. On June 1, 1990, he assumed duties as the commanding general,  |2nd_Force_Service_Support_Group|2Nd_Marine_Logistics_Group|  Group/Commanding general, 6th Marine Expeditionary Brigade, Fleet Marine Force Atlantic and commanded the 2d FSSG during the  |Gulf_War|Gulf_War| . He served in this capacity until July 12, 1991, and was assigned duty as assistant deputy chief of staff for manpower and reserve affairs , Headquarters Marine Corps on August 5, 1991. He was advanced to  |major_general|Major_General|  on March 20, 1992. Krulak was assigned as commanding general,  |Marine_Corps_Combat_Development_Command|Marine_Corps_Combat_Development_Command| , Quantico, on August 24, 1992, and was promoted to  |lieutenant_general|Lieutenant_General|  on September 1, 1992. On July 22, 1994, he was assigned as commander of Marine Forces Pacific/commanding general, Fleet Marine Force Pacific, and in March 1995 he was nominated to serve as the  |Commandant_of_the_Marine_Corps|Commandant_Of_The_Marine_Corps| . On June, 29, he was promoted to  |general|General|  and assumed duties as the 31st commandant on June 30, 1995. He was relieved on June 30, 1999, by General  |James_L_Jones|James_L_Jones| .     In 1997, Krulak became a Life Member of the  |Sons_of_the_Revolution|Sons_Of_The_Revolution|  in the State of California.      Krulak attracted some attention during his tenure as Commandant by his custom of delivering Christmas cookies to each Marine duty post in the Washington area.        Krulak joined  |MBNA|Mbna|  America in September 1999 as chief administrative officer, responsible for personnel, benefits, compensation, education, and other administrative services. Krulak has served as the Senior Vice Chairman and  |Chief_Executive_Officer|Chief_Executive_Officer|  of MBNA Europe and was based at the Chester campus in the UK. He was the executive vice chairman and chief administration officer of MBNA Corporation . He retired from MBNA in 2005.     Following the takeover of English football club  |Aston_Villa|Aston_Villa_F_C_|  by MBNA Chairman  |Randy_Lerner|Randy_Lerner|  in August 2006 and as of September 19, 2006, Krulak joined the board of Aston Villa as non-executive director where he posted on several fans forums. Krulak was generally referred to as The General by fans on these boards.     Krulak also serves on the boards of  |ConocoPhillips|Conocophillips| ,  |Freeport_McMoran|Freeport_Mcmoran|  and  |Union_Pacific_Corporation|Union_Pacific_Corporation| .    In addition, he serves on the advisory council of  |Hope_For_The_Warriors|Hope_For_The_Warriors| , a national non-profit dedicated to provide a full cycle of non-medical care to combat wounded service members, their families, and families of the fallen from each military branch.      Krulak was elected as the 13th President of  |Birmingham–Southern_College|Birmingham–Southern_College|  in Birmingham, Alabama on March 21, 2011, and retired June 1, 2015. He received an honorary doctorate of Humane Letters from Birmingham-Southern College. The Krulak Institute Institute for Leadership, Experiential Learning, and Civic Engagement at Birmingham-Southern College is named for him.     Krulak is the Vice Chair of the  |Sweet_Briar_College|Sweet_Briar_College|  Board of Directors. He joined the Board in the Summer of 2015.        General Krulaks decorations and medals include:             Krulak famously referred to the Strategic Corporal and the  |Three_Block_War|Three_Block_War|  as two of the key lessons identified from the deployments in  |Somalia|Somalia| ,  |Haiti|Haiti|  and  |Bosnia|Bosnia_And_Herzegovina| . These concepts are still considered vital in understanding the increasing complexity of modern  |battle|Battle| fields.     Krulak explained some of his warfighting philosophy in an interview with  |Tom_Clancy|Tom_Clancy|  in Clancys nonfiction book  Marine . Clancy referred to Krulak as Warrior Prince of the Corps. Krulak also rewrote the Marine Corps basic combat study text,  MCDP 1: Warfighting , incorporating his theories on operations in the modern battlefield.       Krulak is married to Zandi Meyers from Annapolis. They have two sons: CAPT. David C. Krulak, the current Executive Officer for Naval Hospital Yokosuka, Japan  and Dr. Todd C. Krulak, PhD., a retired freelance rave DJ who is now a professor at Samford University; and five grandchildren: 2ndLT Brian Krulak, Katie, Mary, Matthew and Charles.
