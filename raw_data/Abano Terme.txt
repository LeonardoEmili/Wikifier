     Abano Terme  is a town and  comune  in the  |province_of_Padua|Province_Of_Padua| , in the  |Veneto|Veneto|  region,  |Italy|Italy| , on the eastern slope of the  |Colli_Euganei|Colli_Euganei| ; it is  southwest by rail from  |Padua|Padua| . Abano Termes population is 19,062 .     The towns hot springs and mud baths are an important economic resource. The waters have a temperature of about .       The baths were known to the Romans as  Aponi fons or Aquae Patavinae. A description of them is given in a letter to  |Theodoric|Theodoric_The_Great| , the king of the  |Ostrogoths|Ostrogoths| , from  |Cassiodorus|Cassiodorus| . Some remains of the ancient baths have been discovered . An oracle of  |Geryon|Geryon|  lay near, and the so-called sortes Praenestinae , small bronze cylinders inscribed, and used as oracles, were perhaps found here in the 16th century.      The baths were destroyed by the Lombards in the 6th century, but they were rebuilt and enlarged when Abano became an autonomous  |comune|Medieval_Commune|  in the 12th century and, again, in the late 14th century. The city was under the  |Republic_of_Venice|Republic_Of_Venice|  from 1405 to 1797.        |Abano_Cathedral|Abano_Cathedral| , or the cathedral  of St. Lawrence. The Sanctuary of the Madonna della Salute or of Monteortone . It lies on the site where the Madonna appeared to Pietro Falco, healing his wounds.
