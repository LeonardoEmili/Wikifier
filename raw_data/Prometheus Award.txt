   The Prometheus Award is an award for  |libertarian_science_fiction|Libertarian_Science_Fiction|  novels given annually by the Libertarian Futurist Society.  |L._Neil_Smith|L._Neil_Smith|  established the award in 1979, but it was not awarded regularly until the newly founded Libertarian Futurist Society revived it in 1982. The Society created a Hall of Fame Award in 1983, and also presents occasional one-off Special Awards.         Some authors have won the award for best novel more than once:    |Cory_Doctorow|Cory_Doctorow| |Victor_Koman|Victor_Koman| |Ken_MacLeod|Ken_Macleod| |L._Neil_Smith|L._Neil_Smith| |Travis_J_I_Corcoran|Travis_J_I_Corcoran| |James_P._Hogan|James_P._Hogan| |Neal_Stephenson|Neal_Stephenson| |Vernor_Vinge|Vernor_Vinge| |F._Paul_Wilson|F._Paul_Wilson|      A few authors have won the Prometheus Hall of Fame award more than once:    |Poul_Anderson|Poul_Anderson| |Robert_Heinlein|Robert_Heinlein| |George_Orwell|George_Orwell| |Ayn_Rand|Ayn_Rand| |F._Paul_Wilson|F._Paul_Wilson|          Books published in a given year are eligible .     All members may nominate novels for the award.   Members of the Best Novel Committee read at least 10-12 of the nominated novels and vote for a slate of 5 finalists.   Full members then vote on the finalists.     Step 2 happens in the first few months of the following year.     Step 3 happens in early summer of the following year.     The awards are given at the Annual  |Worldcon|Worldcon| .
