                The bass guitar  is a  |plucked_string_instrument|Plucked_String_Instrument|  similar in appearance and construction to an  |electric|Electric_Guitar|  or an  |acoustic_guitar|Acoustic_Guitar| , except with a longer  |neck|Neck|  and  |scale_length|Scale_Length| , and typically four to six  |strings|String|  or  |courses|Course| . Since the 1960s, the bass guitar has largely replaced the  |double_bass|Double_Bass|  in  |popular_music|Popular_Music| .     The four-string bass is usually tuned the same as the  |double_bass|Double_Bass| , which corresponds to pitches one  |octave|Octave|  lower than the four lowest-pitched strings of a guitar . It is played primarily with the fingers or thumb, or  |striking|Guitar_Pick|  with a  |pick|Plectrum| . The electric bass guitar has  |pickups|Pickup|  and must be connected to an  |amplifier_and_speaker|Bass_Amplifier|  to be loud enough to compete with other instruments.       According to the New Grove Dictionary of Music and Musicians, an Electric bass guitar a Guitar, usually with four heavy strings tuned E 1 –A 1 –D 2 –G 2 .  It also defines bass as Bass . A contraction of Double bass or Electric bass guitar. According to some authors the proper term is electric bass . Common names for the instrument are bass guitar , electric bass guitar , and electric bass  and some authors claim that they are historically accurate.
