         Nancy Davis Reagan  was an American film actress and the wife of  |Ronald_Reagan|Ronald_Reagan| , the 40th  |president_of_the_United_States|President_Of_The_United_States| . She was the  |first_lady_of_the_United_States|First_Lady_Of_The_United_States|  from 1981 to 1989.     She was born in New York City. After her parents separated, she lived in  |Maryland|Maryland|  with an aunt and uncle for six years. When her mother remarried in 1929, she moved to Chicago and later took the name Davis from her stepfather. As Nancy Davis, she was a Hollywood actress in the 1940s and 1950s, starring in films such as  The Next Voice You Hear... ,  Night into Morning , and  Donovans Brain . In 1952, she married Ronald Reagan, who was then president of the  |Screen_Actors_Guild|Screen_Actors_Guild| . They had two children together. Reagan was the first lady of California when her husband was  |governor|Governor_Of_California|  from 1967 to 1975, and she began to work with the  |Foster_Grandparents_Program|Foster_Grandparents_Program| .     Reagan became First Lady of the United States in January 1981, following her husbands victory in the  |1980_presidential_election|1980_United_States_Presidential_Election| . Early in his first term, she was criticized largely due to her decision to replace the  |White_House_china|White_House_China| , which had been paid for by private donations. Following years of lax formality, she decided to restore a  |Kennedyesque|Kennedyesque|  glamour to the White House, and her interest in high-end fashion garnered much attention as well as criticism. She championed  |recreational_drug_prevention|Recreational_Drug_Use|  causes when she founded the  |Just_Say_No|Just_Say_No|  drug awareness campaign, which was considered her major initiative as First Lady. More discussion of her role ensued following a 1988 revelation that she had consulted an  |astrologer|Astrology|  to assist in planning the presidents schedule after the  |attempted_assassination_of_her_husband_in_1981|Attempted_Assassination_Of_Ronald_Reagan| . She generally had a strong influence on her husband and played a role in a few of his personnel and diplomatic decisions.     After Ronald Reagans term as president ended, the couple returned to their home in  |Bel_Air,_Los_Angeles|Bel_Air,_Los_Angeles| ,  |California|California| . Nancy devoted most of her time to caring for her husband, who was diagnosed with  |Alzheimers_disease|Alzheimers_Disease|  in 1994, until  |his_death|Death_And_State_Funeral_Of_Ronald_Reagan|  at the age of 93 on June 5, 2004. Reagan remained active within the  |Reagan_Library|Ronald_Reagan_Presidential_Library|  and in politics, particularly in support of  |embryonic_stem_cell_research|Embryonic_Stem_Cell_Research| , until her death from  |congestive_heart_failure|Congestive_Heart_Failure|  at age 94 on March 6, 2016. When Nancy Davis signed with MGM, she gave her birth date as July 6, 1923, shaving two years off her age, a common practice in Hollywood . This caused subsequent confusion as some sources would continue to use the incorrect birth year.  Some sources and websites erroneously list her as either being born in Flushing or being raised in Manhattan.
