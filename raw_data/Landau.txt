      Landau, or Landau in der Pfalz, is an autonomous  town surrounded by the  |Südliche_Weinstraße|Südliche_Weinstraße|  district of southern  |Rhineland-Palatinate|Rhineland-Palatinate| ,  |Germany|Germany| . It is a university town , a long-standing cultural centre, and a market and shopping town, surrounded by vineyards and wine-growing villages of the  |Palatinate_wine_region|Palatinate| . Landau lies east of the  |Palatinate_forest|Palatinate_Forest| , Europes largest contiguous forest, on the  |German_Wine_Route|German_Wine_Route| .     It contains the districts  of Arzheim, Dammheim, Godramstein, Mörlheim, Mörzheim, Nussdorf, Queichheim, and Wollmesheim.       Landau was first mentioned as a settlement in 1106. It was in the possession of the counts of Leiningen-Dagsburg-Landeck, whose arms, differenced by an  |escutcheon|Escutcheon|  of the Imperial eagle, served as the arms of Landau until 1955 . The town was granted a charter in 1274 by King  |Rudolf_I|Rudolph_I_Of_Germany|  of  |Germany|Kingdom_Of_Germany| , who declared the town a  |Free_Imperial_Town|Free_Imperial_Town|  in 1291; nevertheless Prince-Bishop Emich of  |Speyer|Prince-Bishopric_Of_Speyer| , a major landowner in the district, seized the town in 1324. The town did not regain its ancient rights until 1511 from  |Maximilian_I|Maximilian_I,_Holy_Roman_Emperor| . An  |Augustinian|Augustinians|  monastery was founded in 1276.     Landau was later part of  |France|France|  from 1680 to 1815, during which it was one of the  Décapole , the ten free cities of  |Alsace|Alsace| , and received its modern fortifications by  |Louis_XIVs|Louis_Xiv|  military architect  |Vauban|Vauban|  in 1688–99, making the little town one of Europes strongest citadels. In the  |War_of_the_Spanish_Succession|War_Of_The_Spanish_Succession|  it had four Sieges. After the  |Siege|Siege_Of_Landau|  in 1702 that was lost by the French, an Imperial garrison was installed in Landau. After the  |2nd_Siege|Siege_Of_Landau|  from 13 October to 15 November 1703 the French had regained the city, caused by their victory in the  |Battle_of_Speyerbach|Battle_Of_Speyerbach| .  The  |3rd_Siege|Siege_Of_Landau|  began on 12 September 1704 by  |Louis,_Margrave_of_Baden-Baden|Louis_William,_Margrave_Of_Baden-Baden| , and ended on 23 November 1704 with the French defeat.  During this siege  |King_Joseph_I|Joseph_I,_Holy_Roman_Emperor|  arrived at Landau coming from Vienna in a newly developed convertible carriage. It became very popular, named  |Landau|Landau|  in English, or  |Landauer|:De:Landauer|  in German. The French got Landau back after the  |4th_Siege|Siege_Of_Landau|  which lasted from 6 June to 20 August 1713 by Marshal General  |Villars|Claude_Louis_Hector_De_Villars| .      Landau was part of  |Bas-Rhin|Bas-Rhin|  department between 1789 and 1815. After  |Napoleons|Napoleon| |Hundred_Days|Hundred_Days|  following his escape from Elba, Landau, which had remained French, was granted to the  |Kingdom_of_Bavaria|Kingdom_Of_Bavaria|  in 1815 and became the capital of one of the thirteen Bezirksämter of the Bavarian  |Rheinkreis|Palatinate| , later renamed Pfalz.     In 1840 famous political cartoonist  |Thomas_Nast|Thomas_Nast|  was born in Landau.     Following  |World_War_II|World_War_Ii| , Landau was an important barracks town for the  |French_occupation|History_Of_Germany_Since_1945| .          Landaus large main square  is dominated by the town hall  and the market hall . in the 19th-century, the former fortifications gave way to a ring road that encircles the old town centre, from which the old industrial buildings have been excluded. A convention hall, the Festhalle, was built in  |Art_Nouveau|Art_Nouveau|  style, 1905–07 on a rise overlooking the town park and facing the modernist Bundesamt, the regional government building.   The Protestant  |Collegiate_Church|Collegiate_Church|   in Landau in der Pfalz is one of the oldest buildings in the town. With the construction of the church started in the 14th century, was completed in the mid 16th Century.   The zoo is located close to the center of Landau alongside the historical fortifications. Animals are held in natural enclosures. The zoo contains numerous exotic species such as tigers and cheetahs, but also seals, penguins, kangaroos and flamingos and many more.       Wine-making continues to be an important industry of Landau.       The  |landau|Landau| , a luxury open carriage with a pair of folding tops, was invented in the town.
