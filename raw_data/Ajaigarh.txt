      Ajaigarh or Adjygurh is a town and a  |nagar_panchayat|Nagar_Panchayat|  in the  |Panna_District|Panna_District|  of  |Madhya_Pradesh|Madhya_Pradesh|  state in central  |India|India| .             Ajaigarh was the capital of a  |princely_state|Princely_State|  of the same name during the  |British_Raj|British_Raj| . Ajaigarh was founded in 1765 by Guman Singh, a  |bundela|Bundela| |_Rajput|Rajput|  who was the nephew of Raja Pahar Singh of Jaitpur. After Ajaigarh was captured by the British in 1809, it became a princely state in the  |Bundelkhand_Agency|Bundelkhand_Agency|  of the  |Central_India_Agency|Central_India_Agency| . It had an area of , and a population of 78,236 in 1901. The rulers bore the title of sawai maharaja. He commanded an estimated annual revenue of about £15,000/-, and paid a tribute of £460/-. The chief resided at the town of  |Nowgong|Nowgong_Chhatarpur| , at the foot of the hill-fortress of Ajaigarh, from which the state took its name. This fort, situated on a steep hill, towers more than 800  ft above the eponymous township, and contains the ruins of several temples adorned with elaborately carved sculptures. The town was often afflicted by  |malaria|Malaria| , and suffered severely from famine in 1868–1869 and 1896–1897.     The state acceded to the  |Government_of_India|Government_Of_India|  on 1 January 1950; the ruling chief was granted a privy purse of Rs. 74,700/-, and the courtesy use of his styles and titles. All of these were revoked by the government of  |India|India|  in 1971, at the time when these privileges were revoked from all erstwhile princes. The former princely state became part of the new Indian state of  |Vindhya_Pradesh|Vindhya_Pradesh| , and most of the territory of the former state, including the town of Ajaigarh, became part of Panna District, with a smaller portion going to  |Chhatarpur_District|Chhatarpur_District| . Vindhya Pradesh was merged into  |Madhya_Pradesh|Madhya_Pradesh|  on 1 November 1956.                   |Maharajadhiraja_Chhatrasal|Maharaja_Chhatrasal| |_shramanas|Shramanas| |_census|Census| ,  Ajaigarh had a population of 13,979. Males constitute 53% of the population and females 47%. Ajaigarh has an average literacy rate of 59%, which is lower than the national average of 59.5%; with 61% of the males and 39% of females literate. 16% of the population is under 6 years of age.
