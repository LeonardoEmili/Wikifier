            Eithne Pádraigín Ní Bhraonáin , known professionally as Enya, is an Irish singer, songwriter, record producer and musician. Born into a musical family and raised in the Irish-speaking area of  |Gweedore|Gweedore|  in  |County_Donegal|County_Donegal| , Enya began her music career when she joined her familys  |Celtic|Celtic_Music|  band  |Clannad|Clannad|  in 1980 on keyboards and backing vocals. She left in 1982 with their manager and producer  |Nicky_Ryan|Nicky_Ryan|  to pursue a solo career, with Ryans wife  |Roma_Ryan|Roma_Ryan|  as her lyricist. Enya developed her sound over the following four years with multitracked vocals and keyboards with elements of  |new_age|New_Age_Music| , Celtic, classical, church, and folk music. She has sung in ten languages.     Enyas first projects as a solo artist included soundtrack work for  The Frog Prince  and the 1987  |BBC|Bbc|  documentary series  The Celts , which was released as her debut album,  Enya  . She signed with  |Warner_Music_UK|Warner_Music_Group| , which granted her artistic freedom and minimal interference from the label. The commercial and critical success of  Watermark  propelled her to worldwide fame, helped by the international top-10 hit single  |Orinoco_Flow|Orinoco_Flow|  . This was followed by the multi-million selling albums  Shepherd Moons  ,  The Memory of Trees  and  A Day Without Rain  . Sales of the latter and its lead single,  |Only_Time|Only_Time|  , surged in the United States following its use in the media coverage of the  |September_11_attacks|September_11_Attacks| . Following  Amarantine  and  And Winter Came...  , Enya took an extended break from music; she returned in 2012 and released  Dark Sky Island  .     Enya is known for her privacy and has never undertaken a concert tour. She is Irelands bestselling solo artist  and second-bestselling artist behind  |U2|U2| , with a  |discography|Enya_Discography|  that has sold 26.5  million  |certified_albums|Recording_Industry_Association_Of_America|  in the United States  and an estimated 75 million records worldwide, making her one of the  |best_selling_music_artists_of_all_time|List_Of_Best_Selling_Music_Artists| .  A Day Without Rain remains the bestselling new-age album, with an estimated 16 million copies sold worldwide.  Enya has won awards including seven  |World_Music_Awards|World_Music_Award| , four  |Grammy_Awards|Grammy_Award|  for  |Best_New_Age_Album|Grammy_Award_For_Best_New_Age_Album| ,  and an  |Ivor_Novello_Award|Ivor_Novello_Award| . She was nominated for an  |Academy_Award|Academy_Awards|  and a  |Golden_Globe_Award|Golden_Globe_Award|  for  |May_It_Be|May_It_Be|  , written for  The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring  .          Eithne Pádraigín Ní Bhraonáin was born on 17 May 1961 in Dore, within the area of the parish  |Gaoth_Dobhair|Gaoth_Dobhair| , in the northwestern county of  |Donegal|County_Donegal| , Ireland.  It is a  |Gaeltacht|Gaeltacht|  region where  |Irish|Irish_Language|  is the primary language. Her name is  |anglicised|Anglicisation|  as Enya Patricia Brennan,  where Enya is the phonetic spelling of how Eithne is pronounced in her native  |Ulster_dialect_of_Irish|Ulster_Irish| ; Ní Bhraonáin translates to daughter of Brennan .  The fifth of nine children, Enya was born into a Roman Catholic  |family_of_musicians|Clannadthe_Brennan_Family| .  Her father, Leo Brennan, was the leader of the Slieve Foy Band, an  |Irish_showband|Irish_Showband| , and ran  |Leos_Tavern|Leos_Tavern|  in Meenaleck; her mother, Máire Brennan , who had distant Spanish roots and whose ancestors settled on  |Tory_Island|Tory_Island| , was an amateur musician who played in Leos band  and taught music at  |Gweedore_Community_School|Pobalscoil_Ghaoth_Dobhair| .  Enyas maternal grandfather Aodh was the headmaster of the primary school in Dore, and her grandmother was a teacher there. Aodh was also the founder of the  |Gweedore_Theatre|Amharclann_Ghaoth_Dobhair|  company.      Enya described her upbringing as very quiet and happy. At age three, she took part in her first singing competition at the annual  |Feis_Ceoil|Feis_Ceoil|  music festival. She took part in pantomimes at Gweedore Theatre and sang with her siblings in her mothers choir at St Marys church in  |Derrybeg|Derrybeg| . She learned English at primary school and began piano lessons at age four. I had to do school work and then travel to a neighbouring town for piano lessons, and then more school work. I ... remember my brothers and sisters playing outside ... and I would be inside playing the piano. This one big book of  |scales|Scale| , practising them over and over.  At eleven, Enyas grandfather paid for her education at a strict  |convent|Convent|  boarding school in  |Milford|Milford_County_Donegal|   run by nuns of the  |Loreto_order|Sisters_Of_Loreto| , where she developed a taste for classical music, art,  |Latin|Latin|  and  |watercolour|Watercolour|  painting. It was devastating to be torn away from such a large family, but it was good for my music. Enya left the school at 17 and studied classical music in college for one year with the aim of becoming a piano teacher sort of person. I never thought of myself composing or being on stage.
