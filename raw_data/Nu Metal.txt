Nu metal became popular in the late 1990s with bands and artists such as  |Korn|Korn| ,  |Limp_Bizkit|Limp_Bizkit| , and  |Kid_Rock|Kid_Rock|  all releasing albums that sold millions of copies. Nu metals popularity continued during the early 2000s, with bands such as  |Papa_Roach|Papa_Roach| ,  |Staind|Staind| , and  |P.O.D.|P.O.D.|  all selling multi-platinum albums, and came to a peak with  |Linkin_Parks|Linkin_Park|  diamond-selling album  Hybrid Theory . However, by the  the oversaturation of bands combined with the under-performance of a number of  releases led to nu metals decline, leading to the rise of  |metalcore|Metalcore|  and many nu metal bands disbanding or abandoning their established sound in favor of other genres.     During the 2010s, there was a nu metal revival; many bands that combine nu metal with other genres  emerged and some nu metal bands from the 1990s and early 2000s returned to the nu metal sound. Bands like Staind, Linkin Park and Papa Roach went back to making nu metal songs after abandoning the genre. Bands like  |Of_Mice_&_Men|Of_Mice_&_Men| ,  |Emmure|Emmure| ,  |Issues|Issues|  and  |My_Ticket_Home|My_Ticket_Home|  would combine nu metal with metalcore or deathcore. In the 2010s, nu metal bands like  |From_Ashes_to_New|From_Ashes_To_New| ,  |Islander|Islander|  and  |Ded|Ded|  emerged. Nu metal received criticism from a lot of fans of heavy metal and nu metal was often labelled with pejorative words like mallcore . Some fans of heavy metal did not consider nu metal a subgenre of heavy metal. Some nu metal musicians did not view their own music as heavy metal and some nu metal musicians rejected the nu metal label.                Nu metal is also known as nü-metal  and aggro-metal.   It is a subgenre of  |alternative_metal|Alternative_Metal| .   |MTV|Mtv|  states that the early nu metal group  |Korn|Korn|  arrived in 1993 into the burgeoning alternative metal scene, which would morph into nü-metal the way  |college_rock|College_Rock|  became alternative rock. Nu metal merges elements of heavy metal music   with elements of other music genres such as  |grunge|Grunge| ,  |hip_hop|Hip_Hop_Music| ,  and alternative rock. Lyrics in nu metal songs are often angry or nihilistic; many of the genres lyrics focus on topics such as pain, angst, bullying, emotional issues, abandonment, betrayal, and personal alienation, in a way similar to those of grunge.   A lot of nu metal lyrics that are about these topics tend to be in a very direct tone. However, some  songs have lyrics that are about other topics.  |P.O.D.|P.O.D.|  have used positive lyrics about promise and hope. Oddly, audiences took frontman  |Fred_Durst|Fred_Durst|  more seriously than he wanted, failing to see the intentional silliness in many of his songs . Limp Bizkits lyrics also have been described as misogynistic.  |Dopes|Dope|  lyrics are usually about sex, drugs, parties, women, violence and relationships.  According to Josh Chesler of the  Phoenix New Times , the lyrics of  |Deftones|Deftones| , who were once a nu metal band, tend to have complex allusions and leave the songs open to many different interpretations. The aggressive riffs of Korn, the rapping of Limp Bizkit, and the melodic ballads of Staind created the sonic template for nu metal. The origins of the term nu metal are often attributed to the work of producer  |Ross_Robinson|Ross_Robinson| , who has been called The Godfather of Nu Metal between producers. Robinson has produced for nu metal bands such as Korn,   Limp Bizkit  and Slipknot. In 1994, Korn released  |their_self-titled_debut_album|Korn| , which is widely considered the first nu metal album.   Korn had experienced underground popularity at this time; their debut album peaked at number 72 on the  |Billboard_200|Billboard_200| . In the same year, P.O.D.s album  Snuff the Punk  was also released, which was later recognized as another early example of nu metal.  In 1995, the band  |Sugar_Ray|Sugar_Ray|  released its debut studio album  Lemonade and Brownies , an album described as nu metal.  In 1995, Deftones released their debut album  Adrenaline . The album peaked at number 23 on the  |Heatseekers_Albums|Heatseekers_Albums|  chart on October 5, 1996.  Deftones also were temporarily controversial in 1996 when their vocalist  |Chino_Moreno|Chino_Moreno|  was blamed by TV news reports for a riot that occurred at the 1996 U-Fest festival.
