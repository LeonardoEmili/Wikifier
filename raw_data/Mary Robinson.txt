              Mary Therese Winifred Robinson  is an Irish  |Independent|Independent_Politician|  politician who served as the  |seventh_President_of_Ireland|List_Of_Presidents_Of_Ireland|  from December 1990 to September 1997, becoming the first woman to hold this office. She also served as  |United_Nations_High_Commissioner_for_Human_Rights|Office_Of_The_United_Nations_High_Commissioner_For_Human_Rights|  from 1997 to 2002 and a  |Senator|Seanad_Éireann|  for the  |University_of_Dublin|University_Of_Dublin|  from 1969 to 1989. She first rose to prominence as an academic,  |barrister|Barrister|  and campaigner. She defeated  |Fianna_Fáils|Fianna_Fáil| |Brian_Lenihan|Brian_Lenihan_Snr|  and  |Fine_Gaels|Fine_Gael| |Austin_Currie|Austin_Currie|  in the  |1990_presidential_election|1990_Irish_Presidential_Election| , becoming the first Independent candidate nominated by the  |Labour_Party|Labour_Party| , the  |Workers_Party|Workers_Party_Of_Ireland|  and Independent Senators. She was the first elected President in the offices history not to have had the support of  |Fianna_Fáil|Fianna_Fáil| .     She is widely regarded as a transformative figure for Ireland, and for the Irish presidency, revitalising and liberalising a previously conservative, low-profile political office. She resigned the presidency two months ahead of the end of her term of office to take up her post in the  |United_Nations|United_Nations| . During her UN tenure she visited  |Tibet|Tibet|  , the first High Commissioner to do so; she criticised Irelands immigrant policy; and criticised the use of  |capital_punishment_in_the_United_States|Capital_Punishment_In_The_United_States| . She extended her intended single four-year term by a year to preside over the  |World_Conference_against_Racism_2001|World_Conference_Against_Racism_2001|  in  |Durban|Durban| ,  |South_Africa|South_Africa| ; the conference proved controversial. Under continuing pressure from the United States, Robinson resigned her post in September 2002.
