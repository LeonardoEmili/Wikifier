     Ruslan Imranovich Khasbulatov  is a  |Russia|Russia| n  |economist|Economist|  and  |politician|Politician|  and the Chairman of Parliament of Russia of  |Chechen|Chechen_People|  descent who played a central role in the events leading to the  |1993_constitutional_crisis|Russian_Constitutional_Crisis_Of_1993|  in the  |Russian_Federation|Russian_Federation| .       Khasbulatov was born in Tolstoy-yurt, a village near  |Grozny|Grozny| , the capital of  |Chechnya|Chechnya| , on November 22, 1942. In February 1944, he was deported to  |Central_Asia|Central_Asia|  during the  |Chechen_deportations|Deportation_Of_The_Chechens_And_Ingush| .     After studying in  |Almaty|Almaty| , Khasbulatov moved to  |Moscow|Moscow|  in 1962, where he studied law at the prestigious  |Moscow_State_University|Moscow_State_University| . After graduating in 1966, he joined the  |Communist_Party_of_the_Soviet_Union|Communist_Party_Of_The_Soviet_Union| . He continued his studies, focusing on the political, social and economic development of capitalist countries, and received several higher degrees between 1970 and 1980. During the 1970s and 1980s, he published a number of books on international economics and trade.       In the late 1980s, Khasbulatov began to work closely with rising maverick in the Communist Party  |Boris_Yeltsin|Boris_Yeltsin| . He was elected to the  |Congress_of_Peoples_Deputies_of_the_Russian_SFSR|Congress_Of_Peoples_Deputies_Of_Russia|  in 1990. He followed Yeltsin in the successful resistance to the  |putsch_attempt|Soviet_Coup_Attempt_Of_1991|  in 1991. He quit the Communist Party in August 1991, and on 29 October 1991 he was elected speaker of the  |Supreme_Soviet_of_RSFSR|Supreme_Soviet_Of_Russia| .       Khasbulatov had been an ally of Yeltsin in this period, and played a key role in leading the resistance to the  |1991_coup_attempt|1991_Soviet_Coup_Detat_Attempt| . However, he and Yeltsin drifted apart following the collapse of the  |Soviet_Union|Soviet_Union|  at the end of 1991.     After the collapse of the USSR, Khasbulatov consolidated his control over the Russian parliament and became the second most powerful man in Russia after Yeltsin himself. Among other factors, the escalating clash of egos between Khasbulatov and Yeltsin led to the  |Russian_constitutional_crisis_of_1993|Russian_Constitutional_Crisis_Of_1993| , in which Khasbulatov  led the Supreme Soviet of Russia in its power struggle with the president, which ended with Yeltsins violent assault on and subsequent dissolution of the parliament in October 1993.     Khasbulatov was arrested along with the other leaders of the parliament. In 1994, the newly elected  |Duma|State_Duma|  pardoned him along with other key leaders of the anti-Yeltsin resistance.       Following the end of his political career, Khasbulatov returned to his earlier profession as a teacher of  |economics|Economics|  as founder and head of the Department of International Economy at the  |Plekhanov_Russian_Academy_of_Economics|Plekhanov_Russian_Academy_Of_Economics|  . He continues to comment on political developments in Russia.
