     Carl Rolf Ekéus  is a  |Swedish|Sweden| |diplomat|Diplomat| . From 1978 to 1983, he was a representative to the  |Conference_on_Disarmament|Conference_On_Disarmament|  in  |Geneva|Geneva| , and he has worked on various other  |disarmament|Disarmament|  committees and commissions.     Between 1991 and 1997 he was director of the  |United_Nations_Special_Commission_on_Iraq|United_Nations_Special_Commission_On_Iraq| , the  |United_Nations|United_Nations| |disarmament|Disarmament|  observers in  |Iraq|Iraq|  after the  |Gulf_War|Gulf_War| . In late July 2002 he reportedly said in the  |Svenska_Dagbladet|Svenska_Dagbladet|  newspaper that during his time in this position he attempted to resist attempts by the  |United_States|United_States|  to use the Commission to perform  |espionage|Espionage| . His successor as director was  |Richard_Butler|Richard_Butler| . Iraq suspended the inspections in 1998 after claiming that it was a cover for espionage.     Journalist  |Andrew_Cockburn|Andrew_Cockburn|  reported in Britains The First Post that Ekéus told him how former  |U.S.|United_States|  President  |Bill_Clinton|Bill_Clinton|  attempted to prevent  |Saddam_Husseins|Saddam_Hussein| |Iraq|Iraq|  from being certified as free of  |weapons_of_mass_destruction|Weapons_Of_Mass_Destruction| . Despite Ekéus belief that Iraq was nearly certifiable as being free of such weapons, U.S. Secretary of State  |Madeleine_Albright|Madeleine_Albright|  announced that  |United_Nations|United_Nations|  sanctions would not be lifted until such time as Hussein was no longer in power. The Iraqi government responded by ending its previous cooperation with the U.N. weapons inspectors.      Ekéus later became Swedens ambassador to the  |United_States|United_States|  and the chairman of the board of the  |Stockholm_International_Peace_Research_Institute|Stockholm_International_Peace_Research_Institute| .     According to the journalist  |Christopher_Hitchens|Christopher_Hitchens| , Ekéus told me that hed been offered by  |Tariq_Aziz|Tariq_Aziz|  in person, to his face, a  |bribe|Bribe|  of a million and a half dollars to change his inspection report. That was going on throughout the entire process. Rolf wouldnt, of course, agree to take it, but if they were asking him, it means they were asking everybody.    The story has also been covered by  |The_Telegraph|The_Daily_Telegraph| .      In January 2000, Ekéus was nominated to head the UN Monitoring, Verification and Inspections Commission , charged with investigating allegations that  |Iraq|Iraq|  possessed  |weapons_of_mass_destruction|Weapons_Of_Mass_Destruction| . But Ekéus name failed to receive the approval of the  |UN_Security_Council|Un_Security_Council| , due to the opposition of  |France|France| ,  |Russia|Russia|  and  |China|China| , and so  |Hans_Blix|Hans_Blix|  was appointed instead.     Ekéus was  |High_Commissioner_on_National_Minorities|High_Commissioner_On_National_Minorities|  at the  |OSCE|Organization_For_Security_And_Co-Operation_In_Europe|  from 2001 till 2007, as well as on the board of directors for the  |Nuclear_Threat_Initiative|Nuclear_Threat_Initiative|  . Since 2005, Ekéus has been a Commissioner of the  |International_Commission_on_Missing_Persons|International_Commission_On_Missing_Persons| . He is also Member of the Supervisory Council of the  |International_Luxembourg_Forum_on_Preventing_Nuclear_Catastrophe|International_Luxembourg_Forum_On_Preventing_Nuclear_Catastrophe| , a not-for-profit organization uniting leading experts on  |non-proliferation|Non-Proliferation|  of  |nuclear_weapon|Nuclear_Weapon|.
