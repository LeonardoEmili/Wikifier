         A raven is one of several larger-bodied species of the  |genus|Genus|   Corvus . These species do not form a single  |taxonomic_group|Taxon|  within the genus.     There is no consistent distinction between  |crows|Crow|  and ravens , and these appellations have been assigned to different species chiefly on the basis of their size, crows generally being smaller than ravens.     The largest raven species are the  |common_raven|Common_Raven|  and the  |thick-billed_raven|Thick-Billed_Raven| .       The term raven originally referred to the  |common_raven|Common_Raven|  , the type species of the genus Corvus, which has a larger distribution than any other species of Corvus, ranging over much of the  |Northern_Hemisphere|Northern_Hemisphere| .     The modern English word raven has cognates in all other  |Germanic_languages|Germanic_Languages| , including  |Old_Norse|Old_Norse|   hrafn Oxford English Dictionary entry for raven. and  |Old_High_German|Old_High_German|  raban,  all of which descend from  |Proto-Germanic|Proto-Germanic|  hrabanaz.       |Collective_nouns|Collective_Noun|  for a group of ravens include unkindness , treachery ,  and conspiracy .  In practice, most people use the more generic flock .
