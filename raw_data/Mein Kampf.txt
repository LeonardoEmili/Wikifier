                  Mein Kampf  is a 1925  |autobiographical_manifesto|Autobiographical_Manifesto|  by  |Nazi_Party|Nazi_Party|  leader  |Adolf_Hitler|Adolf_Hitler| . The work describes the process by which Hitler became  |antisemitic|Antisemitism|  and outlines his  |political_ideology_and_future_plans|Political_Views_Of_Adolf_Hitler|  for Germany. Volume 1 of Mein Kampf was published in 1925 and Volume 2 in 1926. Waite, The Psychopathic God: Adolf Hitler, Basic Books, 1977, pp.237–243     Hitler began Mein Kampf while imprisoned for what he considered to be political crimes following  |his_failed_Putsch_in_Munich|Beer_Hall_Putsch|  in November 1923. Although Hitler received many visitors initially, he soon devoted himself entirely to the book. As he continued, Hitler realized that it would have to be a two-volume work, with the first volume scheduled for release in early 1925. The governor of  |Landsberg|Landsberg_Prison|  noted at the time that he hopes the book will run into many editions, thus enabling him to fulfill his financial obligations and to defray the expenses incurred at the time of his trial.   After slow initial sales, the book was a bestseller in Germany after  |Hitlers_rise_to_power|Adolf_Hitlers_Rise_To_Power|  in 1933.     After Hitlers death, copyright of Mein Kampf passed to the state government of  |Bavaria|Bavaria| , which refused to allow any copying or printing of the book in Germany. In 2016, following the expiration of the copyright held by the Bavarian state government, Mein Kampf was republished in Germany for the first time since 1945, which prompted public debate and divided reactions from Jewish groups. He speaks of not having met a  |Jew|Jew|  until he arrived in Vienna, and that at first his attitude was liberal and tolerant. Mein Kampf has also been studied as a work on  |political_theory|Political_Theory| . For example, Hitler announces his hatred of what he believed to be the worlds two evils:  |Communism|Communism|  and  |Judaism|Judaism| .     In the book Hitler blamed Germanys chief woes on the  |parliament|Reichstag|  of the  |Weimar_Republic|Weimar_Republic| , the Jews, and  |Social_Democrats|Social_Democratic_Party_Of_Germany| , as well as  |Marxists|Marxism| , though he believed that Marxists, Social Democrats, and the parliament were all working for Jewish interests.  He announced that he wanted to completely destroy the  |parliamentary_system|Parliamentary_System| , believing it to be corrupt in principle, as those who reach power are inherent  |opportunists|Opportunism| .
