        Politics of Mauritius  takes place in a framework of a  |parliamentary|Parliamentary_System| |democracy|Representative_Democracy| . The separation of powers is among the three branches of the  |Government_of_Mauritius|Government_Of_Mauritius| , namely the  |legislative|Legislative_Power| , the  |executive|Executive_Power|  and the  |Judiciary|Judiciary_Of_Mauritius| , is embedded in the  |Constitution_of_Mauritius|Constitution_Of_Mauritius| .     Being a  |Westminster_system|Westminster_System|  of government, Mauritiuss  |unicameral|Unicameral|  house of parliament officially, the  |National_Assembly|National_Assembly_Of_Mauritius| , is supreme. It elects the President and the Prime Minister. While the President is voted by a single majority of votes in the house, the Prime Minister is the MP who supports a majority in the house.     The  |President|President_Of_Mauritius|  is the  |Head_of_State|Head_Of_State|  while the  |Prime_Minister|Prime_Minister_Of_Mauritius|  has full  |executive_power|Executive_Power|  and is the  |Head_of_Government|Head_Of_Government|  who is assisted by a  |council_of_Ministers|Cabinet_Of_Ministers_Of_Mauritius| . Mauritius has a  |multi-party_system|Multi-Party_System| .      Historically, Mauritiuss government has been led by the  |Labour_Party|Labour_Party|  or the  |MSM|Militant_Socialist_Movement|  for the exception of short periods from 1982–1983 and 2003–2005 where the  |MMM|Mauritian_Militant_Movement|  was at the head of the country. LAlliance Lepep, a coalition of several political parties including MSM, Muvmen Liberater and PMSD, won the 2014 elections.  Two years into the political term, the PMSD announced their resignation from the coalition government on 19 December 2016, and joined the ranks of the opposition.   On January 23, 2017, the then Prime Minister, 86 year old Sir Aneerood Jugnauth,  a key political figure in Mauritian politics who has previously served several political terms spanning over many decades, announced that he was stepping down as Prime Minister.  Following the Westminster tradition, the leader of the governing party in the coalition government, Pravind Jugnauth who is Sir Aneerood Jugnauths son and then Minister of Finance, was sworn in as Prime Minister. Although Sir Aneerood Jugnauth resigned as Prime Minister, he is still serving as Minister Mentor, Minister of Defence, Minister for Rodrigues.            The president and vice president are elected by the National Assembly for five-year terms. They form part along with the Speaker of the National Assembly, the legislative offices which under the constitution have the final decision and last word on any legislative matter including the laws of Mauritius. Most of the work is executed by the Executive Branch which consists of the  |Cabinet_of_Ministers|Cabinet_Of_Ministers_Of_Mauritius| ,  |Leader_of_the_Opposition|Leader_Of_The_Opposition|  and also other members of the parliament.       Another important structure of the government of Mauritius is the executive branch . The Prime Minister is appointed by the president and is responsible to the National Assembly. The  |Council_of_Ministers|Cabinet_Of_Ministers_Of_Mauritius|  is appointed by the president on the recommendation of the prime minister. The Council of Ministers , responsible for the direction and control of the government, consists of the prime minister , the leader of the majority party in the legislature, and about 24 ministers including one Deputy Prime Minister and/or one Vice Prime Minister.     The Executive branch being with the Cabinet have 4 most powerful executive offices, Prime Minister, Deputy Prime Minister and 2 offices of Vice Prime Minister.
