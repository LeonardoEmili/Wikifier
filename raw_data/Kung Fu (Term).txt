            In general, kung fu/kungfu or gung fu/gongfu  refers to the  |Chinese_martial_arts|Chinese_Martial_Arts| , also called  |wushu|Wushu|  and  |quanfa|Quanfa| . In China, it refers to any study, learning, or practice that requires patience, energy, and time to complete. In its original meaning, kung fu can refer to any  |discipline|Discipline|  or  |skill|Skill|  achieved through hard work and practice, not necessarily  |martial_arts|Martial_Arts| . There are many forms of kung fu, namely  |Shaolin_Kung_Fu|Shaolin_Kung_Fu| ,  |Wing_Chun|Wing_Chun| ,  |Tai_chi|Tai_Chi| , etc. and are practiced all over the world. Each form of kung fu has its own principles and techniques, but is best known for its trickery and quickness, which is where the word Kung Fu is derived. It is only in the late twentieth century, that this term was used in relation to Chinese martial arts by the Chinese community.  The  Oxford English Dictionary  defines the term kung-fu as a primarily unarmed Chinese martial art resembling  |karate|Karate| .  This illustrates how the meaning of this term has been changed in English. The origin of this change can be attributed to the misunderstanding or mistranslation of the term through movie subtitles or dubbing.
