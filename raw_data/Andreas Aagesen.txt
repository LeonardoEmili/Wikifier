Andreas Aagesen was a  |Danish|Denmark|  jurist.       Aagesen was educated for the law at  |Christianshavn|Christianshavn|  and  |Copenhagen|Copenhagen| , and interrupted his studies in 1848 to take part in the  |First_Schleswig_War|First_Schleswig_War| , in which he served as the leader of a reserve battalion.     In 1855 Aagesen became a professor of  |jurisprudence|Jurisprudence|  at the  |University_of_Copenhagen|University_Of_Copenhagen| . In 1870 he was appointed a member of the commission for drawing up a maritime and commercial code, and the navigation law of 1882 is mainly his work. In 1879 he was elected a member of the  |Landsting|Landsting|  ; but it is as a teacher at the university that he won his reputation. Aagesen was  |Carl_Christian_Halls|Carl_Christian_Hall|  successor as lecturer on  |Roman_law|Roman_Law|  at the university, and in this department his research was epoch-making.       Among his numerous juridical works may be mentioned: Bidrag til Læren om Overdragelse af Ejendomsret, Bemærkinger om Rettigheder over Ting ;   Fortegnelse over Retssamlinger, Retslitteratur i Danmark, Norge, Sverige .
