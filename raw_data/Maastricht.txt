      Maastricht  is a  |city|City|  and a  |municipality|Municipality|  in the southeast of the  |Netherlands|Netherlands| . It is the  |capital|Capital_City|  and largest city of the province of  |Limburg|Limburg| . Maastricht is located on both sides of the  |Meuse|Meuse|  , at the point where the  |Jeker|Jeker|  joins it. It is adjacent to the border with  |Belgium|Belgium| .     Maastricht developed from a  |Roman|Roman_Republic|  settlement to a medieval religious centre. In the 16th century it became a garrison town and in the 19th century an early industrial city.  Today, the city is a thriving cultural and regional hub. It became well known through the  |Maastricht_Treaty|Maastricht_Treaty|  and as the birthplace of the  |euro|Euro| .  Maastricht has 1677 national heritage buildings , the second highest number in the Netherlands, after  |Amsterdam|Amsterdam| . The city is visited by tourists for shopping and recreation, and has a large international student population. Maastricht is a member of the  |Most_Ancient_European_Towns_Network|Most_Ancient_European_Towns_Network|   and is part of the  |Meuse-Rhine_Euroregion|Meuse-Rhine_Euroregion| , which includes the nearby German and Belgian cities of  |Aachen|Aachen| ,  |Eupen|Eupen| ,  |Hasselt|Hasselt| ,  |Liège|Liège| , and  |Tongeren|Tongeren| . The Meuse-Rhine Euroregion is a metropolis with a population of about 3.9 million with several international universities.              Maastricht is mentioned in ancient documents as  Treiectinsem urbem ab. 575, Treiectensis in 634, Triecto, Triectu in 7th century, Triiect in 768-781, Traiecto in 945, Masetrieth in 1051.
