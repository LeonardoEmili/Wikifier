     Politics of Solomon Islands takes place within the framework of a  |parliamentary|Parliamentary_System| |representative_democratic|Representative_Democracy| ,  |constitutional_monarchy|Constitutional_Monarchy| .  |Solomon_Islands|Solomon_Islands|  is an independent  |Commonwealth_realm|Commonwealth_Realm| , where  |executive_power|Executive_Power|  is exercised by the government.  |Legislative_power|Legislative_Power|  is vested in both the  |government|Government|  and a  |multi-party|Multi-Party_System|  parliament.     The head of state, the  |King_or_Queen_of_Solomon_Islands|Monarchy_Of_Solomon_Islands| , is represented by the  |Governor-General|Governor-General_Of_Solomon_Islands| . The  |head_of_government|Head_Of_Government|  is the  |Prime_Minister|Prime_Minister_Of_Solomon_Islands| .   Constitutional safeguards include freedom of speech, press, worship, movement, and association. The  |Judiciary|Judiciary|  is independent of the executive and the legislature.       As head of state,  |Queen_Elizabeth_II|Elizabeth_Ii|  is represented in  |Solomon_Islands|Solomon_Islands|  by a governor general who acts on the advice of the prime minister and the cabinet. The  |Governor-General_of_Solomon_Islands|Governor-General_Of_Solomon_Islands|  is elected by parliament.     Solomon Islands governments are characterized by weak political parties and highly unstable parliamentary coalitions. They are subject to frequent votes of no confidence, and government leadership changes frequently as a result. Cabinet changes are common.     The  |Prime_Minister|Prime_Minister_Of_Solomon_Islands| , elected by  |Parliament|National_Parliament_Of_Solomon_Islands| , chooses the other members of the  |cabinet|Cabinet| . Each ministry is headed by a cabinet member, who is assisted by a permanent secretary, a career public servant, who directs the staff of the ministry. The cabinet consists members, including the Prime Minister and ministers of executive departments.
