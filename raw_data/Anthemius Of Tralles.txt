Anthemius of Tralles  was a  |Greek|Ancient_Greeks|  from  |Tralles|Tralles|  who worked as a  |geometer|Euclidean_Geometry|  and  |architect|Byzantine_Architecture|  in  |Constantinople|Constantinople| , the capital of the  |Byzantine_Empire|Byzantine_Empire| . With  |Isidore_of_Miletus|Isidore_Of_Miletus| , he designed the  |Hagia_Sophia|Hagia_Sophia|  for  |Justinian_I|Justinian_&_Nbsp;I| .          Anthemius was one of the five sons of Stephanus of Tralles, a physician. His brothers were Dioscorus, Alexander, Olympius, and Metrodorus. Dioscorus followed his fathers profession in Tralles; Alexander did so in  |Rome|Ancient_Rome|  and became one of the most celebrated medical men of his time; Olympius became a noted  |lawyer|Roman_Law| ; and Metrodorus worked as a  |grammarian|Grammarian|  in Constantinople.     Anthemius was said to have annoyed his neighbor Zeno in two ways: first, by engineering a miniature earthquake by sending steam through leather tubes he had fixed among the  |joists|Joist|  and flooring of Zenos parlor while he was entertaining friends and, second, by simulating  |thunder|Thunder|  and  |lightning|Lightning|  and flashing intolerable light into Zenos eyes from a slightly hollowed mirror. In addition to his familiarity with steam, some dubious authorities credited Anthemius with a knowledge of  |gunpowder|Gunpowder|  or other explosive compound.       Anthemius was a capable  |mathematician|Greek_Mathematics| . In the course of his treatise On  |Burning_Glass|Burning_Glass| es , intended to facilitate the construction of surfaces to reflect light to a single point, he described the string construction of the  |ellipse|Ellipse|  and assumed a property of ellipses not found in  |Apollonius_of_Pergas|Apollonius_Of_Perga|  Conics |directrix|Directrix| |parabola|Parabola| . This work was later known to  |Arab_mathematicians|Mathematics_In_Medieval_Islam|  such as  |Alhazen|Book_Of_Optics| .     Eutociuss commentary on Apolloniuss Conics was dedicated to Anthemius.          As an architect, Anthemius is best known for his work designing the  |Hagia_Sophia|Hagia_Sophia| . He was commissioned with  |Isidore_of_Miletus|Isidore_Of_Miletus|  by  |Justinian_I|Justinian_&_Nbsp;I|  shortly after the earlier church on the site burned down in 532 but died early on in the project. He is also said to have repaired the flood defenses at  |Daras|Dara| .
