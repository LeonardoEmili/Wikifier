Karel van het Reve was a Dutch writer, translator and literary historian, teaching and writing on  |Russian_literature|Russian_Literature| .     He was born in  |Amsterdam|Amsterdam|  and was raised as a  |communist|Communism| . He lost his faith in his twenties and became an active critic and opponent of the  |Soviet|Soviet_Union|  regime. With his help, work of dissident  |Andrei_Sakharov|Andrei_Sakharov|  was smuggled to the west, and his  |Alexander_Herzen_Foundation|Alexander_Herzen_Foundation|  published dissident Soviet literature.     He is considered to be one of the finest Dutch essayists, his interests ranging from the fallacies of  |Marxism|Marxism|  to  |nude_beach|Nude_Beach| |etiquette|Etiquette| . His works include a history of  |Russian_literature|Russian_Literature| , 2  |novels|Novel|  and several collections of  |essays|Essay| . In 1978, Karel van het Reve delivered the  |Huizinga_Lecture|Huizinga_Lecture| , under the title: Literatuurwetenschap: het raadsel der onleesbaarheid .     His brother,  |Gerard_Reve|Gerard_Reve| , was a prominent prose writer.
