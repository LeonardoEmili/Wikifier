   Paper-and-pencil games or paper-and-pen games are  |games|Game|  that can be played solely with  |paper|Paper|  and  |pencils|Pencil|  , usually without erasing. Examples of paper-and-pencil games are  |Tic_tac_toe|Tic_Tac_Toe| ,  |Sprouts|Sprouts| , and  |Dots_and_Boxes|Dots_And_Boxes| . Other examples include:  |Hangman|Hangman| ,  |Connect_5|Connect_5| ,  |M_A_S_H_|Mash| ,  |Battleships|Battleships| ,  |Paper_Soccer|Paper_Soccer| ,  |Golf|Golf| , WedgoLogic, and MLine.     Additionally, the term is used to distinguish  |role_playing_games|Role_Playing_Game|  of the  |tabletop_variety|Tabletop_Role_Playing_Game|  from  |role_playing_video_games|Role_Playing_Video_Game| , although those traditional role-playing games do not necessarily use either paper or pencils.     In some  |board_games|Board_Game| , particularly  |abstract_strategy_games|Abstract_Strategy_Game|  like  |Gomoku|Gomoku| , a piece, once played, will not be moved or removed from the board.
