 Saint Vincent and the Grenadines  is an  |island_state|Island_State|  in the  |Windward_Islands|Windward_Islands|  of the  |Lesser_Antilles|Lesser_Antilles| , an  |island_arc|Island_Arc|  of the  |Caribbean_Sea|Caribbean_Sea|  in  |North_America|North_America| . The country consists of the main island of  |Saint_Vincent|Saint_Vincent|  and the northern two-thirds of the  |Grenadines|Grenadines| , a chain of small islands stretching south from Saint Vincent to  |Grenada|Grenada| . Its total land area is 390  km² of which 342.7  km² is the main island of Saint Vincent. The countrys capital is at  |Kingstown|Kingstown|  on Saint Vincent.         The main island of Saint Vincent and the Grenadines is  |Saint_Vincent|Saint_Vincent| . Its geography is mostly mountainous and includes very little level ground. There is also a large difference between the coastlines on each side of the island; the  |windward|Windward_And_Leeward|  side is very rocky, while the  |leeward|Windward_And_Leeward|  side consists of many sandy beaches and has many more bays. The islands highest peak is the volcanic  |Soufrière|Soufrière|  at 1,234 m. There are several tiny islets offshore of Saint Vincent including  |Young_Island|Young_Island|  and the  |Cow_And_Calves_Islands|Cow_And_Calves_Islands| .
