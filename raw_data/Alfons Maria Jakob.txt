Alfons Maria Jakob  was a German  |neurologist|Neurologist|  who worked in the field of  |neuropathology|Neuropathology| .     He was born in  |Aschaffenburg|Aschaffenburg| ,  |Bavaria|Bavaria|  and educated in  |medicine|Medicine|  at the universities of  |Munich|University_Of_Munich| ,  |Berlin|University_Of_Berlin| , and  |Strasbourg|University_Of_Strasbourg| , where he received his doctorate in 1908. During the following year, he began clinical work under the  |psychiatrist|Psychiatrist| |_Emil_Kraepelin|Emil_Kraepelin|  and did laboratory work with  |Franz_Nissl|Franz_Nissl|  and  |Alois_Alzheimer|Alois_Alzheimer|  in  |Munich|Munich| .  at  |Who_Named_It|Who_Named_It|      In 1911, by way of an invitation from  |Wilhelm_Weygandt|Wilhelm_Weygandt| ,  edited by Peter J. Koehler, George W. Bruyn, John M. S. Pearce he relocated to  |Hamburg|Hamburg| , where he worked with  |Theodor_Kaes|Theodor_Kaes|  and eventually became head of the laboratory of  |anatomical_pathology|Anatomical_Pathology|  at the psychiatric State Hospital Hamburg-Friedrichsberg. Following the death of Kaes in 1913, Jakob succeeded him as  |prosector|Prosector| . During  |World_War_I|World_War_I|  he served as an army physician in  |Belgium|Belgium| , and afterwards returned to Hamburg. In 1919 he obtained his  |habilitation|Habilitation|  for  |neurology|Neurology|  and in 1924 became a professor of neurology. Under Jakobs guidance the department grew rapidly. He made significant contributions to knowledge on  |concussion|Concussion|  and secondary nerve degeneration and became a doyen of  |neuropathology|Neuropathology| .  by Greta Beighton     Jakob was the author of five monographs and nearly 80 scientific papers. His neuropathological research contributed greatly to the delineation of several diseases, including  |multiple_sclerosis|Multiple_Sclerosis|  and  |Friedreichs_ataxia|Friedreichs_Ataxia| . He first recognised and described  |Alpers_disease|Alpers_Disease|  and  |Creutzfeldt–Jakob_disease|Creutzfeldt–Jakob_Disease|  . He gained experience in  |neurosyphilis|Neurosyphilis| , having a 200-bed ward devoted entirely to that disorder. Jakob made a lecture tour of the United States and South America , of which, he wrote a paper on the neuropathology of  |yellow_fever|Yellow_Fever| .     He suffered from chronic  |osteomyelitis|Osteomyelitis|  for the last seven years of his life.
